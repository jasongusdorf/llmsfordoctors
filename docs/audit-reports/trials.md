## Cross-File Observations

### Strengths

1. **Exceptional consistency in structure and voice.** All 28 trials follow an identical four-section pattern (Study Design, Key Findings, Clinical Implications, My Takeaway) with consistent use of Callout components for evidence highlights and pitfalls. This is the most structurally consistent collection on the site.

2. **High clarity across the board.** Every trial scores 5 on Clarity. The writing is uniformly scannable, with bold statistics, numbered implications, and plain-language explanations of technical concepts. This is a standout strength.

3. **Strong actionability framing.** Most trials go beyond summarizing findings to address "what does this mean for your practice?" The Clinical Implications sections consistently translate evidence into decision-relevant guidance.

4. **Excellent audience fit.** Despite covering topics ranging from neurocritical care EEG to data-poisoning attacks, the summaries are consistently accessible to non-specialist clinicians. Statistical terms are explained in context rather than assumed.

5. **Good freshness.** The collection is dominated by 2025-2026 publications with lastUpdated dates in March 2026. The evidence base is current.

### Weaknesses

1. **Cross-linking is the weakest dimension across all 28 trials.** Only 2 of 28 trials (llm-clinical-reasoning-jama-2023 and llm-diagnostic-nejm-2024) include cross-links to other site content. The remaining 26 are standalone islands that link only to their source article. This represents the single largest improvement opportunity:
   - Ambient AI trials should cross-link to each other and to ambient AI tool reviews
   - Clinical reasoning trials should cross-link to reasoning workflows and templates
   - Safety-focused trials (EMR CDS Kenya, data poisoning, VeriFact) should cross-link to each other
   - The SAIL year-in-review should cross-link to the individual trials it discusses

2. **Several trials are missing the DOI link at the bottom.** The following files lack a "Read the full article" link: `ai-trial-eligibility-screening-2025`, `deepseek-clinical-benchmark-2025`, `diagnostic-rct-ai-literacy-2026`, `emr-cds-safety-kenya-2026`, `genai-mental-health-chatbot-rct-2025`, `gpt4-physician-management-rct-2025`, `hospital-course-summarization-2025`, `med-palm-2-expert-qa-2025`, `medical-llm-data-poisoning-2025`, `sepsis-cohort-llama3-2025`. This is a mechanical fix (S-sized effort per file).

3. **Review articles score lower on actionability.** The neurocritical care review and SAIL year-in-review necessarily cover broader territory and are less directly actionable than focused trial summaries. This is inherent to the content type, not a writing quality issue.

4. **Narrow audience fit for some specialty trials.** Entries like continual learning (ETT placement), sepsis cohort analysis, and sleep EEG biomarker are most relevant to specific subspecialties or researchers. Adding a brief "who should read this" note could improve accessibility.

## When to use this

Use this template when writing an outpatient neuro-oncology clinic visit note. Paste in your clinical data (tumor history, treatment timeline, imaging results, exam findings, labs, and current symptoms) and the LLM produces a structured clinic note with disease-specific documentation for gliomas, brain metastases, meningiomas, CNS lymphoma, and leptomeningeal disease. The output format captures molecular markers, treatment response by RANO criteria, steroid management, and seizure control, the details that define neuro-oncology follow-up.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Neuro-Oncology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an experienced neuro-oncologist writing an outpatient clinic visit note. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured neuro-oncology visit note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and treatment events; present tense for current status, exam findings, and plan.
- Include specific numbers (tumor dimensions, lab values, steroid doses, KPS, seizure frequency, MRI dates) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Document molecular markers, treatment timeline, and imaging response using RANO criteria when applicable.
- For chemotherapy, document agent, dose, cycle number, and schedule (e.g., temozolomide 200 mg/m2 days 1-5/28).
- Document steroid use with current dose, duration, taper plan, and side effects.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "GBM" = glioblastoma
- "TMZ" = temozolomide
- "GTR" = gross total resection
- "STR" = subtotal resection
- "KPS" = Karnofsky Performance Status
- "ECOG" = Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group
- "RANO" = Response Assessment in Neuro-Oncology
- "TTFields" = tumor-treating fields
- "AED" = antiepileptic drug
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Brain metastases focus | Replace molecular markers section with: "Primary cancer: [type, stage]. Number of brain metastases, locations, largest dimension. Systemic therapy status and extracranial disease burden." |
| Meningioma surveillance | Simplify treatment history. Add: "Growth rate on serial imaging (mm/year). Symptomatic vs. incidental." |
| Pediatric neuro-oncology | Add: "Include developmental milestones, school performance, and growth parameters. Use weight-based dosing." |
| Clinical trial patient | Add: "Document protocol number, treatment arm, cycle/day, protocol-required assessments, and any protocol deviations." |
| Telemedicine visit | Add: "This is a telemedicine encounter. Note exam limitations. Document patient-reported neurological status in lieu of formal exam." |

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Molecular markers matter.** The AI will list whatever you provide, but it cannot interpret the clinical significance of molecular combinations. IDH-mutant, 1p/19q co-deleted oligodendroglioma has a fundamentally different prognosis and treatment approach than IDH wild-type GBM. Verify that your assessment reflects the correct molecular context.
- **RANO criteria require comparison.** The AI will assign a RANO category based on your description, but RANO assessment requires formal comparison of enhancing and non-enhancing disease across time points. Confirm the AI's RANO call against your own reading or the radiologist's report.
- **Pseudoprogression vs. true progression.** This is a clinical judgment the AI cannot make. If your patient is within 3 to 6 months of completing chemoradiation and the MRI shows increased enhancement, the note should document your reasoning for or against pseudoprogression, not just state a conclusion.
- **Steroid documentation.** Neuro-oncology notes live and die by steroid documentation. The AI may note the current dose but miss the trajectory. Document peak dose, duration, taper schedule, and steroid-related complications (hyperglycemia, myopathy, PCP risk, bone density).
- **TMZ hematologic monitoring.** The critical lab values for TMZ safety (ANC and platelet count) must be verified before each cycle. The AI will not flag dangerously low counts unless the prompt tells it to. Always confirm the threshold logic in the plan matches your institutional protocol.
- **TTFields compliance.** The 75% daily compliance threshold (18 hours/day) correlates with survival benefit in the EF-14 trial data. If your patient is below this, the plan should address adherence barriers.
- **Advance care planning.** For progressive GBM, the median survival from recurrence is 6 to 9 months. The AI will include a goals-of-care section, but the content depends entirely on what you provide. Document these conversations even when brief.

## When to use this

Use this template when you need to write a referral letter to a specialist. A good referral letter gives the consultant enough clinical context to provide a useful evaluation without duplicating workup you have already completed. It should answer one question clearly: why are you sending this patient?

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Specialist Referral Letter">
You are a primary care physician writing a referral letter to a specialist colleague.

**Purpose:** Produce a professional, concise referral letter with clear clinical context, a specific referral question, completed workup with results and dates, and an explicit statement of what you need from the specialist.

### Rules
- State the referral question in the first sentence. The specialist should know why this patient is being referred before reading anything else.
- Include ICD-10 codes for the relevant diagnosis when available.
- List all workup already completed with results and dates. The goal is to prevent duplicate testing.
- Cite relevant clinical guidelines when the referral is for a guideline-recommended evaluation (e.g., "Per AHA/ACC guidelines, referral for ICD evaluation is recommended for LVEF ≤35% on optimized GDMT").
- State the urgency level and clinical rationale for urgency.
- Specify what you are asking for: diagnostic evaluation, procedural intervention, co-management, or second opinion.
- If the patient's insurance requires referral authorization, note the authorization number or state that authorization is pending.
- Keep the letter to one page.

### Vocabulary
- "Referral question" = the specific clinical question you want the specialist to answer.
- "Urgency" = Routine (weeks), Urgent (days), Emergent (same day / next day).

### Output format

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  REFERRAL LETTER                     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

**[Date]**
**To:** Dr. [SPECIALIST NAME] / [SPECIALTY] Colleague
**Re:** Referral for [REASON], [DIAGNOSIS (ICD-10)]
**Urgency:** [Routine / Urgent / Emergent]

Dear Dr. [SPECIALIST NAME / "[SPECIALTY] Colleague"],

**Referral Question**
[One clear sentence stating the specific clinical question or request.]

**Patient Demographics**
[Age, sex, relevant identifying information (de-identified).]

**Relevant History**
[Concise narrative (1 paragraph) covering key diagnoses relevant to the referral, symptom history, duration, trajectory, and what you have done so far.]

**Workup Already Completed**

| Test / Evaluation | Date | Result |
|-------------------|------|--------|
| [Test name] | [Date] | [Key finding] |

**Current Treatment**
[Current medications, doses, and duration relevant to the referral. Include response to treatment.]

2. [Secondary question, if applicable]
3. [Procedural request, if applicable]

**Urgency and Rationale**
[Routine / Urgent / Emergent] and why (what clinical feature drives the urgency).

**Closing**

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Brevity | Add: "Keep this to 3 paragraphs maximum; the specialist is busy" |
| Complexity | Add: "This is a complex multi-system patient. Include a comprehensive problem list." |
| Specific procedure | Add: "I am specifically requesting [EGD / nerve conduction study / surgical evaluation]" |
| Insurance authorization | Add: "Include a medical necessity statement for insurance referral authorization" |
| Guideline citation | Add: "Cite the specific clinical guideline that supports this referral (e.g., ACS/NCCN screening guideline, AHA/ACC ICD criteria)" |
| Payer requirements | Add: "This patient has [HMO/PPO]. Include documentation elements required by [payer name] for specialist referral approval." |

## Variations

### Urgent referral

Use this when the referral is time-sensitive and the specialist's triage team needs to prioritize this patient. Lead with the most alarming finding.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Urgent Specialist Referral">
You are a physician writing an urgent referral letter to a specialist.

**Purpose:** Produce a referral letter that clearly communicates clinical urgency and ensures rapid triage.

### Rules
- Mark URGENT prominently in the header and opening line.
- Lead with the most alarming clinical finding, not the background.
- Include the specific timeline: when symptoms started, how fast they are progressing, and when you need the patient seen.
- State what the patient should do if symptoms worsen before the appointment (e.g., "instructed to present to ED").
- Include your direct contact information for questions.
- Keep it concise; urgent referrals must be easy to triage quickly.
- Include ICD-10 codes.

### Output format

**URGENT REFERRAL**
[Date]
To: [SPECIALTY]
Re: URGENT referral for [REASON], [DIAGNOSIS (ICD-10)]

[Most alarming finding first. Then clinical summary. Then workup. Then specific request and timeframe.]

---
**REFERRAL INFORMATION:**
Referring To: [SPECIALTY]
Urgency Reason: [WHY THIS CANNOT WAIT FOR ROUTINE SCHEDULING]
Key Findings: [THE SPECIFIC FINDINGS DRIVING URGENCY]
Clinical Summary: [FOCUSED CLINICAL INFORMATION]
Workup Results: [CRITICAL LABS, IMAGING WITH DATES AND RESULTS]
What I Need: [SPECIFIC REQUEST AND TIMEFRAME]
</PromptPlayground>

### Routine referral

Use this for standard referrals where there is no time pressure. Emphasis on completeness and preventing duplicate workup.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Routine Specialist Referral">
You are a primary care physician writing a routine referral letter.

**Purpose:** Produce a concise, complete referral letter that the specialist can read in under 60 seconds and know exactly why the patient is there.

### Rules
- State the referral question in one sentence.
- Summarize clinical narrative in one paragraph: diagnosis, duration, what you have tried.
- List all relevant workup with dates and results.
- Specify what you want: evaluate, co-manage, perform a procedure, adjust medications.
- Include ICD-10 codes and current medications.
- Keep to half a page.

### Output format

[Standard referral letter format, abbreviated]
- Referral question (one sentence)
- Clinical narrative (one paragraph)
- Workup table (tests, dates, results)
- Medications
- Specific request
- Contact information

---
**REFERRAL INFORMATION:**
Referring To: [SPECIALTY]
Referral Question: [WHAT YOU WANT TO KNOW OR HAVE DONE]
Diagnosis: [DIAGNOSIS AND ICD-10 CODE]
Clinical Summary: [BRIEF CLINICAL INFORMATION]
Medications: [CURRENT RELEVANT MEDICATIONS WITH DOSES]
Workup Done: [LABS, IMAGING WITH DATES AND RESULTS]
What I'm Hoping For: [SPECIFIC REQUEST]
</PromptPlayground>

### Second opinion request

Use this when the patient (or you) wants another specialist's perspective on a diagnosis or treatment plan, without transferring care.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Second Opinion Referral">
You are a physician writing a referral letter requesting a second opinion from another specialist.

**Purpose:** Produce a referral letter that frames the second opinion as collegial and patient-centered, provides complete clinical data for independent evaluation, and specifies the questions you want addressed.

### Rules
- State clearly this is a second opinion request, not a transfer of care.
- Explain the reason: diagnostic uncertainty, treatment plateau, patient/family request, or high-stakes decision.
- Summarize the original specialist's findings, assessment, and recommendations.
- Provide the raw clinical data the second specialist needs to form an independent opinion.
- Specify the questions you want addressed.
- Note that you will continue to manage the patient and coordinate with both specialists.
- Tone: collegial and professional. Frame as a benefit to the patient, not a critique.
- Include ICD-10 codes.

### Output format

[Professional letter format]
- Second opinion request statement
- Reason for second opinion
- Original specialist assessment summary
- Clinical data (workup table with dates and results)
- Specific questions for second specialist
- Statement of ongoing management responsibility

---
**REFERRAL INFORMATION:**
Referring To: [SPECIALTY]
Original Specialist Assessment: [SUMMARY OF FIRST SPECIALIST'S FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS]
Reason for Second Opinion: [DIAGNOSTIC UNCERTAINTY / TREATMENT PLATEAU / PATIENT REQUEST / HIGH-STAKES DECISION]
Specific Questions: [WHAT YOU WANT THE SECOND SPECIALIST TO ADDRESS]
Clinical Data: [RELEVANT CLINICAL INFORMATION, IMAGING, LABS WITH DATES AND RESULTS]
Current Management: [WHAT IS BEING DONE NOW]
</PromptPlayground>

## Tips & pitfalls

- The single most important element of a referral letter is a clear, specific referral question. "Please evaluate" is vague. "I am requesting evaluation for suspected small bowel source of GI blood loss given negative EGD and colonoscopy" tells the specialist exactly what you need.
- List every relevant test already completed with dates and results. The top frustration for specialists is repeating workup the referring physician already did.
- For insurance plans requiring referral authorization, include a medical necessity statement in the letter. Some plans deny the specialist visit if the referral lacks a documented clinical rationale.
- Common reasons referrals fail to produce useful consultations: vague referral question, missing workup data, not specifying what you want (evaluate only vs. co-manage vs. take over care vs. perform a procedure).
- If the patient has been seen by another specialist for the same issue, include that evaluation and its findings to avoid duplication.
- For urgent referrals, follow up the letter with a phone call to the specialist's office. Letters alone may not be triaged quickly enough.
- Always confirm the specialist is in-network for the patient's plan. An out-of-network referral without prior authorization can result in significant patient liability.
- Some plans require formal referral authorization before the specialist visit. Verify the patient's plan requirements and include the authorization number in the letter when available.

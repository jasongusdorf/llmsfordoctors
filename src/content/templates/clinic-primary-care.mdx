## When to use this

You finished a 20-minute visit with a patient who has four chronic conditions and is overdue for a colonoscopy. Fifteen of those minutes were clinical. The next five are documentation. Paste your visit data into this template, and the LLM produces a structured primary care note organized around whichever conditions the patient carries: hypertension, type 2 diabetes, hyperlipidemia, depression, low back pain, preventive care, or some combination. The note knows to pull A1c trends, home blood pressure logs, PHQ-9 scores, cancer screening dates, and vaccination status without you remembering to include each one.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Primary Care Clinic Visit Note">
You are a primary care physician writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient for a follow-up, chronic disease management, acute visit, or annual wellness visit. The patient may carry one or more of the following common conditions: hypertension, type 2 diabetes, hyperlipidemia, depression/anxiety, low back pain, or need preventive care. Identify all active conditions from the clinical information provided and structure the note around them.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient primary care clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture chronic disease monitoring data, medication reconciliation with doses and adherence, preventive care tracking, social determinants screening, and a concrete, actionable plan for each problem.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "hemoglobin A1c (A1c) 7.2%"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers for all values: lab results, vitals, doses, dates, screening scores.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a numbered problem list with chronic disease conditions first, then acute issues, then preventive care.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: drug name, dose, titration target, follow-up interval, specific test or referral to order. Never write "continue current management" without specifying what that management is.
- For hypertension: document home blood pressure log average, current regimen, and BP target.
- For type 2 diabetes: document A1c trend, home glucose log if available, foot exam, last eye exam date, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR), and current diabetes regimen.
- For hyperlipidemia: document LDL, statin (drug, dose, intensity), and 10-year atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) risk score.
- For depression/anxiety: document PHQ-9 or GAD-7 score, current psychotropic medications, therapy status.
- For preventive care: document screening due dates, vaccination status, and any shared decision-making discussions.
- Include a complete medication list with indication for each medication and adherence assessment.
- Document social determinants: insurance, food security, housing, transportation, social isolation.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "A1c" = hemoglobin A1c
- "ASCVD" = atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease
- "UACR" = urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio
- "PHQ-9" = Patient Health Questionnaire-9
- "GAD-7" = Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7
- "AUDIT-C" = Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test-Concise
- "BMI" = body mass index
- "USPSTF" = United States Preventive Services Task Force
- "DEXA" = dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry
- "LDCT" = low-dose computed tomography
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Annual wellness visit | Change visit type to "Annual wellness visit" and add: "Emphasize preventive care tracker, screening recommendations per USPSTF guidelines, and health maintenance. Include a comprehensive review of systems." |
| Pediatric / Adolescent | Add: "This patient is a pediatric or adolescent patient. Adjust screening recommendations to age-appropriate guidelines. Include growth chart data, developmental milestones if applicable, and age-appropriate vaccination schedule." |
| Geriatric focus | Add: "This patient is over 65. Include fall risk assessment, cognitive screening (MoCA or MMSE), medication appropriateness review (Beers criteria), and life-expectancy-adjusted cancer screening recommendations." |
| Medicare AWV | Add: "This is a Medicare Annual Wellness Visit. Structure as a Health Risk Assessment including: cognitive screen, depression screen, fall risk, home safety, advance directive status, and a personalized prevention plan." |
| Post-hospital follow-up | Add: "This is a transitional care visit within 14 days of hospital discharge. Emphasize medication reconciliation with discharge medications, symptom surveillance, follow-up on pending studies, and assessment for readmission risk." |

### Annual wellness / preventive visit

Use this for the healthy (or seemingly healthy) patient coming in for their yearly checkup. The note centers on screening, vaccinations, risk factor identification, and health maintenance rather than acute symptom management.

Chronic conditions should be addressed briefly with reference to the management plan, but the note is organized around prevention.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document all USPSTF-recommended screenings applicable to the patient's age and sex with current status and next due date.
- Document vaccination status against current CDC adult immunization schedule.

- Include social determinants screening results.
- Plan must specify which screenings to order, which vaccinations to administer, and which referrals to place.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Chronic disease management (HTN + DM + HLD)

Use this for the bread-and-butter primary care patient with the metabolic triad. The note tracks A1c trend, home blood pressures, LDL target, ASCVD risk, and medication optimization across all three conditions simultaneously.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Chronic Disease Management (HTN + DM2 + HLD)">
You are a primary care physician writing an outpatient note for a patient with hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and hyperlipidemia. Structure the note around disease-specific targets, medication optimization, and end-organ surveillance across all three conditions.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document current value vs. target for: BP, A1c, LDL, UACR.
- Assess ASCVD risk and statin intensity appropriateness.
- Document diabetes complications screening: foot exam, eye exam, UACR, neuropathy symptoms.
- Assess medication adherence for each condition and identify barriers.
- Plan must include specific dose adjustments, lab orders with timing, and follow-up interval.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Acute visit (URI, UTI, MSK pain)

Use this for the patient who calls Monday morning with something that started over the weekend. The note is shorter and structured for same-day evaluation, diagnosis, and treatment of common acute complaints. It still captures chronic conditions and medication reconciliation, because primary care never has the luxury of treating one thing at a time.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Acute Primary Care Visit">
You are a primary care physician writing an outpatient note for an acute same-day visit. The patient presents with a new or worsening symptom: upper respiratory infection, urinary tract infection, musculoskeletal pain, or similar acute complaint. Structure the note around the acute evaluation while briefly documenting chronic conditions.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Focus the HPI on the acute complaint with pertinent positives and negatives.
- Include a targeted review of systems relevant to the chief concern.
- Document red flags assessed and absent (or present).
- Plan must include specific treatment (drug, dose, duration), follow-up criteria, and return precautions.
- Briefly list chronic conditions with current status; do not restructure the note around them.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **The medication list is the backbone.** Primary care notes live or die on medication reconciliation. If you give the model an incomplete list, the plan will have gaps. Include every medication the patient takes, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and inhalers.
- **Home monitoring data makes the note sing.** A home BP log average is more useful than a single office reading. A home glucose log is more useful than a single A1c. The more longitudinal data you provide, the better the interval assessment.
- **PHQ-9 scores need context.** A PHQ-9 of 8 means different things for a patient whose prior score was 18 (improving) versus one whose prior score was 2 (worsening). Always include the prior score and date.
- **Preventive care recommendations change.** The model uses current USPSTF guidelines, but screening intervals and age thresholds are updated periodically. Verify lung cancer screening eligibility criteria (currently ≥20 pack-years), colonoscopy intervals based on findings, and mammography age cutoffs against the most recent guidelines.
- **ASCVD risk calculation requires specific inputs.** The model cannot calculate a 10-year ASCVD risk score accurately unless you provide age, sex, race, total cholesterol, HDL, systolic BP, BP treatment status, diabetes status, and smoking status. If you want a calculated score, provide all the inputs or calculate it yourself and include it.
- **Social determinants screening is not optional decoration.** Food insecurity affects A1c. Transportation barriers affect follow-up. If your system uses a standardized screening tool, include those results. The model will document them, but only if you provide them.
- **The UACR deserves its own line item.** Moderately increased albuminuria (30-300 mg/g) in a diabetic patient has direct treatment implications for ACE/ARB dosing and SGLT2i use. Do not bury it in a general lab list.

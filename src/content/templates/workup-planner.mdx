Converts a clinical impression and differential diagnosis into a prioritized, rationale-driven diagnostic workup plan. Provide your leading diagnoses and the clinical context, and get a structured list of tests organized by urgency and diagnostic yield.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

<Callout type="evidence">
  Diagnostic test recommendations reflect general clinical principles. Institutional protocols, local prevalence, patient values, and cost constraints must all factor into actual test-ordering decisions. AI output does not replace clinical judgment or specialty consultation.
</Callout>

## Prompt Template

<PromptPlayground tool="ChatGPT (GPT-4o)" title="Diagnostic Workup Planner">
You are an experienced attending physician helping to plan a diagnostic workup for an undifferentiated clinical presentation.

Given the clinical scenario and differential diagnoses below, generate a structured workup plan organized by priority tier:

**Tier 1 — Immediate (order now, results needed within hours)**
List tests that must be obtained urgently to rule out dangerous diagnoses or guide immediate management. For each test:
- Test name
- What diagnosis or question it addresses
- Threshold that would change management

**Tier 2 — Early (order now, results within 24–48 hours)**
List tests that are diagnostically important but not immediately time-critical. Include the same format as Tier 1.

**Tier 3 — Deferred (pending Tier 1/2 results or outpatient)**
List tests that can wait for initial results before ordering, or that are appropriate in an outpatient or follow-up setting.

**Procedures / Consultations**
List any procedures (e.g., lumbar puncture, paracentesis, biopsy) or specialty consultations indicated, with the specific question to be answered.

**Decision Branch Points**
Briefly describe how key Tier 1/2 results would shift the workup. Use "If [result], then [next step]" format for the 2–3 most pivotal tests.

**What to Avoid**
List 2–3 tests that might seem indicated but are low-yield or potentially harmful in this specific scenario. Briefly explain why.

Rules:
- Prioritize tests by diagnostic yield and clinical urgency, not by comprehensiveness.
- Do not fabricate test sensitivities, specificities, or cutoff values — state them only if well-established.
- Do not infer clinical information not explicitly provided below.

## Customization Guide

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Setting | Add: "This is an outpatient visit — limit workup to tests available in clinic or sendout" |
| Resource constraints | Add: "Prioritize the 3 highest-yield tests given limited resources" |
| Prior workup | Add: "These tests were already obtained [LIST]. Focus the plan on what remains." |
| Subspecialty | Add: "This is a [nephrology / hematology / rheumatology] workup — use specialty-appropriate sequencing" |
| Patient factors | Add: "This patient has [CKD / contrast allergy / coagulopathy] — adjust accordingly" |

## Notes

- Works best when paired with the **DDx Generator** template: first generate your differential, then paste it here to build the workup plan.
- The **Decision Branch Points** section is the highest-value output — it makes your clinical reasoning explicit and helps anticipate what you'll do before results return.
- The **What to Avoid** section helps with both test stewardship and patient safety (e.g., avoiding contrast in AKI, avoiding LP before CT in certain cases).
- For complex multi-system presentations, run this template once per major diagnostic question rather than trying to cover everything in a single prompt.

## When to use this

Transplant clinic notes are medication notes. The tacrolimus trough that drifted from 8 to 12 after the patient started fluconazole, the mycophenolate dose reduction for GI intolerance that preceded a borderline rejection biopsy, the BK viral load that crept up when immunosuppression was too aggressive. These details live or die in the note. This template covers the six scenarios that fill most transplant surgery outpatient schedules: kidney transplant follow-up, liver transplant follow-up, graft rejection episodes, immunosuppression management, post-transplant malignancy screening, and living donor evaluation. The output format captures trough levels with target ranges, donor-specific antibody trends, viral surveillance panels, and the metabolic complications (new-onset diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia) that accumulate in every transplant patient over time.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Transplant Surgery Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending transplant surgeon writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a transplant recipient or living donor candidate. The visit type is one of: kidney transplant follow-up, liver transplant follow-up, graft rejection management, immunosuppression adjustment, post-transplant malignancy screening, or living donor evaluation. Identify the visit type from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured transplant surgery clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture transplant-specific data: immunosuppression regimen with trough levels, graft function trends, rejection history, viral surveillance, donor-specific antibodies, and metabolic complications.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "donor-specific antibodies (DSA)"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers for all values: trough levels with target ranges, viral loads, creatinine trends, liver function trends, DSA mean fluorescence intensity (MFI).
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with graft function first.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: drug name, dose change, target level, lab timing, biopsy indication.
- For kidney transplant: document creatinine trend, proteinuria, BK viral load, DSA, and biopsy results (Banff classification).
- For liver transplant: document LFTs trend, biliary complications, hepatic artery patency, DSA, and biopsy results (rejection activity index (RAI)).
- Document immunosuppression as a complete regimen: each drug, current dose, trough level, target range, and recent dose changes.
- Document viral surveillance: CMV (viral load, prophylaxis, treatment), BK virus (kidney), EBV (PTLD risk), and opportunistic infection prophylaxis.
- Document metabolic complications: new-onset diabetes after transplant (NODAT), hypertension, dyslipidemia, bone disease.
- For living donor evaluation: document workup completion status, compatibility testing, surgical anatomy imaging.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "DSA" = donor-specific antibodies
- "MFI" = mean fluorescence intensity
- "NODAT" = new-onset diabetes after transplant
- "PTLD" = post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder
- "CMV" = cytomegalovirus
- "BKV" = BK polyomavirus
- "EBV" = Epstein-Barr virus
- "RAI" = rejection activity index
- "DCD" = donation after circulatory death
- "DBD" = donation after brain death
- "CIT" = cold ischemia time
- "ATG" = anti-thymocyte globulin
- "CNI" = calcineurin inhibitor
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Early post-transplant (&lt;3 months) | Add: "This is an early post-transplant visit. Emphasize graft function trajectory, immunosuppression optimization, surgical site healing, and infection prophylaxis. Include more frequent lab monitoring schedule." |
| Rejection workup | Add: "This patient is being evaluated for possible rejection. Document the clinical suspicion, biopsy results with Banff classification, treatment plan, and response monitoring schedule." |
| Living donor evaluation | Remove the transplant summary, rejection, and viral surveillance sections. Add: "This is a living donor evaluation. Document completed workup (labs, imaging, crossmatch, GFR, CT angiography for surgical anatomy), psychosocial evaluation, and remaining steps before clearance." |
| Pancreas transplant | Add: "This is a simultaneous pancreas-kidney or pancreas after kidney transplant. Include pancreatic graft function (C-peptide, glucose, amylase, lipase), enteric or bladder drainage status, and insulin independence." |
| Pediatric transplant | Add: "This is a pediatric transplant recipient. Include growth chart percentiles, developmental milestones, school attendance, medication administration by caregiver, and transition planning if adolescent." |

### Post-transplant rejection management

Use this for patients with suspected or confirmed graft rejection. The note captures the clinical presentation, biopsy findings, treatment response, and immunosuppression adjustment.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Post-Transplant Rejection Management">

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and treatment; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the presentation that prompted rejection workup: creatinine rise (kidney), LFT elevation (liver), symptoms.
- Document biopsy findings: Banff classification (kidney) or RAI (liver), with specific grades.
- Classify rejection: acute cellular (T-cell mediated) vs antibody-mediated (AMR); grade/severity.

- Document response: graft function trend post-treatment.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Living donor evaluation

Use this for potential living donors (kidney or liver) undergoing pre-donation workup. The note documents completed and pending evaluations, compatibility testing, surgical anatomy, and donor clearance status.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Living Donor Evaluation">

### Rules

- Write in past tense for completed evaluations; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the relationship to recipient and motivation for donation.
- Organize the workup as a checklist with completed and pending items.
- Document GFR (measured, not estimated) for kidney donors.
- Document CT angiography findings: number of renal arteries, vascular anatomy, kidney size, laterality decision.
- Document compatibility: ABO, crossmatch, HLA typing, DSA.
- Document contraindications screening: malignancy, infection, renal disease, hepatic disease, cardiovascular risk.
- Include psychosocial evaluation and independent donor advocate assessment.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Tacrolimus trough timing matters.** The trough should be drawn 12 hours after the last dose, immediately before the next dose. A level drawn at the wrong time is uninterpretable. The model documents the number you give it; if the draw timing was off, the plan will be based on a misleading value.
- **BK viremia is not BK nephropathy.** Low-level viremia (under 10,000 copies/mL) does not necessarily mean the graft is being damaged. Biopsy is needed to confirm BK nephropathy. The model documents both viral load and biopsy findings, but it will not conflate them unless you do.
- **CMV D+/R- is the highest-risk combination.** These patients need extended prophylaxis and aggressive post-prophylaxis surveillance. If you provide the donor/recipient serostatus, the model will flag the risk level. If you omit it, the note will lack this critical context.
- **DSA trends, not single values.** A single DSA result of "negative" is reassuring but does not capture a patient who was DSA-positive 3 months ago and cleared. Provide the trend if you have it.
- **Creatinine drift is not the same as acute rejection.** A slow rise from 1.1 to 1.3 over 4 months has a different differential than a jump from 1.1 to 2.0 in a week. The model will frame the trajectory appropriately if you give it multiple data points with dates. A single creatinine value without context invites overreaction.
- **NODAT management affects the graft.** Tacrolimus itself causes NODAT. The model documents A1c and management, but the decision about whether to reduce tacrolimus to help glucose control (at the cost of rejection risk) is yours.
- **Vaccination in transplant patients has rules.** Live vaccines (MMR, varicella, zoster live) are contraindicated. Inactivated vaccines should be given before transplant when possible. The model will not check vaccine type; verify that any vaccine you document or order is appropriate for an immunosuppressed patient.

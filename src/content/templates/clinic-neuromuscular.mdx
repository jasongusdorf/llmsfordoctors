## When to use this

Use this template when writing a neuromuscular medicine subspecialty clinic note. It captures the data that drives diagnosis and management across the neuromuscular disease spectrum: EMG/NCS findings with pattern classification (axonal vs demyelinating, distribution, severity), disease-specific scoring (MG-ADL, QMG, ALSFRS-R), antibody panels, CK trends, FVC measurements (seated and supine), immunotherapy regimens, multidisciplinary clinic coordination, assistive device inventory, and advance care planning. Paste in your clinical data and the LLM produces a note organized around the functional assessment and treatment response that define longitudinal neuromuscular care.

For patients with neuropathy as a single complaint in a general neurology setting, the [general neurology template](/templates/clinic-neurology) or its neuropathy workup variation may be more appropriate.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Neuromuscular Medicine Clinic Visit">
You are a neuromuscular medicine specialist writing an outpatient clinic note after evaluating a patient. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured neuromuscular medicine clinic visit note.

**Purpose:** Generate a neuromuscular subspecialty clinic note that captures EMG/NCS data, disease-specific functional scores, strength testing by MRC grade, respiratory function, immunotherapy management, multidisciplinary coordination, assistive devices, and advance care planning where applicable.

### Rules

- Write the HPI as a narrative paragraph in past tense for history, present tense for current functional status.
- Document EMG/NCS: date, findings, pattern classification (axonal vs demyelinating, length-dependent vs non-length-dependent vs multifocal, sensory vs motor vs sensorimotor, severity).
- For myasthenia gravis: document MG-ADL score, QMG score, antibody status (AChR, MuSK, LRP4), thymectomy status, current immunotherapy regimen (pyridostigmine dose, steroid dose, steroid-sparing agent with dose and duration), crisis history.
- For ALS: document ALSFRS-R score (with subscores and trend from prior visits), FVC (seated and supine, with trend), site of onset, El Escorial criteria category, current disease-modifying therapy (riluzole, edaravone, tofersen if SOD1), and multidisciplinary clinic status.
- For ALS functional domains: speech (intelligibility, AAC device), swallowing (PEG status, aspiration risk, diet texture), respiratory (BiPAP use, hours/night, settings), mobility (wheelchair, power vs manual, assistive devices).
- For myopathy: CK level with trend, muscle biopsy results, genetic testing, steroid response if inflammatory.
- Document strength by MRC grade (0-5) for key muscle groups, with attention to proximal vs distal pattern and respiratory muscles.
- Document sensory exam: modality-specific (vibration, pinprick, light touch, proprioception), distribution.
- Document reflexes with grading (0-4) and pattern (areflexia, hyporeflexia, hyperreflexia).
- Document autonomic testing if applicable (QSART, tilt table).
- Document pulmonary function: FVC trend for neuromuscular disease patients.
- Document PT/OT: current program, assistive devices, orthotics (AFO, wrist splints).
- Document advance care planning: particularly for ALS (ventilator decisions, feeding tube, palliative care, hospice).
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "MG" = myasthenia gravis
- "ALS" = amyotrophic lateral sclerosis
- "ALSFRS-R" = ALS Functional Rating Scale - Revised
- "MG-ADL" = MG Activities of Daily Living
- "QMG" = Quantitative Myasthenia Gravis score
- "AChR" = acetylcholine receptor
- "MuSK" = muscle-specific kinase
- "FVC" = forced vital capacity
- "BiPAP" = bilevel positive airway pressure
- "PEG" = percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy
- "AAC" = augmentative and alternative communication
- "NCS" = nerve conduction studies
- "CMAP" = compound muscle action potential
- "SNAP" = sensory nerve action potential
- "MRC" = Medical Research Council (grading)
- "AFO" = ankle-foot orthosis
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| ALS multidisciplinary clinic | Add: "This is a multidisciplinary ALS clinic visit. Include separate sections for each discipline seen today: neurology, pulmonary, nutrition, PT/OT, SLP, social work, palliative care. Summarize each team's assessment and recommendations." |
| MG crisis documentation | Add: "This patient has a history of myasthenic crisis. Include a crisis action plan: warning signs, when to go to ER, medication adjustments to avoid, and emergency contact information." |
| Post-IVIG visit | Add: "This is a post-IVIG infusion follow-up. Document the infusion details (brand, dose, duration, premedication), response assessment, next infusion date, and any infusion reactions." |
| Telehealth | Add: "This is a telehealth visit. Document observable exam components (facial strength, tongue, speech, visible muscle atrophy, functional tasks on camera). Note that formal strength testing, reflexes, and sensory exam could not be assessed remotely." |
| Genetic counseling | Add: "Include genetic testing results, implications for family members, genetic counseling status, and reproductive counseling if applicable." |

### ALS multidisciplinary visit

Use this for ALS clinic visits where the patient is seen by the multidisciplinary team (neurology, pulmonary, nutrition, PT/OT, SLP, social work, palliative care) and the note needs to integrate assessments from multiple disciplines.

### Myasthenia gravis immunotherapy management

Use this for MG patients where the primary focus is immunotherapy optimization: assessing steroid taper progress, steroid-sparing agent response, complement inhibitor or FcRn antagonist management, and crisis prevention.

### Neuropathy workup and management

Use this for patients with peripheral neuropathy being managed in the neuromuscular clinic, where the note needs to capture the systematic etiologic workup, EMG/NCS classification, and treatment plan beyond the initial evaluation.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Neuropathy Workup and Management">
You are a neuromuscular specialist writing a clinic note for a patient with peripheral neuropathy. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured neuropathy evaluation and management note.

### Rules

- Document the complete etiologic workup: which labs have been sent, which have returned, and what remains pending.
- Correlate EMG/NCS findings with clinical exam.
- If inflammatory neuropathy (CIDP, GBS follow-up): document treatment response, IVIG schedule, and functional trajectory.

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Disease-specific scores define the visit.** MG-ADL, QMG, and ALSFRS-R scores are the language of neuromuscular follow-up. The model will include them if you provide them, but it cannot calculate them from raw exam data. Score the patient before generating the note.
- **ALSFRS-R trend matters more than the number.** A score of 36 is very different in a patient declining 2 points per month versus one stable for 6 months. Always include the prior score and date so the model can calculate the rate of decline.
- **FVC seated vs supine is not redundant.** A supine FVC drop of more than 20% from seated suggests diaphragmatic weakness and is an indication for BiPAP in ALS. The model includes both fields, but it will not interpret the drop unless you flag it.
- **Steroid taper speed in MG.** The model documents the taper schedule you provide but will not recommend a taper rate. Standard practice is slow (typically 5-10 mg reduction per month once below 20 mg), but clinical judgment drives the pace. Do not let the model suggest a faster taper than you intend.
- **Advance care planning is a longitudinal conversation.** The model includes an ACP section for ALS, but a single note cannot capture the full arc of these discussions. Document what was discussed at this visit, what the patient's current stance is, and what remains to be addressed.
- **EMG/NCS interpretation requires clinical correlation.** The model will classify the neuropathy based on your EMG summary, but it cannot verify that classification against the raw data. If your summary says "axonal" but the conduction velocities suggest demyelination, the model will not catch the discrepancy.
- **Complement inhibitor patients need meningococcal vaccination tracking.** The model includes a vaccination field for patients on eculizumab or ravulizumab, but it will not calculate when the booster is due. Verify this against the vaccination record.

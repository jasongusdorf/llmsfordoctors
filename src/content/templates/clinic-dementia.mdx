## When to use this

Use this template when writing an outpatient behavioral neurology or dementia clinic visit note. Paste in your clinical data (diagnosis, cognitive testing, biomarkers, functional status, behavioral symptoms, medications, caregiver assessment, and safety evaluation) and the LLM produces a structured clinic note that captures the full scope of a dementia visit: disease staging, cognitive trajectory, behavioral management, safety assessment, caregiver burden, and advance care planning. Works for Alzheimer's disease, frontotemporal dementia, Lewy body dementia, vascular dementia, MCI, and posterior cortical atrophy.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Behavioral Neurology / Dementia Clinic Visit Note">
You are an experienced behavioral neurologist writing an outpatient dementia clinic visit note. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured dementia clinic visit note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status, exam findings, and plan.
- Include specific scores (MoCA, MMSE, CDR, FAQ, NPI, Zarit) whenever available.
- Document cognitive trajectory with dated scores and comparison.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- The informant/caregiver report is as important as the patient's report; document both perspectives.
- Document safety assessment with specificity (driving, wandering, cooking, firearms, finances).
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "AD" = Alzheimer's disease
- "FTD" = frontotemporal dementia
- "DLB" = dementia with Lewy bodies
- "MCI" = mild cognitive impairment
- "PCA" = posterior cortical atrophy
- "CDR" = Clinical Dementia Rating
- "FAQ" = Functional Activities Questionnaire
- "NPI" = Neuropsychiatric Inventory
- "ARIA" = amyloid-related imaging abnormalities
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| New patient evaluation | Add: "This is a new patient evaluation. Include a comprehensive cognitive history with timeline of symptom onset, differential diagnosis, and recommended diagnostic workup." |
| FTD variant focus | Add: "Emphasize behavioral variant vs. language variant features. Document: social cognition, empathy changes, dietary preferences, compulsive behaviors, language output (fluency, grammar, word-finding)." |
| Capacity evaluation | Add: "This visit includes a formal capacity assessment. Document: specific decision in question, patient's understanding of the situation, appreciation of consequences, reasoning process, and ability to communicate a choice." |
| Telemedicine visit | Add: "This is a telemedicine encounter. Cognitive exam is limited; document which bedside tests were attempted remotely. Rely on caregiver-reported functional and behavioral data." |
| Research participant | Add: "Patient is enrolled in [trial name/number]. Document protocol-required assessments and adverse events per protocol." |

### Lewy body dementia management

Use this for patients with dementia with Lewy bodies or Parkinson's disease dementia, where the overlap of cognitive, motor, autonomic, and neuropsychiatric symptoms requires a specific documentation approach.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Lewy Body Dementia Management Visit">
You are a behavioral neurologist writing a clinic visit note for a patient with dementia with Lewy bodies or Parkinson's disease dementia. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured visit note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Document the four core features (fluctuations, visual hallucinations, REM sleep behavior disorder, parkinsonism) with current status.
- Include cognitive, motor, autonomic, and psychiatric domains.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "DLB" = dementia with Lewy bodies
- "PDD" = Parkinson's disease dementia
- "RBD" = REM sleep behavior disorder
- "VH" = visual hallucinations
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

### Caregiver-focused visit

Use this when the primary purpose of the visit is caregiver assessment, education, and support planning, particularly for patients in moderate-to-severe stages where the caregiver's wellbeing is a clinical priority.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **The caregiver is your second patient.** AI-generated notes tend to focus on the patient's cognition and skip the caregiver. If you don't provide caregiver data (Zarit score, sleep quality, health status), the note will have a caregiver-shaped hole. Always input caregiver information explicitly.
- **MoCA is not diagnostic.** The AI may overinterpret MoCA scores. A MoCA of 22 in a patient with 8 years of education means something different than a MoCA of 22 in a former professor. Adjust your interpretation for education level, language, and sensory impairment (vision, hearing).
- **Anti-amyloid therapy eligibility.** The AI will not determine whether your patient meets criteria for lecanemab or donanemab. Current eligibility requires early-stage disease (MCI or mild dementia), confirmed amyloid positivity, and absence of contraindications (anticoagulation, prior ARIA, extensive microhemorrhages). Verify eligibility yourself.
- **ARIA monitoring schedules are protocol-specific.** Lecanemab and donanemab have different MRI monitoring schedules and ARIA management algorithms. The AI will generate a generic monitoring section; replace it with your specific protocol's timeline.
- **Neuroleptic use in dementia carries a black box warning.** If the plan includes an antipsychotic, the note must document informed consent and the risk-benefit discussion. The AI may include the medication without the required documentation.
- **Driving assessment is a legal obligation in some states.** The AI does not know your state's mandatory reporting requirements. If your state requires physician reporting of dementia diagnoses to the DMV, document that reporting was completed or explain why it was not.
- **Capacity is decision-specific.** A patient may lack capacity for financial decisions while retaining capacity for simple medical decisions. The AI may assign a global "lacks capacity" label when the assessment should be domain-specific.

## When to use this

Use this template when writing a headache medicine subspecialty clinic note. It captures the data that a headache specialist actually needs: headache diary metrics (headache days per month, migraine days per month), acute treatment frequency, medication overuse patterns, preventive therapy response with quantified percent reduction, CGRP-targeted therapy tracking, onabotulinumtoxinA schedules, disability scores (MIDAS, HIT-6), and trigger analysis. Paste in your clinical data and the LLM produces a structured note organized around the treatment response cycle that drives headache management.

For patients with headache as one of several neurological problems, the [general neurology template](/templates/clinic-neurology) may be more appropriate.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Headache Medicine Clinic Visit">
You are a headache medicine specialist writing an outpatient clinic note after evaluating a patient with a headache disorder. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured headache clinic visit note.

**Purpose:** Generate a headache subspecialty clinic note that captures headache diary metrics, acute and preventive treatment response, disability impact, and a management plan organized around treatment optimization.

### Rules

- Write the HPI as a narrative paragraph in past tense for history, present tense for current headache pattern.
- Quantify the headache burden: headache days/month, migraine days/month, average severity (0-10), peak severity, average duration.
- Document acute treatment: agents used, frequency (days/month), effectiveness, time to relief. Flag medication overuse (&ge;10 days/month for triptans/opioids/combination analgesics, &ge;15 days/month for simple analgesics).
- Document preventive therapy: current agent, dose, duration on current dose, response (% reduction in headache days from baseline), side effects. List all prior preventives tried with reason for discontinuation.
- For CGRP-targeted therapies: specific agent, dose, frequency, response, insurance/prior authorization status.
- For onabotulinumtoxinA: last injection date, injection pattern, total units, response, next scheduled date.
- Include disability score: MIDAS score or HIT-6 with interpretation.
- Document identified triggers and lifestyle factors: menstrual cycle, dietary, sleep, stress, weather, caffeine intake.
- Screen for red flags: thunderclap onset, positional component, systemic symptoms, progressive pattern, new neurological deficits.
- Document comorbid conditions relevant to headache management: anxiety, depression, sleep disorder, neck pain.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "HA" = headache
- "MOH" = medication overuse headache
- "CGRP" = calcitonin gene-related peptide
- "MIDAS" = Migraine Disability Assessment
- "HIT-6" = Headache Impact Test
- "onabotA" = onabotulinumtoxinA
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| New patient intake | Add: "This is a new patient evaluation. Include a comprehensive headache history from onset, all prior imaging, and a full list of prior treatments with reasons for failure." |
| CGRP therapy review | Add: "Focus the plan on CGRP therapy assessment. Include documentation to support continued prior authorization: baseline headache frequency, response metrics, and prior preventive failures." |
| Pediatric/adolescent | Add: "This is an adolescent patient. Include school attendance impact, screen time, and parental report of symptoms. Avoid medications not FDA-approved for pediatric migraine." |
| Telehealth | Add: "This is a telehealth visit. Document limitations of remote exam. Note that fundoscopic exam was deferred." |
| Prior auth support | Add: "Include a summary paragraph at the end documenting medical necessity for [specific treatment], listing prior preventive failures, current disability, and clinical rationale." |

### Medication overuse headache detox

Use this for patients with medication overuse headache where the primary goal is documenting the overuse pattern, initiating a bridge/detox strategy, and setting expectations for the withdrawal period.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Medication Overuse Headache Management">
You are a headache medicine specialist writing a clinic note for a patient with medication overuse headache. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on documenting the overuse pattern, bridge strategy, and detox plan.

### Rules

- Document the specific overused agent(s), frequency of use (days/month), duration of overuse pattern, and how the pattern developed.
- Apply ICHD-3 criteria for MOH: headache &ge;15 days/month in a patient with a pre-existing headache disorder, with regular overuse of acute headache medication for &gt;3 months.
- Document the bridge/transitional therapy plan with specific agents, doses, and duration.
- Set patient expectations: rebound worsening during withdrawal, timeline to improvement (typically 2-8 weeks), success rates.
- Document the preventive therapy plan (start or optimize during detox).
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"

### Cluster headache management

Use this for patients with cluster headache where the note needs to capture cycle pattern, acute abortive treatment (oxygen, sumatriptan), transitional therapy, and preventive strategy based on episodic versus chronic classification.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Cluster Headache Management">
You are a headache medicine specialist writing a clinic note for a patient with cluster headache. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note capturing the cluster pattern, acute and preventive treatment, and cycle management.

### Rules

- Classify as episodic or chronic cluster headache per ICHD-3 criteria.
- Document the cluster cycle: typical duration, frequency of cycles per year, current cycle start date, attacks per day, attack duration, and time of day.
- Document autonomic features: lacrimation, conjunctival injection, nasal congestion/rhinorrhea, ptosis, miosis, eyelid edema, forehead/facial sweating, restlessness.
- Document acute treatment: high-flow oxygen (flow rate, mask type, response time), injectable sumatriptan (dose, response), other abortives.
- Document transitional therapy: verapamil (dose, titration, EKG monitoring), corticosteroid bridge (agent, dose, taper schedule), greater occipital nerve block.
- Document preventive therapy: verapamil, lithium, galcanezumab, topiramate, melatonin.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"

- "TAC" = trigeminal autonomic cephalalgia
- "GON" = greater occipital nerve

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Headache diary data is the foundation.** The model will use whatever numbers you provide. If the patient did not keep a diary, say so; do not estimate frequency and let the model build a treatment response narrative on fabricated numbers.
- **Medication overuse thresholds vary by drug class.** The model applies the standard ICHD-3 cutoffs (&ge;10 days for triptans, opioids, combination analgesics; &ge;15 days for simple analgesics), but verify these against your clinical judgment. A patient using sumatriptan 9 days/month is not technically overusing but is close enough to warrant discussion.
- **CGRP prior authorization documentation.** The note structure is designed to capture the data points insurers require: baseline frequency, current frequency, percent reduction, prior preventive failures. Review this section before submitting for PA renewal; missing a required data point can delay approval.
- **OnabotulinumtoxinA timing.** The model will document the injection date and schedule you provide, but it will not calculate the interval or flag if the patient is overdue. Track this independently.
- **Disability scores need context.** A MIDAS of 32 is Grade III, but the model may not comment on whether the score represents improvement or worsening unless you provide the comparison point. Always include the baseline or prior score.
- **The model may understate menstrual migraine.** Menstrually related migraine affects treatment strategy (mini-prophylaxis, continuous OCP, etc.), but the model will only address it if you flag the menstrual association in your input.
- **Topiramate cognitive effects are dose-dependent.** The model will list side effects you report but may not suggest dose reduction. If cognitive side effects are present, consider whether the plan should include a taper trial.

## When to use this

Orthopedic clinic notes are exam-driven in a way most surgical notes are not. A rotator cuff note without degrees of range of motion is incomplete. A knee osteoarthritis note without a Kellgren-Lawrence grade leaves the reader guessing at severity. This template identifies the primary musculoskeletal condition from your clinical data and organizes the note around the exam findings, imaging, functional scores, and conservative treatment history that determine whether a patient needs surgery, more physical therapy, or more time. It covers rotator cuff tears, knee osteoarthritis, ACL tears, lumbar disc herniation, hip fracture follow-up, and carpal tunnel syndrome.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Orthopedic Surgery Clinic Visit Note">
You are an outpatient orthopedic surgeon writing a clinic visit note after seeing a patient for a musculoskeletal complaint. The patient's primary condition is one of: rotator cuff tear, knee osteoarthritis, ACL tear, lumbar disc herniation, hip fracture follow-up, or carpal tunnel syndrome. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient orthopedic clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture joint-specific examination findings with measured values, imaging interpretation, functional status scores, conservative treatment history, and an actionable plan.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and prior events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use (e.g., "range of motion (ROM)"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers: ROM in degrees (active and passive), strength grades, pain scores, imaging measurements.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary orthopedic condition first.
- Plan items must be specific: procedure name, approach, PT prescription (frequency, duration, goals), injection details (type, site, volume), brace type, weight-bearing status. Never write "continue conservative management" without listing exactly what that means.
- For rotator cuff: document tear size and location, retraction, fatty infiltration (Goutallier), muscle atrophy, special tests (Hawkins, Neer, empty can, drop arm, lag signs).
- For knee OA: document Kellgren-Lawrence (KL) grade, compartment involvement, mechanical axis alignment, failed conservative treatments (PT duration, injection history with dates and response), and functional scores.
- For ACL: document mechanism of injury, associated injuries (meniscus, collateral ligaments, chondral damage), pivot shift and Lachman grading, and graft options if surgery planned.
- For lumbar spine: distinguish radiculopathy from myelopathy, document dermatomal and myotomal findings, straight leg raise, and EMG/nerve conduction study (NCS) results.
- For hip fracture follow-up: document fracture type, fixation method, hardware status, weight-bearing progression, and functional milestones.
- For carpal tunnel: document Phalen test, Tinel sign, thenar atrophy, two-point discrimination, and EMG/NCS severity grading.
- Document surgical candidacy factors: BMI, hemoglobin A1c (diabetic patients), smoking status (cessation requirement), bone quality (DEXA if relevant).
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "ROM" = range of motion
- "MRC" = Medical Research Council (strength grading)
- "VAS" = visual analog scale
- "NRS" = numeric rating scale
- "DASH" = Disabilities of the Arm, Shoulder and Hand
- "WOMAC" = Western Ontario and McMaster Universities Osteoarthritis Index
- "KOOS" = Knee injury and Osteoarthritis Outcome Score
- "ODI" = Oswestry Disability Index
- "KL" = Kellgren-Lawrence
- "NCS" = nerve conduction study
- "EMG" = electromyography
- "SLR" = straight leg raise
- "DEXA" = dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Sports medicine focus | Add: "This patient is an athlete. Document sport, position, level of competition, and season timeline. Include sport-specific functional goals and return-to-sport criteria (e.g., hop test, isokinetic strength ratios)." |
| Pediatric orthopedics | Add: "This is a pediatric patient. Document Tanner stage, growth plate status, and skeletal maturity (bone age). Adjust surgical timing considerations for remaining growth." |
| Trauma follow-up | Add: "This is a post-traumatic follow-up. Document fracture classification, reduction quality, healing stage (callus formation on X-ray), weight-bearing progression, and hardware status." |
| Joint replacement pre-op class | Add: "Include pre-operative education checklist: DME ordered, home setup assessed, discharge plan (home vs SNF), blood management (iron studies, autologous donation), VTE prophylaxis plan." |
| Workers' compensation | Add: "Document work-relatedness, employer, job requirements (lifting, standing, repetitive motion), current work restrictions, maximum medical improvement status, and impairment rating if applicable." |

### Lumbar spine surgical consultation

Use this when a patient with lumbar radiculopathy or stenosis has been referred for surgical evaluation. The note separates the neurological findings from the imaging, documents the conservative treatment that has failed, and frames the surgical recommendation around the specific pathology and level.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Lumbar Spine Surgical Consultation">
You are an outpatient orthopedic spine surgeon writing a consultation note for a patient referred with lumbar pathology. Structure the note around neurological assessment, imaging correlation, failed conservative treatment, and surgical recommendation.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Clearly distinguish radiculopathy from mechanical back pain from neurogenic claudication.
- Document neurological exam by level: L2-S1 dermatomal sensation, myotomal strength, reflexes (patellar, Achilles).
- Correlate imaging findings with clinical presentation. State whether they match.
- Document red flags screened: cauda equina syndrome (saddle anesthesia, urinary retention, fecal incontinence), progressive motor deficit, weight loss, fever, history of malignancy.
- Document all failed conservative treatments with dates and response.
- Surgical recommendation must specify: procedure, level(s), approach, decompression vs fusion, instrumentation.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **ROM must be in degrees.** "Decreased range of motion" is not a finding. The model documents specific degrees for active and passive ROM, but you must provide the measurements. If you write "limited flexion," the model will flag it as incomplete rather than guess.
- **KL grade drives the conversation.** A KL II knee gets injections and PT. A KL IV knee gets a surgical discussion. The model uses KL grade to calibrate the plan, so include the grade or the specific X-ray findings that define it (joint space width, osteophytes, sclerosis, deformity).
- **Conservative treatment dates matter for insurance.** Many payers require documented failure of 3-6 months of conservative treatment before authorizing arthroplasty. The model timestamps each treatment, but only if you provide the dates. "Tried PT" without dates is insufficient for prior authorization.
- **Spine notes require level-by-level correlation.** The model will not assume that an L4-5 disc herniation causes the patient's L5 radiculopathy unless the clinical exam supports it. Provide dermatomal sensory findings, myotomal strength, and reflexes so the model can state whether imaging and exam correlate.
- **Rotator cuff tear size determines repairability.** A massive, retracted tear with Goutallier grade 3-4 fatty infiltration may not be repairable. The model uses these MRI details to inform the surgical plan. Without them, it defaults to repair, which may not be the right recommendation.
- **A1c and BMI are gatekeepers.** Many institutional protocols require A1c below 8% and BMI below 40 for elective arthroplasty. The model documents these values and flags optimization needs. If your institution has different thresholds, provide them in the input.
- **Post-op ROM goals are time-dependent.** A patient at 6 weeks after rotator cuff repair should have different ROM than at 12 weeks. The model uses the post-operative week to set appropriate goals. Providing the wrong post-op day leads to inappropriate milestone expectations.

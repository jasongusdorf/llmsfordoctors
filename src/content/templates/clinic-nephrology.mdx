## When to use this

Nephrology clinic moves fast: CKD progression, dialysis adequacy, electrolyte management, transplant workups, and the occasional glomerulonephritis patient who needs both a plan and a paragraph explaining why you ordered the complement levels. This template covers the six conditions that fill the schedule: CKD, secondary hypertension, glomerulonephritis, electrolyte disorders, nephrolithiasis, and ESRD/dialysis management. Paste in the clinical data, and the LLM produces a structured note that tracks GFR trajectory over time, documents dialysis adequacy numbers, captures proteinuria trends, and organizes the bone mineral disease labs without you building the table from memory.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Nephrology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an outpatient nephrologist writing a clinic visit note after seeing a patient for a follow-up or management visit. The patient's primary renal condition is one of: chronic kidney disease, secondary hypertension, glomerulonephritis, electrolyte disorders, nephrolithiasis, or ESRD/dialysis management. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient nephrology clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture disease-specific monitoring data (GFR trends, proteinuria, dialysis adequacy, electrolytes, bone mineral disease parameters), medication reconciliation with attention to nephrotoxins, assessment of disease trajectory, and a concrete plan with specific next steps.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) 28 mL/min/1.73m2"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers for all values: lab results, GFR, proteinuria quantification, dialysis adequacy metrics, BP readings.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary renal condition first.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: drug name with dose, specific lab panel to order, imaging study, referral destination. Never write "continue current management" without specifying what that management is.
- For CKD patients: document CKD stage (GFR and albuminuria category per KDIGO), etiology, GFR trajectory over time (at least 2 data points), and SGLT2 inhibitor status.
- For dialysis patients: document modality, access type and status, adequacy (Kt/V, URR), dry weight, and interdialytic weight gain.
- For glomerulonephritis patients: document histologic diagnosis (if biopsy done), proteinuria trend, complement levels, and immunosuppression regimen.
- Flag all nephrotoxin exposures (NSAIDs, contrast, aminoglycosides, lithium).
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "eGFR" = estimated glomerular filtration rate
- "CKD" = chronic kidney disease
- "ESRD" = end-stage renal disease
- "UACR" = urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio
- "UPCR" = urine protein-to-creatinine ratio
- "Kt/V" = dialysis adequacy (clearance x time / volume)
- "URR" = urea reduction ratio
- "ESA" = erythropoiesis-stimulating agent
- "CKD-MBD" = chronic kidney disease-mineral and bone disorder
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Transplant evaluation | Add: "This is a kidney transplant evaluation visit. Include the transplant checklist: cardiac clearance, cancer screening, dental clearance, psychosocial evaluation, insurance authorization, and immunological workup (PRA, crossmatch, HLA typing)." |
| Post-transplant | Add: "This patient is post-kidney transplant. Document immunosuppression regimen (calcineurin inhibitor trough, mycophenolate dose, prednisone), allograft function, rejection surveillance (DSA, protocol biopsy), and BK virus monitoring." |
| Pediatric nephrology | Add: "This is a pediatric patient. Include growth percentiles, age-appropriate GFR calculation, and developmental assessment." |
| Pregnancy and CKD | Add: "This patient is pregnant or planning pregnancy. Document CKD stage, teratogenic medication review, BP target in pregnancy, proteinuria monitoring plan, and obstetric nephrology coordination." |
| Stone prevention | Add: "This patient has recurrent nephrolithiasis. Include 24-hour urine results (volume, calcium, oxalate, citrate, uric acid, sodium, pH, supersaturation), stone analysis, and metabolic prevention strategy." |

### CKD progression management

Use this for the patient with established CKD (stages 3-5, not yet on dialysis) being followed for disease progression, GDMT optimization, and renal replacement therapy planning. The note centers on GFR trajectory, proteinuria management, and complication prevention.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="CKD Progression Management">

### Rules

- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.

- For CKD stage 4-5: document renal replacement therapy education and modality selection progress.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Dialysis follow-up

Use this for patients on hemodialysis or peritoneal dialysis returning for routine management. The note is structured around dialysis adequacy, access status, anemia and bone mineral disease management, and volume control.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Dialysis Follow-Up Visit">

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.

- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Glomerulonephritis monitoring

Use this for patients with biopsy-proven or clinically diagnosed glomerulonephritis being followed for treatment response, proteinuria trends, and immunosuppression management.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Glomerulonephritis Monitoring">
You are an outpatient nephrologist writing a clinic visit note for a patient with glomerulonephritis. Structure the note around histologic diagnosis, proteinuria and renal function trends, immunosuppression management, and response assessment.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the histologic or clinical diagnosis with biopsy findings if available.
- Track proteinuria (UPCR or 24-hour urine protein) and GFR trends with at least 2 data points.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **GFR trajectory is the most important number in a CKD note.** A single eGFR is a snapshot; the slope tells you the story. If you provide only one data point, the model cannot assess trajectory. Always include at least two eGFR values with dates. A decline of more than 5 mL/min/year qualifies as rapid progression and should change the management plan.
- **Proteinuria quantification method matters.** UACR and UPCR are not interchangeable. UACR is more sensitive for diabetic kidney disease; UPCR captures non-albumin proteins (relevant in myeloma kidney, for instance). The model may not distinguish these unless you specify which was ordered.
- **Dialysis adequacy numbers need context.** A Kt/V of 1.2 meets the minimum for HD, but a patient who is symptomatic with adequate Kt/V may benefit from increased dialysis time or frequency. The model documents the number; the clinical judgment about whether that number is sufficient for this patient is yours.
- **PTH targets are ranges, not thresholds.** KDIGO recommends keeping PTH in the range of 2-9 times the upper limit of normal for ESRD patients, which is deliberately broad. The model may apply a more rigid cutoff. A PTH of 400 in an asymptomatic dialysis patient may not require intervention.
- **Potassium management gets tricky on ACEi + SGLT2i + finerenone.** All three affect potassium. SGLT2i modestly lowers K, ACEi raises it, finerenone raises it. The net effect varies. Monitor closely after stacking these agents, especially in CKD4-5.
- **Nephrotoxin documentation is medicolegally important.** Documenting that you screened for NSAID use and counseled the patient to avoid them protects you. The model includes this section, but only if you provide the data. If you do not mention NSAIDs in your input, the model may leave the section blank.
- **Transplant planning has a long lead time.** It takes 6-12 months to complete a transplant evaluation. For a patient with rapid CKD progression, the referral should happen at stage 4, not stage 5. If the model does not prompt this, add it.

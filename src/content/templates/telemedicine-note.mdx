## When to use this

Use this template when documenting a clinical encounter conducted via telemedicine, whether video or audio-only. Telemedicine notes carry the same clinical and legal weight as in-person documentation, but they require additional elements that in-person notes do not: technology platform, patient consent, patient location, the reason the visit was conducted virtually, and an honest accounting of what you could and could not assess through a screen. Payors audit these elements. So do malpractice attorneys.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
  State licensing requirements for telemedicine vary. The patient's location at the time of the visit, not their home address, determines which state's laws apply. Document the patient's physical location at the time of the encounter.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Telemedicine Visit Note">
You are a physician writing a clinical note for a telemedicine encounter. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete visit note that meets telemedicine-specific documentation requirements in addition to standard clinical documentation.

**Purpose:** Generate a telemedicine visit note that includes all required telehealth documentation elements (consent, platform, location, exam limitations) alongside the standard clinical encounter note.

### Rules

- Write in present tense for current status, assessment, and plan; past tense for history.
- Include all telemedicine-specific documentation elements in a dedicated section at the top of the note.
- The physical exam section must explicitly state what was observed via video AND what could not be assessed due to the virtual format.
- If the patient reported vitals from home devices, label them as "patient-reported" and note the device used.
- Document clinical reasoning for whether the virtual format was sufficient for this encounter or whether an in-person visit is needed.
- For audio-only visits, document why video was not used and note the further limitations on physical assessment.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "MDM" = medical decision-making
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Audio-only | Add: "This was an audio-only visit. Document why video was not available and the additional exam limitations." |
| Specialist visit | Add: "This is a [specialty] telemedicine visit. Include specialty-specific exam elements that can be assessed via video (e.g., dermatology photos, movement disorder observation)." |
| Pediatric | Add: "This is a pediatric telemedicine visit. Document the caregiver present, the child's cooperation with the video exam, and developmental observations." |
| Controlled substance | Add: "This visit involves prescribing a controlled substance via telemedicine. Document DEA telehealth prescribing compliance, the established patient relationship, and state-specific requirements." |
| Post-discharge follow-up | Add: "This is a post-discharge telemedicine follow-up. Include the discharge diagnosis, date of discharge, and any interval changes since discharge." |

## Variations

### Chronic disease follow-up (virtual)

Use this for routine follow-up visits for chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, heart failure) conducted via telemedicine. The emphasis is on home-monitored data, medication management, and disease control metrics.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Chronic Disease Telemedicine Follow-Up">
You are a physician writing a telemedicine visit note for a chronic disease follow-up encounter. The patient is managing one or more chronic conditions and this visit focuses on disease control, medication management, and home monitoring data.

**Purpose:** Generate a chronic disease management note that integrates patient-reported home data (BP, glucose, weight, SpO2) with medication adjustments and disease control assessment.

### Rules

- Write in present tense for current status and plan; past tense for history and prior visits.
- Include telemedicine documentation elements (consent, platform, location).
- Organize home monitoring data clearly with date ranges and trends, not single values.
- Compare current disease control metrics to target goals.
- Document medication adherence and barriers (cost, side effects, complexity).
- The exam section is limited; acknowledge this and state what was observed.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Acute visit via telehealth

Use this for unscheduled or urgent telemedicine visits for acute complaints (URI, UTI, rash, musculoskeletal pain, medication side effects). The emphasis is on triage: can this be managed virtually, or does the patient need to be seen in person or go to the ED?

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Acute Telehealth Visit">
You are a physician writing a telemedicine visit note for an acute clinical complaint. This is an unscheduled or urgent visit where the primary question is whether the complaint can be managed virtually or requires in-person evaluation.

**Purpose:** Generate an acute visit note with explicit documentation of the triage decision: virtual management vs. in-person referral vs. ED referral.

### Rules

- Write in present tense for current symptoms and plan; past tense for onset and history.
- Include telemedicine documentation elements.
- The exam section must clearly delineate what was observed versus what could not be assessed, and how this limitation affects the clinical assessment.
- Document the triage decision explicitly: is virtual management safe and appropriate for this complaint?
- Include specific return precautions and escalation criteria.
- If prescribing antibiotics or other treatments without a confirmatory physical exam or test, document the clinical reasoning and the risk accepted.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Behavioral health telehealth visit

Use this for psychiatric or behavioral health encounters conducted via telemedicine. Behavioral health visits are particularly well-suited to telemedicine, but documentation must capture the mental status exam as performed via video and address any safety concerns with specific protocols.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Behavioral Health Telehealth Visit">
You are a physician or psychiatrist writing a telemedicine visit note for a behavioral health encounter. This includes psychiatric follow-up, therapy check-in, or medication management for mental health conditions.

**Purpose:** Generate a behavioral health visit note conducted via telemedicine with a video-based mental status exam and safety assessment.

### Rules

- Write in present tense for current mental status and plan; past tense for history.
- Include telemedicine documentation elements.
- The mental status exam must be documented as performed via video, noting what was directly observed and any limitations.
- Safety assessment (SI, HI, self-harm) is required at every behavioral health visit, regardless of the reason for the visit.
- If the patient is in crisis, document the crisis plan including the patient's physical location, local emergency resources, and the plan for ensuring safety before ending the visit.
- For controlled substance prescribing, document DEA compliance and state requirements.
- Document the patient's environment as observed on video (private setting, others present, distractions) as it affects the clinical encounter.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- The most common telemedicine documentation deficiency in audits is missing patient location. Payors and state boards require the patient's physical location at the time of the visit because it determines which state's licensure and prescribing laws apply.
- Audio-only visits are reimbursable for many payors but at different rates, and they require a different modifier (93 instead of 95). Document why video was not used: patient preference, lack of technology, connectivity issues.
- Patient-reported vitals are clinically useful but not equivalent to in-office measurements. Label them clearly. A patient's home BP cuff may not be calibrated; a home scale may not match your clinic scale. Acknowledge this in your documentation.
- The AI may generate a full physical exam. Review and delete any exam components you did not actually perform or observe. Documenting auscultation findings on a video visit is indefensible.
- Telehealth prescribing of controlled substances has specific DEA requirements that changed after the COVID-19 public health emergency. As of 2025, DEA requires an initial in-person evaluation before prescribing Schedule II-V controlled substances via telemedicine in most circumstances. Verify current federal and state rules before prescribing.
- Document the adequacy of the virtual encounter for every visit. "This visit was appropriate for telemedicine because..." protects you in an audit and demonstrates clinical judgment about when telemedicine is and is not sufficient.
- If you identify a finding on video that concerns you (visible respiratory distress, facial droop, severe rash), document what you saw and your instructions to the patient (go to the ED, come to clinic today, send photos for further evaluation).

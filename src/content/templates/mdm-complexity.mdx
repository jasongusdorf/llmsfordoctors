## When to use this

Use this template after a clinical encounter when you need to organize your documentation to clearly support the medical decision-making complexity for E&M coding.

Helps document medical decision-making (MDM) complexity to support E&M coding. Provide your clinical encounter details and get documentation organized by the three MDM elements: number and complexity of problems, data reviewed, and risk of management.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

<Callout type="pitfall">
  MDM documentation supports your professional judgment; it does not replace it. The AI cannot determine the correct E&M level for your encounter. Always verify coding against CMS guidelines and your institution's compliance requirements.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Medical Decision-Making Documentation">
You are a physician documentation specialist helping to structure medical decision-making for an encounter note.

Using the encounter details below, organize the documentation into the three MDM elements per 2021 CMS E&M guidelines:

**Element 1: Number and Complexity of Problems Addressed**
List each problem addressed during this encounter:
- Problem name
- Status: acute / chronic stable / chronic worsening / new / acute exacerbation
- Classify complexity: minimal / low / moderate / high

Summarize: The number and complexity of problems support a [MINIMAL / LOW / MODERATE / HIGH] level.

**Element 2: Amount and/or Complexity of Data Reviewed and Analyzed**
Document data elements:
- Tests ordered (list each)
- Tests reviewed (with results)
- External records reviewed (source, content)
- Independent interpretation of imaging/tracing (specify what was interpreted)
- Discussion with external physician or qualified health professional (who, about what)

Summarize: The data reviewed and analyzed supports a [MINIMAL / LIMITED / MODERATE / EXTENSIVE] level.

**Element 3: Risk of Complications and/or Morbidity or Mortality**
Document risk factors:
- Risk of the presenting problem(s)
- Risk of diagnostic procedures ordered
- Risk of management options selected
- Social determinants of health affecting management

Classify overall risk: [MINIMAL / LOW / MODERATE / HIGH]

**Overall MDM Level**
Based on the three elements (2 of 3 determine the level), the MDM supports:
[STRAIGHTFORWARD / LOW / MODERATE / HIGH] complexity

**E&M Code Range**
- Office/Outpatient: 99202-99205 (new) / 99211-99215 (established)
- Suggested level based on MDM: [CODE]

Do not fabricate encounter details. Document only what was actually described. Flag any areas where additional documentation would strengthen coding support.

---
ENCOUNTER DETAILS:
Setting: [Office / ED / Inpatient / Telehealth]
Visit Type: [New / Established]
Problems Addressed: [LIST PROBLEMS DISCUSSED AND MANAGED]
Tests Ordered: [LIST]
Data Reviewed: [LABS, IMAGING, EXTERNAL RECORDS REVIEWED]
Management Decisions: [MEDICATIONS PRESCRIBED, PROCEDURES PLANNED, REFERRALS, COUNSELING]
Risk Factors: [RELEVANT RISKS]
</PromptPlayground>

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Setting | Add: "This is an [ED / inpatient / telehealth] encounter; use the appropriate E&M code range" |
| Time-based billing | Add: "I spent [X] total minutes on this encounter. Document time-based billing elements instead of MDM." |
| Resident involvement | Add: "A resident performed the initial evaluation. Document the attending's role per split/shared billing rules." |
| Procedure same day | Add: "A procedure was also performed during this visit. Document the separately identifiable E&M with modifier -25 justification." |

## Variations

### High-complexity ED visit

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="MDM – High-Complexity ED Visit">
You are a physician documentation specialist helping structure MDM for a high-complexity emergency department encounter.

Organize the documentation per 2021 CMS E&M guidelines, emphasizing elements that support high-complexity MDM (99285 or 99291 if critical care):

**Element 1: Number and Complexity of Problems**
For each problem addressed:
- Problem name and status (acute, new, acute exacerbation of chronic condition)
- Severity and threat to life or function
- Diagnostic uncertainty: how many diagnoses are being considered, and which were ruled out during the encounter

Document why the problems meet HIGH complexity: one or more acute or chronic illnesses that pose a threat to life or bodily function.

**Element 2: Data Reviewed and Analyzed**
Document extensively:
- All tests ordered during this ED visit
- Results reviewed during the encounter (labs, imaging, EKG)
- Independent interpretation performed (e.g., personally reviewed CT images, interpreted EKG)
- External records obtained and reviewed (EMS records, outside hospital records, pharmacy records)
- Discussions with other physicians (consultants called, accepting physicians for transfer)

Document why the data supports EXTENSIVE level.

**Element 3: Risk**
Document:
- Risk of presenting problem(s): threat to life or bodily function
- Risk of diagnostic procedures (contrast, sedation, invasive procedures)
- Risk of management decisions (initiation of IV drugs, decision for emergent procedure, hospital admission)
- Risk of NOT acting (what happens if the condition is untreated)

Classify overall risk as HIGH.

**Overall MDM**
Map to E&M level: 99285 (high-complexity ED visit) or 99291 (critical care, if applicable). State the criteria that are met.

If the encounter qualifies as critical care, document: total critical care time, nature of the life-threatening condition, and interventions requiring the provider's direct personal management.

---
ENCOUNTER DETAILS:
Setting: Emergency Department
Chief Complaint: [CC]
Problems Addressed: [LIST WITH SEVERITY AND ACUITY]
Tests Ordered and Results: [ALL LABS, IMAGING, PROCEDURES]
Independent Interpretations: [IMAGING/TRACINGS YOU PERSONALLY REVIEWED]
Consultations: [SPECIALISTS CONTACTED]
Management Decisions: [MEDICATIONS, PROCEDURES, DISPOSITION]
Risk Factors: [THREATS TO LIFE/FUNCTION]
</PromptPlayground>

### Moderate-complexity outpatient visit

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="MDM – Moderate-Complexity Outpatient Visit">
You are a physician documentation specialist helping structure MDM for a moderate-complexity outpatient encounter.

Organize the documentation per 2021 CMS E&M guidelines to support 99204 (new) or 99214 (established):

**Element 1: Number and Complexity of Problems**
For each problem addressed:
- Problem name and status
- Classify as: chronic stable condition (low), chronic worsening condition (moderate), new problem requiring workup (moderate), acute uncomplicated illness (low), acute illness with systemic symptoms (moderate)

For moderate complexity: document at least one chronic illness with worsening, a new problem requiring additional workup, or two or more chronic stable conditions with medication management.

**Element 2: Data Reviewed and Analyzed**
Document:
- Tests ordered (each category counts: labs, imaging, other)
- Review of prior external records or test results
- Ordering and reviewing of each unique test (each counts as a data point)
- Any independent interpretation of tests

For moderate complexity: document at least 3 data categories, OR ordering and reviewing a test, OR independent interpretation, OR discussion with external physician.

**Element 3: Risk**
Document:
- Prescription drug management (moderate risk)
- Decisions about minor surgery with identified risk factors
- Decisions about elective major surgery with risk factor assessment
- Diagnosis or treatment significantly limited by social determinants

Classify risk as MODERATE.

**Overall MDM and Code**
Confirm 2 of 3 elements support moderate complexity. Map to 99204 (new) or 99214 (established).

---
ENCOUNTER DETAILS:
Setting: Office/Outpatient
Visit Type: [New / Established]
Problems Addressed: [LIST WITH STATUS]
Tests Ordered: [LIST]
Data Reviewed: [PRIOR RESULTS, EXTERNAL RECORDS]
Management Decisions: [MEDICATIONS CHANGED, REFERRALS, COUNSELING]
Risk Factors: [PRESCRIPTION MANAGEMENT, PROCEDURE RISKS, SDOH]
</PromptPlayground>

### Inpatient daily MDM

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="MDM – Inpatient Subsequent Care">
You are a physician documentation specialist helping structure MDM for a subsequent inpatient encounter (daily rounding note).

Organize the documentation per 2021 CMS E&M guidelines to support 99231, 99232, or 99233:

**Element 1: Number and Complexity of Problems**
For each active problem being managed today:
- Problem name, status today (improving, stable, worsening, new complication)
- Changes from yesterday
- Decisions made today for each problem

Note: for subsequent care, document what has CHANGED and what decisions were made TODAY, not a recapitulation of the admission history.

**Element 2: Data Reviewed and Analyzed Today**
Document data reviewed or ordered TODAY:
- New lab results reviewed (with clinical significance noted)
- Imaging reviewed today
- Consultant notes reviewed today
- Discussions with consultants or other providers today
- New external information received

**Element 3: Risk of Today's Management**
Document risks specific to today's decisions:
- Medication changes (new drugs started, dose adjustments)
- Procedure decisions
- Escalation or de-escalation of care level
- Discharge planning risks (safe discharge assessment)

**Overall MDM and Code**
- 99231: Straightforward or low complexity
- 99232: Moderate complexity (most common for daily rounding)
- 99233: High complexity (ICU-level decisions, acute deterioration, multiple active problems worsening)

Map today's encounter to the appropriate code with documentation support.

---
ENCOUNTER DETAILS:
Hospital Day: [#]
Active Problems Today: [LIST WITH TODAY'S STATUS AND CHANGES]
New Data Today: [LABS, IMAGING, CONSULT NOTES FROM TODAY]
Today's Decisions: [MEDICATION CHANGES, PROCEDURES, CARE LEVEL CHANGES]
Disposition Planning: [DISCHARGE TIMELINE, BARRIERS, NEXT STEPS]
Risk Factors Today: [NEW RISKS, CLINICAL INSTABILITY, MANAGEMENT CHANGES]
</PromptPlayground>

>
> **Element 1: Number and Complexity of Problems Addressed**
>
> | Problem | Status | Complexity |
> |---------|--------|------------|
> | Type 2 diabetes mellitus (E11.65) | Chronic, worsening -- A1c increased from 7.2% to 8.9% over 6 months despite current regimen | Moderate |
> | Hypertension (I10) | Chronic, stable -- BP at goal on current medications | Low |
> | Chronic kidney disease stage 3a (N18.31) | Chronic, stable -- eGFR stable at 52 | Low |
>
> Summary: One chronic worsening condition (diabetes with deteriorating glycemic control) and two chronic stable conditions. This supports **MODERATE** complexity of problems.
>
> **Element 2: Amount and Complexity of Data Reviewed**
> - Reviewed today: A1c 8.9% (drawn 1 week ago), BMP with eGFR 52 (stable), urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio 82 mg/g (new microalbuminuria)
> - Reviewed external records: endocrinology consultation note from 2 months ago recommending GLP-1 agonist if A1c remained above 8%
> - Ordered today: lipid panel, repeat urine ACR in 3 months
>
> Summary: Review of prior external consultation, review of lab results, and ordering of new tests. This supports **MODERATE** data complexity.
>
> **Element 3: Risk of Complications and/or Morbidity**
> - Prescription drug management: initiating semaglutide (new medication with GI side effects, dose titration required, pancreatitis risk counseled)
> - Risk of presenting problem: worsening diabetes with new microalbuminuria indicates progressive end-organ damage if not addressed
> - CKD limits metformin dose adjustment options and contraindicates certain agents
>
> Overall risk: **MODERATE** (prescription drug management with identified risks)
>
> **Overall MDM Level**
> Problems: Moderate | Data: Moderate | Risk: Moderate
> 3 of 3 elements support **MODERATE** complexity.
> **Suggested Code: 99214** (Established patient, moderate complexity)

## Tips & pitfalls

- The 2021 CMS E&M guidelines determine level by MDM complexity OR total time; this template addresses MDM only. If billing by time, document total time instead.
- This is a documentation aide, not a billing tool. Always have your coder or compliance team review for accuracy.
- For inpatient E&M (99221-99223, 99231-99233), the same MDM framework applies but the code ranges differ.
- The most common coding error is documenting problems that were not actually addressed during the encounter. Only count problems you discussed, evaluated, or made management decisions about today.
- For the data element, "ordering a test" and "reviewing a test" count as two separate data points. Document both explicitly: "Ordered CBC" and "Reviewed CBC results showing..."
- Independent interpretation of imaging or tracings (e.g., personally reading an EKG or X-ray rather than just reviewing the radiologist's report) counts as a higher-level data element. Document that you performed the interpretation and state your findings.
- Auditors look for consistency between the documented MDM level and the clinical content of the note. A 99215 note that describes a stable chronic condition with no medication changes will be flagged.
- Social determinants of health that affect management (housing instability affecting medication storage, transportation barriers limiting follow-up, health literacy affecting self-management) count toward risk under the 2021 guidelines. Document them when relevant.
- Always document clinical decision-making, not just what you did. "Started metformin" is an action; "Initiated metformin after considering patient's renal function (eGFR 62, above threshold), patient preference, and ADA first-line recommendation" is documentation of decision-making.

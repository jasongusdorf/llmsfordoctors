## When to use this

Use this template when writing a movement disorders subspecialty clinic note. It captures the data that shapes management in this field: Hoehn and Yahr staging, MDS-UPDRS motor scores, levodopa dose timing and on/off correlation, motor fluctuations (wearing off, dyskinesia, freezing), non-motor symptom inventory (REM sleep behavior disorder, orthostatic hypotension, cognitive screening, hallucinations, impulse control disorders), DBS programming parameters, botulinum toxin injection schedules for dystonia, tremor rating scales, and restless legs syndrome severity. Paste in your clinical data and the LLM produces a note organized around the motor examination and the medication timing map that drives most therapeutic decisions.

For patients with tremor or Parkinson's as one of several neurological complaints, the [general neurology template](/templates/clinic-neurology) may be sufficient.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Movement Disorders Clinic Visit">
You are a movement disorders specialist writing an outpatient clinic note after evaluating a patient. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured movement disorders clinic visit note.

**Purpose:** Generate a movement disorders subspecialty clinic note that captures the motor examination with disease-specific rating scales, medication timing and motor fluctuation correlation, non-motor symptom screening, device management, and a plan organized around motor and non-motor domains.

### Rules

- Write the HPI as a narrative paragraph in past tense for history, present tense for current motor status.
- For Parkinson's disease: document Hoehn and Yahr stage, MDS-UPDRS motor score (or components tested), motor fluctuations (wearing off timing, on-off phenomena, peak-dose dyskinesia, freezing of gait), and total daily levodopa equivalent dose (LED).
- Document the motor exam with movement disorders specificity: tremor by type (rest, postural, kinetic, intention) and body part; rigidity; bradykinesia (finger tapping, hand movements, pronation-supination, toe tapping); postural instability (pull test); gait (stride length, arm swing, turning, freezing, festination).
- Document medication timing: each levodopa dose with time of day, and the patient's on/off state relative to dosing.
- Screen non-motor symptoms: REM sleep behavior disorder, constipation, orthostatic hypotension (with BP sitting and standing if available), cognitive changes (MoCA score), depression, hallucinations (formed visual, passage, presence), impulse control disorders (gambling, hypersexuality, compulsive shopping, binge eating).
- For DBS: target, settings (voltage/current, frequency, pulse width for each contact), programmer, battery status, last adjustment date, and response.
- For essential tremor: document FTM Tremor Rating Scale or equivalent, functional impact (writing, eating, drinking from a cup).
- For dystonia: type (focal, segmental, generalized), body regions affected, botulinum toxin injection schedule (last date, muscles injected, units per muscle, total units, response).
- For RLS: IRLS severity scale score, ferritin level, augmentation assessment, current treatment.
- Document PT/OT status: LSVT BIG/LOUD, fall prevention program.
- Document caregiver status and burden.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "PD" = Parkinson's disease
- "ET" = essential tremor
- "DBS" = deep brain stimulation
- "LED" = levodopa equivalent dose
- "MDS-UPDRS" = Movement Disorder Society Unified Parkinson's Disease Rating Scale
- "H&Y" = Hoehn and Yahr
- "RBD" = REM sleep behavior disorder
- "ICD" = impulse control disorder
- "MoCA" = Montreal Cognitive Assessment
- "onabotA" = onabotulinumtoxinA
- "IRLS" = International Restless Legs Severity Scale
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| DBS programming visit | Add: "This is a DBS programming visit. Focus the note on current settings, programming changes made today, patient response observed in clinic, and next adjustment plan." |
| Botulinum toxin injection | Add: "This is a botulinum toxin injection visit for dystonia. Include a detailed injection table: each muscle, number of sites, units per site, total units, EMG guidance used, and comparison to prior injection pattern." |
| Advanced PD | Add: "This patient has advanced PD. Include discussion of advanced therapies: LCIG/Duopa, subcutaneous apomorphine, DBS candidacy, and palliative care considerations." |
| Telehealth | Add: "This is a telehealth visit. Document observable motor exam components (tremor, bradykinesia via finger tapping on camera, facial expression). Note that rigidity, pull test, and gait could not be assessed remotely." |
| Huntington's disease | Add: "Include UHDRS (Unified Huntington's Disease Rating Scale) components: motor, cognitive (SDMT, Stroop), behavioral, and functional assessment. Document genetic testing status and family planning counseling." |

## Variations

### Parkinson's motor fluctuation management

Use this for Parkinson's patients where the primary focus is optimizing levodopa timing to reduce wearing off, manage dyskinesia, and address freezing of gait. The output format centers on the medication timing map and on/off state correlation.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Parkinson's Motor Fluctuation Management">
You are a movement disorders specialist writing a clinic note for a Parkinson's disease patient with motor fluctuations. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on medication timing optimization.

### Rules

- Document every levodopa dose with the time taken and the patient's on/off state at each time point.
- Calculate total daily LED.
- Characterize the off state: motor symptoms (bradykinesia, tremor, rigidity, dystonia) and non-motor symptoms (anxiety, pain, urinary urgency).
- Characterize dyskinesia: timing relative to dose (peak-dose, diphasic), severity, distribution, functional limitation.
- Document freezing of gait: frequency, triggers, relationship to on vs off state.
- For each medication change proposed, state the rationale and expected effect on fluctuations.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "PD" = Parkinson's disease
- "C/L" = carbidopa-levodopa

### Botulinum toxin clinic (dystonia)

Use this for botulinum toxin injection visits where the note needs to document the injection pattern, dosing by muscle, comparison to prior session, and response assessment.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Botulinum Toxin Injection Visit - Dystonia">
You are a movement disorders specialist writing a clinic note for a botulinum toxin injection visit for dystonia. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured injection visit note.

### Rules

- Document the dystonia classification and body regions affected.
- For each muscle injected: name, number of injection sites, units per site, total units, and whether EMG guidance was used.
- Calculate total units administered (all muscles combined).
- Compare to the prior injection session: same muscles, dose changes, and clinical rationale for any changes.
- Document the response to the prior injection: onset of benefit, peak effect, duration, and any side effects (dysphagia, excessive weakness, diffusion effects).
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "onabotA" = onabotulinumtoxinA
- "abobotA" = abobotulinumtoxinA

### Essential tremor follow-up

Use this for essential tremor patients where the note focuses on tremor severity, functional impact, medication response, and consideration of advanced therapies (DBS, focused ultrasound).

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Essential Tremor Follow-Up">
You are a movement disorders specialist writing a follow-up note for a patient with essential tremor. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on tremor severity, functional impact, and treatment response.

### Rules

- Document tremor by type (postural, kinetic, rest if present) and body part, with severity rating.
- Quantify functional impact: handwriting (sample if available), eating (fork, spoon), drinking from a cup, buttoning clothes, pouring liquids.
- Document current medication(s) with dose, duration, response, and side effects.
- List prior medications tried with max dose and reason for stopping.
- If considering advanced therapies, document candidacy discussion (DBS, MRI-guided focused ultrasound thalamotomy).
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "ET" = essential tremor
- "FTM" = Fahn-Tolosa-Marin (tremor rating scale)

## Tips & pitfalls

- **The medication timing map drives the visit.** If the patient did not keep a medication diary, the model will use whatever timing you estimate. An inaccurate timing map leads to inaccurate fluctuation characterization. When the diary is unreliable, document that.
- **ON vs OFF exam matters.** The model will state whether the exam was performed in the ON or OFF state, but it cannot infer this. Always specify. A "normal" motor exam in the ON state does not mean the patient is well controlled.
- **Levodopa equivalent dose calculation.** The model will estimate LED, but the conversion factors vary by source (particularly for dopamine agonists and COMT inhibitors). Verify the calculation or use a validated LED calculator.
- **Impulse control disorders are underreported.** The model includes an ICD screening field, but patients rarely volunteer these symptoms. If you screened using a structured questionnaire (QUIP), include the score. If you screened informally, note that.
- **Orthostatic hypotension requires standing BP.** The model will calculate the drop if you provide both sitting and standing values. Without both values, the orthostatic assessment is incomplete. If you did not check standing BP, say so.
- **DBS settings are precise and consequential.** The model will list whatever settings you provide, but it will not verify them against the device. Double-check contact numbers, amplitudes, and frequencies against the programmer readout.
- **Caregiver burden is clinical data.** Document it. It influences discharge planning, home safety, medication management, and the decision to escalate care level.

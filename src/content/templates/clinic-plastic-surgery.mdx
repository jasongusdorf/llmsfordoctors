## When to use this

Plastic surgery clinic notes require dimensional precision that other surgical subspecialties do not. A breast reconstruction follow-up needs expander fill volume, symmetry assessment, and coordination with the oncology radiation timeline. A Mohs defect consultation needs defect dimensions, depth, and a reconstruction ladder analysis. A hand surgery visit needs range of motion measured in degrees at each joint, grip strength, and two-point discrimination. This template covers the six conditions that fill most plastic surgery outpatient schedules: breast reconstruction, skin cancer excision and reconstruction, carpal tunnel release, hand injuries, burn contractures, and facial trauma. The output format captures wound assessment, functional measurements, and photographic documentation references that generic surgical note templates miss entirely.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Plastic Surgery Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending plastic surgeon writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient. The patient's primary condition is one of: breast reconstruction, skin cancer excision/reconstruction, carpal tunnel or hand surgery, hand injury, burn contracture, or facial trauma. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured plastic surgery clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture wound or scar assessment with dimensions, functional measurements, photographic comparison references, disease-specific data, and a concrete surgical or rehabilitative plan.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "deep inferior epigastric perforator (DIEP) flap"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific measurements: wound dimensions (length x width x depth in cm), range of motion (degrees), grip strength (kg or lbs), two-point discrimination (mm), expander fill volumes (mL).
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary reconstructive or surgical issue first.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: procedure name, flap type, graft type, therapy protocol, medication dose.
- For breast reconstruction: document reconstruction type, stage, expander volume, symmetry, cancer treatment coordination (radiation timing, chemotherapy completion), and nipple reconstruction status.
- For skin cancer: document lesion type, Breslow depth (melanoma), margins, Mohs defect size and location, reconstruction plan (primary closure, local flap, skin graft, free flap).
- For hand: document affected structures, ROM by joint in degrees, grip and pinch strength, sensation (2-point discrimination), tendon glide assessment.
- For burns: document TBSA, depth, graft donor sites, scar maturation (color, texture, height), pressure garment compliance, and ROM at burn sites.
- Reference photography: note comparison to prior visit photographs with standard angles.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "DIEP" = deep inferior epigastric perforator
- "TRAM" = transverse rectus abdominis myocutaneous
- "LD" = latissimus dorsi
- "BCC" = basal cell carcinoma
- "SCC" = squamous cell carcinoma
- "SLNB" = sentinel lymph node biopsy
- "ROM" = range of motion
- "2PD" = two-point discrimination
- "DASH" = Disabilities of the Arm, Shoulder and Hand
- "TBSA" = total body surface area
- "STSG" = split-thickness skin graft
- "FTSG" = full-thickness skin graft
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Microsurgery focus | Add: "This is a free flap reconstruction or replantation follow-up. Document flap viability (color, turgor, capillary refill, Doppler signal), donor site healing, and sensory recovery." |
| Aesthetic surgery | Add: "This is an aesthetic surgery consultation or follow-up. Document patient goals, photographic analysis with standard aesthetic landmarks, and discuss realistic expectations." |
| Pediatric plastic surgery | Add: "This is a pediatric patient. Include growth considerations, developmental impact, parental input, and age-appropriate activity restrictions." |
| Lymphedema | Add: "This patient has lymphedema. Document circumferential measurements at standard points, pitting vs non-pitting, cellulitis history, compression garment status, and therapy compliance." |
| Wound care clinic | Add: "This is a chronic wound follow-up. Document wound bed appearance (granulation, slough, necrotic tissue percentage), wound VAC settings if applicable, offloading compliance, and nutrition status." |

### Mohs defect reconstruction planning

Use this for patients referred after Mohs micrographic surgery with a defect requiring reconstruction. The note documents the defect, analyzes reconstruction options using the reconstructive ladder, and outlines the surgical plan.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Mohs Defect Reconstruction Planning">
You are a plastic surgeon writing a consultation note for a patient referred after Mohs surgery for skin cancer with a defect requiring reconstruction. Structure the note around defect analysis, reconstruction options, and surgical planning.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for Mohs history; present tense for current assessment and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the defect: location (anatomic subunit), dimensions (length x width x depth), structures exposed, tissue loss type.
- Analyze reconstruction options using the reconstructive ladder: secondary intention, primary closure, skin graft (STSG vs FTSG), local flap (type), regional flap, free flap.
- Justify the recommended reconstruction: why higher-rung options are needed (aesthetic subunit, functional preservation, wound healing concerns).
- Document the original skin cancer: type, Breslow depth (melanoma), margins, number of Mohs stages.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Hand surgery post-operative rehabilitation

Use this for patients following hand surgery (carpal tunnel release, tendon repair, fracture fixation, nerve repair) during the rehabilitation phase. The note tracks range of motion recovery, strength return, therapy progress, and functional milestones.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Hand Surgery Post-Op Rehabilitation">
You are a plastic or hand surgeon writing a post-operative rehabilitation visit note for a patient recovering from hand surgery. Structure the note around functional recovery: ROM, strength, sensation, therapy progress, and return-to-activity milestones.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for surgical history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document ROM at each affected joint in degrees (active and passive).
- Document grip and pinch strength with comparison to contralateral hand.
- Document sensation in specific nerve territories with 2PD or Semmes-Weinstein.
- For tendon repairs: document tendon glide protocol, active vs passive ROM restrictions by week.
- For nerve repairs: document Tinel sign advancement, sensory recovery, motor recovery.
- For fractures: document radiographic healing, hardware status, weight-bearing restrictions.
- Include therapy compliance and home exercise program adherence.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Photography is the medical record.** The model will reference comparison to prior photographs if you mention it in your input, but it cannot see the photos. The note documents what changed; the photos prove it. Always take standard-angle photos at every visit.
- **ROM needs both sides.** A PIP flexion of 70 degrees means nothing without the contralateral comparison. If the uninjured side flexes to 100 degrees, you have a 30-degree deficit. If it flexes to 75, the patient is nearly recovered. The model will calculate the percentage if you provide both numbers.
- **Expander volume is not implant size.** Patients and notes sometimes conflate these. The final implant may be a different volume than the maximum expander fill. The model documents what you provide; make sure you distinguish expander volume from planned implant volume.
- **Scar maturation takes 12-18 months.** A hypertrophic scar at 3 months may flatten on its own. The model will document current scar status, but do not let the note imply that early hypertrophy means revision is needed. Include the time since surgery as context.
- **DASH scores require the questionnaire.** The model will include a DASH score if you provide one, but it cannot calculate it from your clinical narrative. Administer the questionnaire to the patient before the visit and enter the result.
- **Baker classification is subjective but consequential.** Baker II (feels firm, looks normal) does not require intervention. Baker III (feels firm, looks distorted) and IV (painful, hard, distorted) usually do. The model documents the grade you assign; verify it matches what you felt and saw.

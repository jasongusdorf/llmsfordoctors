## When to use this

Use this template immediately after completing a procedure to generate a structured post-procedure note from your procedural details and findings.

Generates a structured post-procedure note from your procedure details. Covers indication, consent, technique, findings, specimens, complications, and post-procedure plan.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

---

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Post-Procedure Note">
You are a physician writing a post-procedure note for the medical record.

Using the procedure details below, write a structured procedure note with these sections:

**Procedure:** [Name of procedure performed]
**Date:** [DATE OF PROCEDURE]
**Operator(s):** [OPERATOR ROLE, e.g., "Attending with resident assistance"]
**Assistant(s):** [If applicable]

**Indication**
One to two sentences stating why the procedure was performed.

**Consent**
State how consent was obtained (e.g., "Informed consent obtained from the patient after discussion of risks, benefits, and alternatives" or "Emergency, consent waived").

**Timeout**
Confirm timeout was performed per institutional protocol.

**Technique**
Step-by-step narrative of the procedure in the order performed. Include:
- Patient positioning and preparation
- Anesthesia/sedation used (type, dose if provided)
- Anatomic landmarks or imaging guidance used
- Key technical steps
- Any deviations from standard technique

**Findings**
Describe what was found or visualized during the procedure.

**Specimens**
List any specimens obtained and where they were sent (e.g., pathology, culture, cytology). Write "None" if no specimens.

**Estimated Blood Loss:** [EBL]
**Complications:** [None / describe]

**Post-Procedure Plan**
- Monitoring instructions
- Activity restrictions
- Follow-up plan
- Pending results

Do not infer or fabricate any clinical details not provided. If a section's data is missing, write "[Verify and complete before signing]".

---
PROCEDURE DETAILS:
[Paste your procedure details: what you did, findings, complications, specimens, etc.]
</PromptPlayground>

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Procedure type | Add: "This is a [bedside/OR/endoscopic/interventional] procedure" for appropriate formatting |
| Brevity | Add: "Keep the technique section to 3–4 sentences maximum" |
| Teaching | Add: "Note the trainee's level of participation in each step" |
| Billing | Add: "Include time start/stop for procedures billed by time" |

## Variations

### Minor bedside procedure

Use this for quick bedside procedures (e.g., central line placement, paracentesis, thoracentesis, lumbar puncture, arthrocentesis) where a shorter, focused format is appropriate.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Bedside Procedure Note">
You are a physician writing a brief post-procedure note for a bedside procedure.

Using the details below, write a concise procedure note:

**Procedure:** [Name]
**Date/Time:** [DATE/TIME]
**Operator:** [Role and supervision level]
**Indication:** One sentence.

**Consent:** [Obtained from patient / emergency waiver / surrogate]
**Timeout:** Performed per protocol.

**Technique:**
- Position: [patient positioning]
- Sterile prep and drape: [site]
- Anesthesia: [local anesthetic, dose]
- Ultrasound guidance: [Yes/No; if yes, note real-time vs landmark-assisted]
- Access: [needle/catheter size, number of attempts, approach]
- Procedure: [key steps in 3–5 sentences]
- Secured/dressed: [how the site was secured]

**Findings:** [What was obtained — fluid appearance, opening pressure for LP, etc.]
**Specimens sent:** [Lab tests ordered on fluid/tissue, or "None"]
**EBL:** [Minimal / amount]
**Complications:** [None / describe]

**Post-Procedure:**
- Monitoring: [e.g., "CXR ordered post-central line to confirm placement"]
- Restrictions: [e.g., "Flat for 1 hour post-LP"]
- Follow-up: [Pending results]

Do not fabricate details. Write "[Complete before signing]" for missing information.

### Complex surgical/OR procedure

Use this for operative procedures performed in the OR that require detailed documentation of surgical technique, including trainee participation, implants, and operative findings.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Operative/Surgical Procedure Note">
You are a surgeon writing a detailed operative note for a procedure performed in the operating room.

Using the operative details below, write a comprehensive procedure note:

**Operation Performed:** [Full procedure name with CPT-relevant detail]
**Date:** [DATE]
**Surgeon:** [Attending surgeon]
**Assistant(s):** [Residents, fellows, PA — with level of participation]
**Anesthesia:** [General / MAC / Regional / Spinal — with anesthesiologist name if provided]
**Anesthesia time:** [Start to end if provided]

**Pre-Operative Diagnosis:** [Diagnosis prior to surgery]
**Post-Operative Diagnosis:** [Diagnosis after surgery — may differ from pre-op]

**Indication**
Brief clinical justification for the operative intervention.

**Consent**
Informed consent obtained. Risks discussed included: [bleeding, infection, nerve injury, anesthetic risks, procedure-specific risks].

**Timeout**
Surgical timeout performed per institutional protocol. Correct patient, correct site, correct procedure confirmed. Antibiotics: [drug, dose, timing]. DVT prophylaxis: [SCDs / pharmacologic].

**Operative Technique**
Detailed step-by-step narrative:
1. Patient positioning, skin prep, and draping
2. Incision (type, location, length)
3. Dissection and exposure
4. Key operative steps (in order performed)
5. Hemostasis technique
6. Implants or hardware placed (if applicable — include manufacturer, size, lot number if provided)
7. Irrigation and closure (layer by layer, suture type and size)
8. Dressing applied
9. Trainee participation: describe which steps the trainee performed and level of supervision

**Operative Findings**
Describe intraoperative findings, including normal and abnormal anatomy.

**Specimens**
- Specimens sent: [pathology, culture, frozen section]
- Laterality confirmed on specimen: [Yes/No]

**Drains:** [Type, location, output if known]
**EBL:** [amount in mL]
**Fluids:** [Crystalloid, colloid, blood products given]
**UOP:** [if relevant]

**Complications:** [None / describe intraoperative complications and management]

**Disposition:** Patient transferred to [PACU / ICU / floor] in [stable / guarded] condition.

**Post-Operative Orders:**
- Diet, activity, DVT prophylaxis
- Pain management plan
- Monitoring (drains, neurovascular checks, etc.)
- Labs to obtain
- Follow-up plan

Do not fabricate any operative details. Write "[Dictate/complete before signing]" for any missing information.

### Image-guided interventional procedure

Use this for procedures performed under fluoroscopy, CT, or ultrasound guidance (e.g., IR procedures, biopsies, drain placements, joint injections) that require documentation of imaging modality and radiation exposure.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Image-Guided Procedure Note">
You are a physician writing a post-procedure note for an image-guided interventional procedure.

Using the details below, write a structured procedure note:

**Procedure:** [Name of procedure]
**Date:** [DATE]
**Operator:** [Name, role]
**Imaging Modality:** [Fluoroscopy / CT / Ultrasound / combination]

**Indication**
Clinical indication and relevant prior imaging findings.

**Consent**
Informed consent obtained after discussion of risks including: bleeding, infection, damage to adjacent structures, radiation exposure (if fluoroscopy/CT), contrast reaction (if applicable), procedural failure.

**Timeout:** Performed per protocol.

**Technique**
- Patient positioning and prep
- Imaging guidance: [modality, approach, real-time vs intermittent]
- Access: [needle type/size, approach, target]
- Key procedural steps
- Contrast used: [type, volume] or "None"
- Devices placed: [catheter/drain type, size, position]
- Final imaging confirmation: [describe post-procedure images]

**Findings:** [What was seen on imaging, aspirate appearance, etc.]
**Specimens:** [Sent to pathology/micro/cytology, or "None"]

**Radiation Exposure** (if applicable)
- Fluoroscopy time: [minutes]
- Dose: [mGy or DAP if available]

**EBL:** [Minimal / amount]
**Complications:** [None / describe]

**Post-Procedure Plan**
- Monitoring: [duration, parameters]
- Imaging: [post-procedure CXR, CT, etc.]
- Activity: [restrictions]
- Drain care: [if applicable — flush instructions, output monitoring]
- Follow-up: [clinic visit, pending results]

Do not fabricate details. Write "[Complete before signing]" for missing data.

**Input provided:**
Ultrasound-guided right thoracentesis performed at bedside for a large right pleural effusion causing dyspnea. 1.5L of straw-colored fluid removed. Fluid sent for cell count, chemistry, cultures, cytology. No complications. Attending with intern performing under direct supervision.

**LLM output:**

**Procedure:** Ultrasound-guided right thoracentesis
**Date:** [DATE OF PROCEDURE]
**Operator(s):** Intern performing under direct attending supervision
**Assistant(s):** None

**Indication**
Large right-sided pleural effusion causing progressive dyspnea and hypoxia, requiring diagnostic and therapeutic drainage.

**Consent**
Informed consent obtained from the patient after discussion of risks (pneumothorax, bleeding, infection, re-accumulation), benefits (symptomatic relief, diagnostic information), and alternatives (observation, chest tube).

**Timeout**
Timeout performed per institutional protocol. Correct patient, correct side (right), correct procedure confirmed.

**Technique**
Patient positioned sitting upright, leaning forward over a bedside table. Right posterior thorax prepped with chlorhexidine and draped in sterile fashion. A total of 1,500 mL of serous, straw-colored fluid was drained by gravity and gentle aspiration. The catheter was removed, and a sterile occlusive dressing was applied. **Complications:** None

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Always include laterality.** AI will not always specify left vs right; verify and correct this before signing. Incorrect laterality in a procedure note is a serious documentation error.
- **Consent documentation:** AI defaults to "informed consent obtained." If consent was obtained by a different provider, from a surrogate, or waived in an emergency, update this section to reflect reality.
- **Technique specificity:** The model may write generic procedural steps. Add specific details that were unique to your case (e.g., number of attempts, unexpected anatomy, technique modifications).
- **Common AI failure mode:** The model may fabricate plausible-sounding procedural details (e.g., specific needle gauges, anesthetic doses) that you did not provide. Verify every technical detail.
- **Specimens:** Always confirm that the specimens listed in the note match what was actually sent from the lab. Missing a specimen in the note can lead to lost results.
- **EBL accuracy:** AI defaults to "minimal" for EBL. If there was significant bleeding, update with the actual estimated amount.
- **Billing:** For time-based procedure billing, you must include start and stop times. Add these manually if not included in the AI output.

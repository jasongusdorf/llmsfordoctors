## When to use this

Use this template immediately after completing a procedure to generate a structured post-procedure note from your details and findings. Covers indication, consent, technique, specimens, complications, and post-procedure plan. Works for bedside procedures, OR cases, and image-guided interventions alike.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

---

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Post-Procedure Note">
You are a physician writing a post-procedure note for the medical record. After receiving the procedure details below, produce a complete, structured procedure note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense throughout. The procedure has already been performed.
- Write the Technique section as a narrative paragraph, not bullet points. Every other section may use brief structured entries.
- Include specific numbers (needle gauges, catheter sizes, anesthetic doses, fluid volumes, EBL) whenever provided.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Always specify laterality (left vs. right) when anatomically relevant.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical details not provided. If a section's data is missing, write "[Verify and complete before signing]".
- Include trainee participation and supervision level when provided.
- Document imaging guidance modality if used (ultrasound, fluoroscopy, CT).

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "EBL" = estimated blood loss
- "US" = ultrasound
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Procedure type | Add: "This is a [bedside / OR / endoscopic / interventional] procedure" for appropriate formatting |
| Brevity | Add: "Keep the technique section to 3-4 sentences maximum" |
| Teaching | Add: "Note the trainee's level of participation in each step" |
| Billing | Add: "Include time start/stop for procedures billed by time" |
| Radiation documentation | Add: "Include fluoroscopy time and radiation dose" |

### Complex surgical / OR procedure

Use this for operative procedures performed in the OR that require detailed documentation of surgical technique, trainee participation, implants, and operative findings.

### Rules

- Write in past tense throughout.
- Write the Operative Technique as a detailed step-by-step narrative in paragraph prose.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Always include laterality.** The AI will not always specify left vs. right. Incorrect laterality in a procedure note is a serious documentation error; verify and correct before signing.
- **Consent documentation.** The AI defaults to "informed consent obtained." If consent was obtained by a different provider, from a surrogate, or waived in an emergency, update this section.
- **Technique fabrication.** The model may fabricate plausible-sounding procedural details (specific needle gauges, anesthetic doses) that you did not provide. Verify every technical detail against what actually happened.
- **Specimen verification.** Confirm that specimens listed in the note match what was actually sent from the lab. A missing specimen in the note can lead to lost results.
- **EBL defaults.** The AI defaults to "minimal" for EBL. If there was significant bleeding, replace with the actual estimated amount.
- **Time-based billing.** For procedures billed by time, you must include start and stop times. The AI will not add these unless you provide them in the input.
- **Trainee documentation.** If a trainee performed the procedure, the note must reflect their level of participation and your supervision. The AI may understate or overstate trainee involvement.

## When to use this

Cardiothoracic surgery clinic notes require a density of hemodynamic and imaging data that separates them from general surgical documentation. An aortic stenosis pre-op note without an STS risk score is incomplete. A post-CABG note without graft patency data and cardiac rehab status misses the point. This template identifies the primary cardiothoracic surgical condition and structures the note around the echocardiographic data, catheterization findings, pulmonary function, and risk stratification that determine operative candidacy and recovery trajectory. It covers coronary artery disease (CABG candidates), aortic stenosis, mitral regurgitation, lung cancer, thoracic aortic aneurysm, and pericardial disease.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Cardiothoracic Surgery Clinic Visit Note">
You are an outpatient cardiothoracic surgeon writing a clinic visit note after seeing a patient. The patient's primary condition is one of: coronary artery disease (CABG candidate), aortic stenosis, mitral regurgitation, lung cancer, thoracic aortic aneurysm, or pericardial disease. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient cardiothoracic surgery clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture disease-specific hemodynamic data, imaging, risk stratification scores, and a concrete operative or surveillance plan.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and prior events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use (e.g., "Society of Thoracic Surgeons (STS) risk score"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers: valve gradients, ejection fraction, aneurysm dimensions, PFT values, surgical risk scores.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary cardiothoracic condition first.
- Plan items must be specific: procedure name, approach, conduit plan, valve type, surveillance imaging interval. Never write "surgical management recommended" without specifying the operation.
- For CAD/CABG: document coronary anatomy (from catheterization), SYNTAX score if available, number and type of planned grafts, conduit assessment (Allen test, radial harvest candidacy, vein mapping), and STS risk score.
- For aortic stenosis: document valve area, mean gradient, peak velocity, LVEF, symptom status (NYHA class), STS score, anatomic considerations (porcelain aorta, prior sternotomy, annular size for prosthesis), and SAVR vs TAVR discussion.
- For mitral regurgitation: document MR mechanism (degenerative vs functional), severity grading, repair vs replacement assessment, LVEF, LV dimensions, and pulmonary pressures.
- For lung cancer: document CT findings, PET/CT results (SUV), biopsy pathology and staging, pulmonary function tests (PFTs) with predicted post-operative values (FEV1, DLCO), and operative plan (lobectomy vs segmentectomy vs wedge vs pneumonectomy).
- For thoracic aortic aneurysm: document size, location (ascending, arch, descending), growth rate from serial imaging, connective tissue disorder screening, and repair threshold.
- For pericardial disease: document pericardial effusion size, tamponade physiology, constrictive physiology, surgical approach (window vs pericardiectomy).
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "STS" = Society of Thoracic Surgeons
- "SAVR" = surgical aortic valve replacement
- "TAVR" = transcatheter aortic valve replacement
- "CABG" = coronary artery bypass grafting
- "LIMA" = left internal mammary artery
- "RIMA" = right internal mammary artery
- "SVG" = saphenous vein graft
- "LVEF" = left ventricular ejection fraction
- "NYHA" = New York Heart Association
- "PFT" = pulmonary function test
- "FEV1" = forced expiratory volume in 1 second
- "DLCO" = diffusing capacity of the lungs for carbon monoxide
- "SUV" = standardized uptake value
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| TAVR evaluation | Add: "This is a TAVR evaluation. Include CT gating measurements (annular area, perimeter, diameter), access vessel sizing (femoral, subclavian), coronary height, and heart team decision rationale for TAVR vs SAVR." |
| Redo sternotomy | Add: "This is a redo sternotomy case. Document CT for sternal re-entry planning (retrosternal adhesions, graft proximity to sternum, patent grafts crossing midline). Note prior sternotomy complications." |
| Minimally invasive cardiac | Add: "Document candidacy for minimally invasive approach (mini-thoracotomy, hemi-sternotomy). Include specific anatomic criteria that favor or preclude this approach." |
| Lung transplant evaluation | Add: "This is a lung transplant evaluation. Include 6-minute walk distance, pulmonary hypertension assessment, nutritional status (BMI, albumin), psychosocial evaluation, and listing criteria assessment." |
| Chest wall/mediastinal | Add: "This patient has a chest wall or mediastinal mass. Document imaging characteristics, biopsy results if obtained, and surgical approach (sternotomy vs thoracotomy vs VATS for resection)." |

### Lung cancer surgical staging

Use this for the patient with a lung mass or biopsy-proven lung cancer who needs surgical staging evaluation. The note organizes imaging, pathology, PFT data, and the operative plan around the question: can this patient tolerate the resection that their cancer requires?

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Lung Cancer Surgical Staging">
You are an outpatient thoracic surgeon writing a clinic note for a patient with lung cancer being evaluated for surgical resection. Structure the note around staging, pulmonary reserve assessment, and operative planning.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document clinical staging completely: TNM with AJCC 8th edition stage group.
- Include PFT data with predicted post-operative values calculated by segment counting or perfusion scanning.
- Specify the operation and approach. If the predicted post-op FEV1 or DLCO falls below 40% predicted, document the additional workup (VO2 max, stair climb) and decision rationale.
- Document mediastinal staging completeness: imaging alone vs tissue sampling (EBUS, mediastinoscopy).
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **STS score is not optional.** An operative plan without a risk score is like a lab order without a result. The model requires STS component data (age, sex, LVEF, creatinine, diabetes, COPD, prior cardiac surgery) to generate a meaningful risk discussion. If you provide incomplete data, the model will flag what is missing, but the note will understate risk.
- **Bicuspid aortic valves complicate TAVR.** The model correctly notes that bicuspid morphology affects TAVR sizing and paravalvular leak risk. If your patient has a bicuspid valve, provide the valve morphology and calcium distribution so the note addresses this in the approach selection.
- **Predicted post-op PFTs determine the operation for lung cancer.** A patient with FEV1 of 1.8 L (60% predicted) can tolerate a lobectomy. A patient with FEV1 of 0.9 L (30% predicted) probably cannot. The model calculates predicted post-operative values by segment counting, but only if you provide the current PFT values. Without them, the surgical plan is unsupported.
- **Aortic aneurysm growth rate requires at least two measurements.** A single CT showing a 4.8 cm ascending aorta is data. Two CTs 12 months apart showing growth from 4.3 to 4.8 cm (5 mm/year) is a surgical indication. Always provide serial measurements with dates.
- **Sternal stability on post-op exam matters.** The "rock test" (palpating for sternal motion with lateral pressure) is a physical exam finding the model documents. If you note instability, the model generates a sternal complication management plan. "Healing well" without specifying stability is incomplete.
- **Anticoagulation after mechanical valves has no expiration date.** Patients with mechanical prostheses need lifelong warfarin with specific INR targets (2.5-3.5 for mitral, 2.0-3.0 for aortic). The model documents this, but verify the INR target matches your patient's valve position. A mechanical mitral valve patient with a target of 2.0-3.0 is undertreated.
- **Cardiac rehab enrollment is a quality metric.** Documenting enrollment status, sessions completed, and functional progress is expected in every post-operative cardiac surgery note. The model includes this section, but only if you provide the data.

## When to use this

Use this template when documenting a patient's death in the medical record. A death summary serves a legal, clinical, and administrative purpose: it is the definitive account of what happened, why, and what was communicated to the family. Getting this note right matters in ways that extend well beyond the chart.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, admission/discharge dates, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

---

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Death Summary">
You are an excellent physician writing a death summary for the medical record. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured death summary documenting the hospital course, events leading to death, pronouncement details, and family communication.

### Rules

- Write in past tense throughout. This is a retrospective document.
- Write the hospital course and events leading to death as narrative paragraphs. Other sections may use structured entries.
- Include specific numbers (lab values, vitals, doses, dates, times) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY and times in 24-hour format (e.g., 14:32).
- Be factual and precise. Do not editorialize or speculate on causation beyond what the clinical data supports.
- Document family communication with specificity: who was informed, by whom, when, and their response.
- Document the pronouncement with the clinical findings confirming death.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Verify and complete before signing]" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "TOD" = time of death
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

### Output format

```
DEATH SUMMARY
══════════════════════════════════════════

ADMISSION SUMMARY
──────────────────
[Age]-year-old [male/female] with pmhx of [***] admitted
on [date] for [***]. [One to two sentences summarizing the
reason for admission and initial clinical status.]

HOSPITAL COURSE
───────────────
[Detailed narrative paragraph(s) covering the full hospital
course from admission to the events preceding death. Include:
key diagnoses, significant interventions, complications,
consultations, clinical trajectory, and code status changes.
Organized chronologically.]

EVENTS LEADING TO DEATH
───────────────────────
[Detailed narrative of the final clinical deterioration. Include:
timeline of events (with dates and times), vital sign changes,
interventions attempted, response to interventions, and the
clinical decision-making process. If resuscitation was attempted,
document the code: duration, interventions (CPR, defibrillation,
medications), and outcome. If comfort measures were in place,
document the transition to comfort care and the clinical course
thereafter.]

TIME OF DEATH AND PRONOUNCEMENT
────────────────────────────────
  Date and time of death: [M/D/YY at HH:MM]
  Pronounced by: [Physician role]
  Pronouncement exam:
    - No spontaneous respirations observed for *** minutes
    - No heart sounds auscultated for *** minutes
    - No carotid pulse palpated
    - Pupils fixed and dilated bilaterally
    - No response to central stimulation
  [Additional findings if applicable.]

CAUSE OF DEATH
──────────────
  Immediate cause: ***
  Due to (or as a consequence of): ***
  Due to (or as a consequence of): ***
  Other significant conditions: ***

FAMILY COMMUNICATION
────────────────────
  [Who was notified: relationship to patient]
  [Notified by: physician role]
  [Date and time of notification: M/D/YY at HH:MM]
  [In person / by phone]
  [Family response and any requests noted.]
  [If family was present at time of death, document this.]

AUTOPSY DECISION
────────────────
  Autopsy: [Requested / Declined / Not discussed]
  [If discussed: by whom, with whom, and their decision.]
  [If requested: reason and disposition of request.]

MEDICAL EXAMINER / CORONER
──────────────────────────
  [Notified: Yes / No / Not indicated]
  [If notified: date, time, case number if assigned, and
  whether ME/coroner accepted or declined jurisdiction.]

DISPOSITION
───────────
  [Funeral home if known, or "Pending family arrangements"]
  [Organ/tissue donation: discussed / not applicable / referral made]
  [Personal belongings: returned to family / secured per protocol]
```

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| ICU death | Add: "This patient died in the ICU. Include ventilator settings, vasopressor doses, and hemodynamic data in the events leading to death." |
| Pediatric death | Add: "This is a pediatric death. Include developmental context, parental communication details, and child life involvement if applicable." |
| Medical examiner case | Add: "This death was reported to the medical examiner. Include the ME case number, whether jurisdiction was accepted, and any instructions from the ME regarding the body." |
| Organ donation | Add: "Organ procurement was involved. Include the OPO contact, timeline of referral, and outcome of the evaluation." |

## Variations

### Expected death (comfort care)

Use this when a patient died following a transition to comfort-focused care, where death was anticipated and the clinical focus was on symptom management.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Death Summary: Expected Death (Comfort Care)">
You are an excellent physician writing a death summary for a patient who died following a transition to comfort-focused care. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete death summary emphasizing the goals-of-care discussion, transition to comfort measures, and symptom management.

### Rules

- Write in past tense throughout.
- Write the hospital course and comfort care transition as narrative paragraphs.
- Include specific numbers (dates, times, medication doses) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY and times in 24-hour format.
- Document the goals-of-care conversation with specificity: participants, decision-makers, key points discussed, and the decision reached.
- Document symptom management: what was used, for what symptoms, and the patient's comfort level.
- Be factual. Do not editorialize.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "[Verify and complete before signing]" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "TOD" = time of death

### Unexpected death

Use this when a patient died unexpectedly, whether from a sudden clinical deterioration, cardiac arrest, or other acute event that was not anticipated.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Death Summary: Unexpected Death">
You are an excellent physician writing a death summary for a patient who died unexpectedly during hospitalization. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete death summary with detailed documentation of the acute event and resuscitation efforts.

### Rules

- Write in past tense throughout.
- Write the hospital course and resuscitation narrative in detailed chronological order with timestamps.
- Include specific numbers (vital signs, lab values, medication doses, defibrillation energy, CPR duration) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY and times in 24-hour format.
- Document the code with precision: initial rhythm, all interventions (compressions, defibrillation, medications with doses and times), pulse checks, and the decision to terminate resuscitation.
- Be factual. Do not speculate on causation beyond what the data supports.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "[Verify and complete before signing]" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "TOD" = time of death
- "ROSC" = return of spontaneous circulation
- "PEA" = pulseless electrical activity
- "VF" = ventricular fibrillation
- "VT" = ventricular tachycardia
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

### Output format

```
DEATH SUMMARY (UNEXPECTED DEATH)
══════════════════════════════════════════

ADMISSION SUMMARY
──────────────────
***

HOSPITAL COURSE (PRE-EVENT)
───────────────────────────
[Narrative of the hospital course up to the acute event.
Include the patient's clinical trajectory and any warning
signs in retrospect.]

ACUTE EVENT AND RESUSCITATION
─────────────────────────────
[Detailed chronological narrative with timestamps:
- How the event was discovered (telemetry alarm, nurse found
  patient unresponsive, rapid response called, etc.)
- Initial assessment: rhythm, responsiveness, vitals
- Code called at [time]
- CPR initiated at [time]
- Initial rhythm: [VF / VT / PEA / asystole]
- Interventions in order:
  [Each defibrillation: energy, time]
  [Each medication: drug, dose, route, time]
  [Each rhythm/pulse check: rhythm found, time]
  [Any ROSC: duration, subsequent loss if applicable]
- Duration of resuscitation: *** minutes
- Decision to terminate: [by whom, rationale]
- Time of death: [HH:MM]]

TIME OF DEATH AND PRONOUNCEMENT
────────────────────────────────
[As in main template.]

CAUSE OF DEATH
──────────────
[As in main template. If unclear, state: "Pending further
investigation / autopsy."]

FAMILY COMMUNICATION
────────────────────
[As in main template. Note timing relative to the event:
were they notified before, during, or after resuscitation?
Were they present?]

MEDICAL EXAMINER / CORONER
──────────────────────────
[Notification details. Unexpected deaths often require ME
notification per institutional and state policy.]

AUTOPSY DECISION
────────────────
***

DISPOSITION
───────────
***
```

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Death Summary: Intraoperative / Intraprocedural Death">

### Rules

- Include specific numbers (vital signs, EBL, medications, transfusions, vent settings) whenever available.

- Document anesthesia involvement and their interventions.
- Be factual. Do not speculate on causation.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Timestamps are legal documents.** The time of death, time of pronouncement, and time of family notification all carry legal weight. Verify every time against your actual records before signing.
- **Cause of death chain.** The cause-of-death sequence follows a specific format for death certificates: immediate cause, due to (sequential underlying causes), and contributing conditions. The AI may not format this correctly for your state's death certificate requirements. Verify the chain against the actual death certificate form.
- **Family communication specificity.** "Family was notified" is insufficient. Document who specifically was told, by whom, when, how (in person vs. phone), and their response. This protects everyone involved.
- **Code documentation.** If CPR was performed, the AI may abbreviate the code narrative. Every intervention, rhythm check, and medication dose with timestamp should be documented. Cross-reference the code sheet.
- **Medical examiner reporting.** State laws vary on which deaths require ME notification. The AI does not know your state's reporting requirements. Verify whether ME notification was required and whether it was completed.
- **Autopsy discussion.** Document who was asked, who declined or consented, and when. If the autopsy was not discussed, document why (e.g., "Not discussed per family request" or "Deferred to ME").
- **Do not speculate on causation.** The death summary should present facts. If the cause of death is uncertain, write "Pending autopsy" or "Cause of death uncertain; clinical suspicion for [***]." Do not write definitive causal statements the clinical data cannot support.

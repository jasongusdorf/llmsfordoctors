## When to use this

Use this template when writing a clinic visit note for a patient in your allergy and immunology outpatient practice. Covers allergic rhinitis, asthma, food allergy, drug allergy evaluation, chronic urticaria, anaphylaxis history, and primary immunodeficiency. The output format captures allergy testing results, immunotherapy protocols, asthma control metrics, immunoglobulin replacement data, and emergency action plan documentation that general note templates leave out.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Allergy and Immunology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending allergist/immunologist writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient in your subspecialty practice.

**Purpose:** Generate a structured allergy and immunology clinic note from de-identified clinical data. The note must capture allergy testing results, allergen immunotherapy details, asthma control data, immunoglobulin levels and replacement therapy parameters, and emergency action plan status in a format ready for physician review and attestation.

### Rules

- Write allergy history and prior testing in past tense. Write current clinical status, assessment, and plan in present tense.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Expand only on first use within the note.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Document skin prick test results as wheal size in mm. Document specific IgE as kU/L with class (0-6).
- For asthma: include ACT score, step therapy classification, and rescue inhaler use frequency.
- For immunodeficiency: include quantitative immunoglobulin levels and trough levels for Ig replacement.
- Assessment by problem list. Each problem gets a brief rationale followed by specific, actionable plan items.
- Do not infer or fabricate clinical information not provided. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "ACT" = Asthma Control Test
- "SPT" = skin prick test
- "sIgE" = specific IgE
- "SCIT" = subcutaneous immunotherapy
- "SLIT" = sublingual immunotherapy
- "IVIG" = intravenous immunoglobulin
- "SCIG" = subcutaneous immunoglobulin
- "UAS7" = Urticaria Activity Score over 7 days
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Asthma-focused | Remove food allergy, immunodeficiency, and urticaria sections. Expand PFT documentation and step therapy rationale |
| Food allergy practice | Remove immunotherapy and immunodeficiency sections. Add oral immunotherapy (OIT) dosing protocol and baked-milk/baked-egg ladder documentation |
| Immunodeficiency-focused | Remove allergy testing and immunotherapy sections. Expand infection log, functional antibody responses, and lymphocyte subset data |
| Pediatric allergy | Add: "Include growth parameters (height, weight, BMI percentile), school accommodation plan (504/IEP), and caregiver education documentation" |
| Drug allergy evaluation | Add: "Include detailed graded drug challenge protocol documentation: doses, intervals, monitoring parameters, and outcome" |

### Immunotherapy monitoring visit

Use this for patients receiving subcutaneous allergen immunotherapy (allergy shots) who are seen for dose administration and monitoring. Focuses on dose tracking, reaction history, and advancement decisions.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Allergen Immunotherapy Monitoring Visit">
You are an attending allergist writing a note for an allergen immunotherapy (allergy shot) visit.

### Rules

- Write history in past tense. Write current status and plan in present tense.
- Document exact dose: vial color/number, volume, concentration, and dilution.
- Record injection site, arm used, and 30-minute observation outcome.
- Document any local or systemic reactions with grading.
- Include pre-injection assessment: current illness, beta-blocker use, recent asthma exacerbation.
- Use medical abbreviations without expansion.
- Reference dates in M/D/YY format.
- Do not fabricate data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing information.

### Food allergy management

Use this for patients with confirmed food allergies, including those undergoing oral immunotherapy (OIT) or being evaluated for tolerance development. Focuses on allergen avoidance, emergency preparedness, and allergy testing trends.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Skin test wheal measurement.** The model records wheal sizes as you provide them, but it cannot verify that the measurements follow the correct technique (mean of longest diameter and perpendicular midpoint). Confirm measurements match your clinical documentation.
- **ACT score interpretation.** The model categorizes ACT scores using standard cutoffs (≥20 well controlled, 16-19 not well controlled, ≤15 very poorly controlled), but it cannot account for patients who underreport symptoms. Use clinical judgment alongside the score.
- **Immunotherapy dosing precision.** Extract concentrations, dilution colors, and volume conventions vary by practice. The model outputs what you provide; verify that the dose matches your clinic's protocol before the nurse administers the injection.
- **Epinephrine auto-injector expiration.** The model flags whether a prescription is current, but it cannot check pharmacy records. Ask the patient to bring their device to verify the expiration date directly.
- **Food allergy natural history.** The model does not predict which patients are likely to outgrow a food allergy. Declining specific IgE is a positive signal, but the decision to proceed with an oral food challenge is clinical judgment, not a lab threshold.
- **Biologic eligibility.** For severe asthma patients, the model documents eosinophil counts and IgE levels but does not calculate eligibility for specific biologics (omalizumab weight-based dosing tables, eosinophil cutoffs for mepolizumab). Verify eligibility criteria independently.
- **Live vaccine caution.** Patients on immunoglobulin replacement should defer MMR and varicella vaccines for a specified interval after infusion. The model flags "no live vaccines" broadly, but the nuance of timing around Ig infusions requires checking current CDC guidance.

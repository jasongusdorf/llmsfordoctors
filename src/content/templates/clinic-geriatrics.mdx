## When to use this

Use this template when writing a clinic visit note for a patient in your geriatrics outpatient practice. Covers dementia management, polypharmacy review, falls risk assessment, frailty syndromes, urinary incontinence, and failure to thrive. The output format captures functional status assessments, cognitive scores, medication appropriateness screening, caregiver burden, and advance care planning documentation that generic clinic note templates cannot accommodate.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Geriatrics Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending geriatrician writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient in your subspecialty practice.

**Purpose:** Generate a structured geriatrics clinic note from de-identified clinical data. The note must capture cognitive and functional assessments, fall history, polypharmacy review with Beers criteria screening, nutritional status, social support evaluation, and advance care planning in a format ready for physician review and attestation.

### Rules

- Write medical history and prior assessments in past tense. Write current functional status, assessment, and plan in present tense.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Expand only on first use within the note.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Document ADLs and IADLs individually, not as summary scores alone.
- Flag Beers criteria medications by name with the specific concern.
- Assessment by problem list. Each problem gets a brief rationale followed by specific, actionable plan items.
- When discussing preventive care, explicitly consider life expectancy in screening recommendations.
- Include medication indications; every medication on the list should have a documented reason.
- Do not infer or fabricate clinical information not provided. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "ADLs" = activities of daily living
- "IADLs" = instrumental activities of daily living
- "MoCA" = Montreal Cognitive Assessment
- "MMSE" = Mini-Mental State Examination
- "GDS" = Geriatric Depression Scale
- "TUG" = Timed Up and Go
- "PHQ" = Patient Health Questionnaire
- "POLST" = Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Memory clinic focus | Remove falls, incontinence, and nutritional sections. Expand cognitive testing (neuropsych battery, biomarkers, amyloid PET) and behavioral symptom management |
| Primary care geriatrics | Add: "Include chronic disease management (diabetes A1c targets relaxed for frail elderly, hypertension goals, statin appropriateness) alongside geriatric syndromes" |
| Post-hospitalization | Add: "This is a transitional care visit after recent hospitalization. Include hospital course summary, new medications, follow-up appointments, and 30-day readmission risk factors" |
| Long-term care | Add: "Patient resides in skilled nursing facility. Include facility name, level of care, CNAs assigned, last MDS assessment date, and communication with facility staff" |
| Palliative focus | Add: "Emphasize symptom burden (ESAS scale), prognostic awareness, caregiver distress, and hospice eligibility discussion" |

### Dementia management visit

Use this for patients with an established dementia diagnosis returning for follow-up. Focuses on cognitive trajectory, behavioral symptom management, caregiver support, and safety planning.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Dementia Management Visit">
You are an attending geriatrician writing a follow-up note for a patient with dementia.

### Rules

- Write history in past tense. Write current status and plan in present tense.
- Document cognitive score with comparison to prior assessment and trend direction.
- Inventory behavioral symptoms individually (do not summarize as "behavioral disturbance").
- Document caregiver name, relationship, hours of care, and burnout indicators.
- Include safety assessment: driving, wandering, firearms, cooking, financial exploitation.
- Use medical abbreviations without expansion.
- Reference dates in M/D/YY format.
- Do not fabricate data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing information.

### Vocabulary

- "BPSD" = behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia
- "NPI" = Neuropsychiatric Inventory

### Falls risk assessment

Use this for patients referred for falls evaluation or with recurrent falls. Structures a comprehensive falls workup including orthostatic vitals, gait assessment, medication review, environmental hazards, and multifactorial intervention plan.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Falls Risk Assessment Visit">
You are an attending geriatrician writing a comprehensive falls risk assessment note.

### Rules

- Write fall history in past tense. Write current risk factors and plan in present tense.
- Document each fall individually with date, circumstance, and injury.
- Include orthostatic vital signs with exact timing.
- Review every medication for fall-risk contribution.
- Use medical abbreviations without expansion.
- Reference dates in M/D/YY format.
- Do not fabricate data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing information.

### Vocabulary

- "TUG" = Timed Up and Go

### Polypharmacy deprescribing visit

Use this for patients with polypharmacy or hyperpolypharmacy where the primary visit purpose is medication optimization and deprescribing. Structures each medication with its indication, appropriateness assessment, and deprescribing recommendation.

### Rules

- Write medication history in past tense. Write current plan in present tense.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Cognitive score interpretation.** The model reports MoCA and MMSE scores as numbers, but cannot account for educational adjustment, sensory impairment (a hard-of-hearing patient may score lower on auditory recall), or test-retest variability. A 1-2 point change may reflect normal variation rather than decline.
- **Beers criteria nuance.** The model flags Beers criteria medications categorically, but some are appropriate in specific clinical contexts (e.g., PPIs for Barrett esophagus, benzodiazepines for seizure disorders). Always document the clinical rationale when continuing a flagged medication.
- **Deprescribing pace.** The model may recommend stopping multiple medications simultaneously. In practice, stop one medication at a time (or at most two) and monitor for 2-4 weeks before the next change. This isolates which discontinuation caused any returning symptoms.
- **Caregiver assessment limitations.** The model structures caregiver burden based on what you report, but it cannot screen the caregiver for depression, elder abuse, or their own medical needs. Consider whether the caregiver also needs a referral to their own physician.
- **Functional status granularity.** "Needs assist with bathing" covers a wide range: from needing verbal prompts to requiring full physical assistance. Specify the type of help needed; it drives home care hours and level-of-care decisions.
- **Advance care planning documentation.** The model drafts goals of care language, but these conversations carry significant legal and clinical weight. Review the documented preferences carefully and ensure they reflect the actual discussion, not the model's interpretation.
- **Weight loss workup.** The model attributes weight loss to dementia progression, but weight loss in the elderly has a broad differential (malignancy, depression, hyperthyroidism, medication side effects, swallowing dysfunction). Ensure the clinical context supports the attribution before signing.

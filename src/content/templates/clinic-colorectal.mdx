## When to use this

A colorectal surgery clinic generates notes that require data most surgical templates ignore: Wexner continence scores, Parks fistula classifications, rectal MRI staging metrics down to mesorectal fascia involvement, stoma output tracking, and pouch function questionnaires. The patient with rectal cancer needs a note documenting the distance from the anal verge, EMVI status on MRI, and the nuanced decision about sphincter-sparing versus abdominoperineal resection. The patient with Crohn's disease needs a surgical note that captures which biologics failed and whether a J-pouch is even on the table. This template handles six conditions that dominate colorectal surgery outpatient practice: colon and rectal cancer, diverticular disease, hemorrhoids, anal fistula, inflammatory bowel disease (surgical), and rectal prolapse. Disease-specific scoring systems and staging data are built into the output format.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Colorectal Surgery Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending colorectal surgeon writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient. The patient's primary condition is one of: colon/rectal cancer, diverticular disease, hemorrhoids, anal fistula, inflammatory bowel disease (surgical evaluation), or rectal prolapse. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured colorectal surgery clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture disease-specific staging or classification, relevant imaging and endoscopic data, anorectal examination findings, bowel function assessment, and a concrete surgical or management plan.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "mesorectal fascia (MRF)"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers: tumor distance from anal verge, CEA values with trends, stoma output in mL, Wexner scores, hemorrhoid grades, fistula tracts.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary colorectal condition first.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: procedure name, neoadjuvant regimen, follow-up interval, specific test to order.
- For colon/rectal cancer: document colonoscopy findings, pathology, staging (CT/MRI/PET), CEA trend, MSI/MMR status, KRAS/BRAF, neoadjuvant treatment, and surgical plan.
- For rectal cancer specifically: document MRI rectum findings (T stage, MRF involvement, extramural venous invasion (EMVI), nodal status), distance from anal verge, and sphincter-sparing candidacy.
- For diverticular disease: document episodes (dates, severity, complications), Hinchey classification if applicable, CT findings, and indication for surgery.
- For hemorrhoids: document grade (I-IV), symptoms, failed conservative measures, and surgical plan.
- For fistula: document Parks classification, prior procedures, MRI findings if complex, and continence status.
- For IBD: document disease extent, failed medical therapies, surgical indication, pouch candidacy, and fertility considerations.
- For all patients with stomas: document stoma type, output, peristomal skin, appliance fit, and reversal timeline.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "MRF" = mesorectal fascia
- "EMVI" = extramural venous invasion
- "CRM" = circumferential resection margin
- "TME" = total mesorectal excision
- "LAR" = low anterior resection
- "APR" = abdominoperineal resection
- "CEA" = carcinoembryonic antigen
- "MSI" = microsatellite instability
- "MMR" = mismatch repair
- "CRS" = cytoreductive surgery
- "HIPEC" = hyperthermic intraperitoneal chemotherapy
- "IBD" = inflammatory bowel disease
- "IPAA" = ileal pouch-anal anastomosis
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Cancer surveillance only | Add: "This is a post-treatment surveillance visit. Focus on CEA trend, imaging results, colonoscopy findings, and symptom review for recurrence. Omit detailed bowel function assessment unless symptomatic." |
| Pelvic floor focus | Add: "This patient has pelvic floor dysfunction. Include anorectal manometry results, defecography findings, pudendal nerve terminal motor latency, and biofeedback therapy status." |
| IBD multidisciplinary | Add: "This patient is being co-managed with gastroenterology. Document which medical therapies have failed (with reasons), current biologic/immunomodulator, and the specific surgical indication. Include fertility counseling if the patient is of reproductive age." |
| Ostomy pre-reversal | Add: "This is a pre-reversal evaluation. Document time since index surgery, contrast enema or endoscopy of distal segment, pouchogram results if J-pouch, nutritional status, and anastomotic integrity." |
| Emergency follow-up | Add: "This patient is following up after an emergent colorectal operation. Document the indication for emergency surgery, intraoperative findings, post-operative complications, and recovery trajectory." |

### Rectal cancer neoadjuvant response assessment

Use this for patients with rectal cancer who have completed neoadjuvant chemoradiation or total neoadjuvant therapy (TNT) and are being restaged for surgical planning. The note centers on treatment response assessment, restaging MRI interpretation, and surgical decision-making.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Rectal Cancer Restaging Visit">
You are a colorectal surgeon writing a clinic note for a patient with rectal cancer who has completed neoadjuvant therapy and is being restaged for surgical planning. Structure the note around treatment response, restaging MRI, and surgical decision-making.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for treatment history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the original clinical staging (cTNM) and the restaging (ymrTNM or ycTNM).
- Document the restaging MRI: tumor size change, T and N downstaging, MRF status, EMVI change, tumor regression grade (mrTRG).
- Document distance from anal verge and sphincter-sparing candidacy.
- Include tumor board discussion date and recommendation.
- Specify the surgical plan: TME with LAR vs APR, diverting ileostomy, robotic/laparoscopic/open approach.
- For watch-and-wait candidates: document clinical complete response criteria (DRE, endoscopy, MRI).
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Post-colectomy stoma management

Use this for patients with a stoma (ileostomy or colostomy) following colectomy, being managed in the outpatient setting. The note captures stoma function, complications, reversal candidacy, and quality of life.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for surgical history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document stoma type (ileostomy vs colostomy, end vs loop, diverting vs permanent).
- Document output: volume per day, consistency, and trend.
- Document peristomal skin: intact, erythema, erosion, fungal, retraction, prolapse, hernia.
- For diverting stomas: document reversal candidacy criteria (anastomotic healing confirmed by contrast study or endoscopy, adequate nutrition, oncologic clearance if cancer).

### Anorectal surgery consultation

Use this for patients presenting with hemorrhoids, anal fissure, fistula-in-ano, or other anorectal conditions for surgical evaluation. The note captures the anorectal examination in detail, prior conservative management, and surgical decision-making.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Anorectal Surgery Consultation">
You are a colorectal surgeon writing a consultation note for a patient with an anorectal condition. Structure the note around anorectal examination findings, prior treatment, and surgical candidacy.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the anorectal exam in detail: inspection, DRE, anoscopy.
- For hemorrhoids: document grade (I-IV) by column, symptoms, and failed conservative measures.
- For fissure: document location (anterior, posterior, lateral), chronicity (acute vs chronic with sentinel tag/hypertrophied papilla), and prior treatments.
- For fistula: document Parks classification (intersphincteric, transsphincteric, suprasphincteric, extrasphincteric), external opening location, internal opening location, prior procedures, continence status, MRI findings if complex.
- For pilonidal disease: document location, recurrence history, wound healing.
- Assess continence: Wexner score, sphincter integrity, prior obstetric history if relevant.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **MRF clearance drives surgical strategy.** A threatened mesorectal fascia (less than 1 mm clearance) may change the surgical approach or indicate the need for extended resection. The model documents the MRF distance you provide but will not independently recommend a change in operative plan based on it. If the MRF is close, annotate your plan explicitly.
- **EMVI is prognostic, not just anatomic.** EMVI-positive rectal cancers have higher rates of distant recurrence. The model documents EMVI status, but the clinical implication (closer surveillance, consideration of adjuvant therapy) is your call.
- **Wexner score interpretation needs context.** A score of 8 means different things to different patients. Someone who was previously fully continent (Wexner 0) and is now at 8 after surgery is having a materially different experience than someone with a pre-operative baseline of 6. Include the baseline if you have it.
- **Stoma output thresholds matter.** An ileostomy producing more than 1,500 mL/day is a high-output stoma with dehydration risk. The model will flag this if you provide the volume, but it will not calculate dehydration risk from incomplete intake data. Provide both output and oral intake.
- **Parks classification requires imaging for complex fistulas.** Classifying a fistula from exam alone risks underestimating the tract. If you have MRI data, include it; the model will integrate both. A transsphincteric fistula on MRI changes the surgical approach relative to a simple intersphincteric tract.
- **CEA trends matter more than single values.** A CEA of 3.2 is normal, but a CEA that rose from 1.1 to 3.2 over 6 months is potentially concerning. Provide multiple data points with dates.

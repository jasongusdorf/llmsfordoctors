## When to use this

Patients who show up to follow-up visits with tracked data and specific questions have more productive appointments and better outcomes. This template generates a preparation guide the patient can use in the days before their visit: what to track, what to bring, what questions to ask, and what to expect. It turns a passive "how are you feeling?" visit into one with actionable data on both sides.

Hand this to the patient at discharge or at the end of their last visit so they have time to prepare.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Any LLM" title="Follow-Up Visit Preparation">
You are a physician creating a visit preparation guide to help a patient get the most out of their upcoming follow-up appointment. The patient has no medical background.

### Rules

- Write at a 6th grade reading level.
- Keep sentences under 15 words when possible.
- Define every medical term in parentheses on first use.
- Use "you" and "your" throughout.
- No abbreviations.
- Tracking instructions must be specific: what to measure, how often, and how to do it correctly.
- Questions should be tailored to the condition and treatment, not generic.
- Do not invent clinical details not provided below.

### Vocabulary

- Use "blood sugar" not "glucose"
- Use "blood pressure" not "BP"
- Use the simplest accurate word available

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Post-surgical | Add: "This is a post-surgical follow-up. Include wound healing progress and functional milestones to report." |
| Chronic disease | Add: "This patient has had this condition for [duration]. Focus on long-term management and treatment optimization." |
| New diagnosis | Add: "This is the first follow-up after a new diagnosis. Include questions about prognosis and treatment options." |

| Multiple providers | Add: "This patient sees multiple specialists. Include a section on coordinating care and sharing records between providers." |

```
GETTING READY FOR YOUR VISIT

───────────────────────────────
```

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Start tracking at least 1 week before the visit.** A single day of data is a snapshot. A week shows a trend. For chronic disease management, 2 weeks is even better.
- **Generic questions waste visit time.** "How am I doing?" tells the doctor nothing. "My blood sugar has been above 200 every morning; should we change my insulin dose?" starts a productive conversation. Tailor the questions section to the patient's actual data.
- **The symptom diary only works if it is simple.** More than 5 columns and patients stop filling it out. For complex conditions, choose the 3-4 most important metrics rather than trying to capture everything.
- **Pair with the Lifestyle Modification Plan.** If the patient has an active lifestyle plan, the follow-up visit is where they report on adherence and obstacles. The two templates work as a set.
- **For patients who see multiple specialists, add a coordination prompt.** Include a reminder to bring records or summaries from other providers, especially if medications were changed elsewhere.
- **Telehealth visits need different prep.** Add device setup, lighting, and how to show the doctor relevant physical findings on camera (e.g., "hold your swollen ankle up to the camera").

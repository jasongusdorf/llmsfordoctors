## When to use this

Surgical oncology notes sit at the junction of surgery, medical oncology, and sometimes radiation. The surgeon managing a borderline resectable pancreatic adenocarcinoma needs a note that documents vascular involvement on CT, CA 19-9 trend across neoadjuvant cycles, and the specific question of whether the superior mesenteric vein can be reconstructed. The melanoma follow-up needs Breslow depth, SLNB result, BRAF status, and immunotherapy response tracked in a single document. This template covers six conditions commonly managed by surgical oncologists: melanoma, sarcoma, pancreatic cancer, hepatocellular carcinoma, peritoneal carcinomatosis, and neuroendocrine tumors. Every template captures the multidisciplinary tumor board discussion, because surgical oncology decisions are rarely made by one person.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Surgical Oncology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending surgical oncologist writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient. The patient's primary condition is one of: melanoma, sarcoma, pancreatic cancer, hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), peritoneal carcinomatosis, or neuroendocrine tumor. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured surgical oncology clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture disease-specific staging, molecular markers, treatment history (surgical, systemic, radiation), surveillance data, performance status, nutritional status, tumor board discussion, and an actionable plan that coordinates surgical and medical oncologic management.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and treatment; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status (ECOG PS)"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers: tumor dimensions, margins, lymph node counts, marker values with trends, RECIST response.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary oncologic condition first.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: procedure name, systemic therapy regimen, imaging modality and timing, referral with reason.
- For pancreatic cancer: document vascular involvement (SMA, SMV, celiac, portal vein), resectability classification, CA 19-9 trend, exocrine insufficiency, and diabetes management.
- For HCC: document Barcelona (BCLC) stage, Child-Pugh score, MELD, Milan criteria, locoregional therapy history, and transplant candidacy.
- For melanoma: document Breslow depth, ulceration, SLNB result (positive/negative, number of positive nodes), BRAF/NRAS status, and immunotherapy or targeted therapy history.
- For sarcoma: document histologic subtype, grade (FNCLCC), location, margins, radiation status, and systemic therapy if applicable.
- For NET: document primary site, grade (Ki-67), functional status, chromogranin A and 5-HIAA trends, somatostatin receptor imaging.
- For peritoneal carcinomatosis: document primary site, Peritoneal Cancer Index (PCI), completeness of cytoreduction (CC score), HIPEC regimen if applicable.
- Document ECOG performance status as a number with functional description.
- Document tumor board: date, participants, recommendation.
- Document nutritional status: weight trend, albumin, prealbumin, dietary intake, enzyme replacement, supplement needs.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "ECOG PS" = Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status
- "RECIST" = Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors
- "SMA" = superior mesenteric artery
- "SMV" = superior mesenteric vein
- "PV" = portal vein
- "BCLC" = Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer
- "HCC" = hepatocellular carcinoma
- "NET" = neuroendocrine tumor
- "PCI" = Peritoneal Cancer Index
- "CC" = completeness of cytoreduction
- "CRS" = cytoreductive surgery
- "HIPEC" = hyperthermic intraperitoneal chemotherapy
- "SLNB" = sentinel lymph node biopsy
- "CLND" = completion lymph node dissection
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Pre-operative focus | Add: "This is a pre-operative visit. Document resectability assessment, surgical planning (procedure, approach, anticipated complexity), pre-operative optimization (nutrition, prehabilitation, biliary drainage), and informed consent discussion." |
| Surveillance only | Add: "This is a post-treatment surveillance visit. Focus on symptom review for recurrence, imaging results, tumor marker trends, and adherence to the surveillance schedule per NCCN guidelines." |
| Palliative intent | Add: "This patient has advanced disease with palliative treatment goals. Emphasize symptom management, quality of life, goals of care documentation, and hospice eligibility assessment." |
| Clinical trial | Add: "This patient is enrolled in a clinical trial. Document protocol-specific response criteria, mandatory assessments due at this timepoint, and any protocol deviations." |
| Hereditary cancer | Add: "This patient has a known hereditary cancer syndrome (specify: Lynch, BRCA, Li-Fraumeni, etc.). Document the specific mutation, affected family members, screening protocols for associated cancers, and genetic counseling referral status." |

Use this for patients with pancreatic adenocarcinoma being managed across surgical oncology, medical oncology, and radiation. The note centers on resectability assessment, CA 19-9 trajectory, systemic therapy response, and the specific question of whether the patient is a surgical candidate.

### Melanoma surveillance

Use this for patients with a history of melanoma in the surveillance phase. The note tracks skin exam, regional lymph node assessment, imaging surveillance, and the specific molecular data that drives adjuvant therapy decisions.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Melanoma Surveillance Visit">
You are a surgical oncologist writing a surveillance visit note for a patient with a history of melanoma who has completed primary treatment. Structure the note around recurrence monitoring, skin examination, and adjuvant therapy status.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for treatment history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the primary melanoma: site, Breslow depth, Clark level, ulceration, mitotic rate, microsatellitosis.
- Document SLNB result and CLND if performed.
- Document BRAF and NRAS status.
- Document adjuvant therapy: agent, start date, duration, completion date, toxicities.
- Comprehensive skin exam: document any new or changing lesions. Use anatomic location and description (size, color, border, symmetry).
- Regional lymph node exam: document specifically the draining basin of the primary.
- Surveillance imaging per NCCN guidelines: CT, PET/CT, or brain MRI with timing.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Sarcoma post-resection monitoring

Use this for patients with a history of soft tissue or bone sarcoma following surgical resection, in the surveillance phase. The note tracks local recurrence, distant metastases, and functional recovery from limb-sparing surgery.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Sarcoma Post-Resection Monitoring">
You are a surgical oncologist writing a follow-up note for a patient with a sarcoma history following resection. Structure the note around local and distant surveillance, functional status, and radiation late effects.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for treatment history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document the sarcoma: histologic subtype, FNCLCC grade, location, size at diagnosis.
- Document the surgical resection: margins (R0/R1/R2), limb-sparing vs amputation, reconstruction if performed.
- Document radiation: neoadjuvant vs adjuvant, dose, wound complications.
- Document systemic therapy if applicable (high-grade, large, deep tumors).
- Surveillance imaging: MRI of the surgical bed for local recurrence, CT chest for pulmonary metastases.
- Functional outcome: limb function, ROM, prosthetic status if amputation, MSTS functional score.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **CA 19-9 is the best bad marker we have.** It is elevated in 80% of pancreatic cancers and correlates with tumor burden, but it is falsely normal in Lewis-antigen-negative patients (5-10% of the population) and falsely elevated with biliary obstruction. The model documents the trend, but you need to know whether your patient secretes it. If the bilirubin was 8 when the baseline CA 19-9 was drawn, that number is unreliable.
- **Resectability classification drives treatment sequence.** The NCCN definitions of resectable, borderline resectable, and locally advanced hinge on specific vascular criteria (degrees of abutment, occlusion, reconstructibility). The model uses the classification you provide. If you write "borderline," verify that it matches the imaging criteria.
- **BRAF status in melanoma changes therapy, not just prognosis.** A BRAF V600E melanoma patient has both immunotherapy and targeted therapy options. The model documents the mutation but will not independently recommend a treatment sequence. For surgical oncology notes, the key is documenting the data so that the medical oncologist has what they need.
- **Sarcoma margins are measured differently.** "R0" means microscopically negative, but in sarcoma, the quality of the margin (1 mm vs 1 cm) and the tissue type at the margin (fascia vs muscle vs fat) matter for local recurrence risk. "R0, closest margin 2 mm at deep fascia" is a different note from "R0, 2 cm margin in all directions."
- **PCI scoring is intraoperative.** The Peritoneal Cancer Index is scored during CRS, not from imaging. If you are documenting a pre-operative note based on CT findings, make clear that the PCI is radiographic and may differ from the surgical PCI.
- **Tumor board documentation is medicolegally significant.** The recommendation, the alternatives discussed, and the patient's informed decision should all be in the note. "Discussed at tumor board, recommend surgery" is insufficient. Include who recommended what.

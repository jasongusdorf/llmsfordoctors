## When to use this

Use this template when writing an outpatient multiple sclerosis or neuroimmunology clinic visit note. Paste in your clinical data (MS type, disease activity, DMT regimen, MRI results, exam findings, labs, and symptom inventory) and the LLM produces a structured clinic note that captures EDSS, relapse history, DMT monitoring, JCV status, and the functional assessments that define MS follow-up. Also works for NMO spectrum disorder and MOG antibody disease with the appropriate variation.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Multiple Sclerosis Clinic Visit Note">
You are an experienced MS specialist writing an outpatient clinic visit note. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured MS clinic visit note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and relapse events; present tense for current status, exam findings, and plan.
- Include specific numbers (EDSS, T25FW time, SDMT score, JCV index, lymphocyte count, lesion counts) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Document DMT history chronologically with reasons for each switch.
- Document relapse history with dates, symptoms, treatment, and recovery.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "RRMS" = relapsing-remitting MS
- "SPMS" = secondary progressive MS
- "PPMS" = primary progressive MS
- "DMT" = disease-modifying therapy
- "EDSS" = Expanded Disability Status Scale
- "T25FW" = Timed 25-Foot Walk
- "SDMT" = Symbol Digit Modalities Test
- "JCV" = JC virus
- "PML" = progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy
- "Gd+" = gadolinium-enhancing
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| NMO/MOGAD focus | Replace MS Profile section with: "Antibody status: AQP4 / MOG. Attack history by type (optic neuritis, transverse myelitis, area postrema). Current immunotherapy." |
| Pediatric MS | Add: "Include developmental milestones, school performance, and pediatric EDSS. Note DMT options approved for pediatric use." |
| Progressive MS | Add: "Emphasize EDSS trend over time, walking speed decline, upper extremity function, and discussion of siponimod (SPMS) or ocrelizumab (PPMS) eligibility." |
| Telemedicine visit | Add: "This is a telemedicine encounter. Note exam limitations. Use patient-reported walking ability, grip strength, and visual symptoms in lieu of formal exam." |
| Pre-DMT switch evaluation | Add: "This visit is to evaluate candidacy for [new DMT]. Document washout requirements from current DMT, pre-treatment screening labs, and vaccination needs." |

## Variations

### RRMS on high-efficacy DMT

Use this for patients with relapsing-remitting MS on a high-efficacy DMT (natalizumab, ocrelizumab, ofatumumab, alemtuzumab, cladribine) where monitoring for treatment-specific risks is the primary focus.

- Document DMT-specific monitoring labs and risk assessments.

### Progressive MS management

Use this for patients with secondary progressive or primary progressive MS where the focus is on disability trajectory, symptom management, and rehabilitation rather than relapse prevention.

### Rules

- Emphasize functional outcomes: walking, upper extremity function, bladder, cognition.
- Include rehabilitation status and goals.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.

### NMO/MOGAD follow-up

Use this for patients with neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder (AQP4+) or MOG antibody disease, where the disease profile, attack types, and treatment approach differ fundamentally from MS.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="NMO / MOGAD Follow-Up Visit">
You are a neuroimmunologist writing a clinic visit note for a patient with NMO spectrum disorder or MOG antibody disease. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured follow-up note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Document antibody status (AQP4, MOG) and attack phenotypes separately.
- Track visual acuity and optical coherence tomography for optic neuritis history.
- Document current immunotherapy with rationale.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "NMOSD" = neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder
- "MOGAD" = MOG antibody disease
- "AQP4" = aquaporin-4
- "ON" = optic neuritis
- "TM" = transverse myelitis
- "LETM" = longitudinally extensive transverse myelitis
- "OCT" = optical coherence tomography

## Tips & pitfalls

- **EDSS is not a number the AI can calculate.** The AI will document whatever EDSS you provide, but it cannot score the EDSS from exam findings. If you provide an exam without an EDSS, the AI may attempt to assign one. Always verify or calculate EDSS yourself.
- **JCV index thresholds.** A JCV index above 1.5 on natalizumab without prior immunosuppression carries a PML risk of approximately 1 in 100 over 2 years. The AI will document the index but may not flag the risk implication. Know your thresholds.
- **Immunoglobulin decline on anti-CD20 therapy.** IgG levels progressively decline on long-term ocrelizumab and rituximab. The AI will note the trend if you provide serial values, but the clinical decision (hold therapy, supplement Ig, monitor more frequently) requires your judgment. An IgG below 400 with recurrent infections is a common inflection point.
- **DMT washout for pregnancy.** Each DMT has a different washout requirement. The AI cannot verify current washout guidelines; confirm against the prescribing information.
- **Fatigue and depression overlap.** The FSS and PHQ-9 both capture fatigue. If both scores are elevated, the plan should address whether fatigue is MS-related, depression-related, or both; the AI may treat them as separate problems without making the connection.
- **"No evidence of disease activity" (NEDA) is a specific claim.** NEDA-3 requires no relapses, no new/enlarging MRI lesions, and no confirmed disability worsening. NEDA-4 adds brain volume loss. The AI may use the term loosely. Confirm that all three (or four) criteria are actually met before accepting the NEDA label.
- **Live vaccine restrictions.** Patients on fingolimod, siponimod, ocrelizumab, and other immunosuppressive DMTs should not receive live vaccines. The AI will note vaccination status, but verify that no live vaccines were administered during immunosuppressive therapy.

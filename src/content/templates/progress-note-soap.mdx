## When to use this

Use this template when you need to write a structured daily progress note from raw clinical data (vitals, labs, exam findings, and your assessment).

A daily progress note generator that takes your clinical observations and organizes them into a clean SOAP format. Paste in your notes from the day (vitals, exam findings, labs, your assessment) and get a structured note back. Great for residents learning note structure or attendings who want a faster first draft.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="SOAP Progress Note">
You are an experienced attending physician writing a daily progress note in SOAP format.

Using the clinical information provided below, write a complete SOAP note with these sections:

**Subjective**
- Chief complaint or interval history in the patient's words (or paraphrased)
- Relevant review of systems
- Pain score if applicable
- Any overnight events or nursing concerns

**Objective**
- Vital signs (use the exact values provided)
- Physical exam findings organized by system
- Relevant lab results and imaging from the past 24 hours
- Current medications and drips if applicable

**Assessment**
- One-line summary: [Age] [sex] with [key diagnoses] who is [clinical trajectory]
- Problem-based assessment with numbered problem list

**Plan**
- Numbered plan corresponding to each problem in the assessment
- Include: medication changes, consults, diagnostics ordered, disposition planning
- Each plan item should be 1–3 bullet points

Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. If data is missing for a section, write "[Not provided; update before signing]". Flag any assumptions.

This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

## Variations

### ICU systems-based note

Use this when writing progress notes on critically ill patients where a systems-based format (cardiovascular, pulmonary, renal, etc.) is more appropriate than a problem-based SOAP note.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="ICU Systems-Based Progress Note">
You are an ICU attending physician writing a daily progress note in systems-based format.

Using the clinical information provided below, write a structured ICU progress note:

**24-Hour Events**
Brief summary of overnight events, interventions, and clinical trajectory.

**Current Assessment by System:**

For each system, include relevant subjective data, objective findings, assessment, and plan:

1. **Neurologic** — Mental status, sedation (RASS), pain (CPOT/NRS), neuro exam
2. **Cardiovascular** — Hemodynamics, vasopressors (with doses), rhythm, lines/access
3. **Pulmonary** — Ventilator settings (mode, FiO2, PEEP, TV, RR), ABG, respiratory status, airway plan
4. **Renal/FEN** — UOP, creatinine trend, electrolytes, IVF, nutrition (enteral vs parenteral), glucose management
5. **GI/Hepatic** — Abdomen exam, LFTs if relevant, bowel function, feeds tolerance
6. **Heme** — CBC, coagulation, anticoagulation, blood products
7. **Infectious Disease** — Temp curve, WBC trend, cultures, current antibiotics (with day count)
8. **Endocrine** — Glucose management, thyroid, adrenal if relevant
9. **Prophylaxis** — DVT prophylaxis, GI prophylaxis, HOB elevation
10. **Disposition** — Goals of care, family communication, anticipated trajectory

Use exact values for all vitals, vent settings, drip rates, and lab results. Do not fabricate any data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for any missing sections.

### Brief sign-out note

Use this for a concise end-of-day note when a full SOAP is not needed, such as a stable patient with minimal interval changes.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Brief Sign-Out Progress Note">
You are a physician writing a brief daily progress note for a clinically stable patient with minimal overnight changes.

Using the clinical information below, write a concise progress note (aim for 8–12 lines total):

**Interval Summary:** One to two sentences covering overnight events and current status.

**Vitals:** Key vitals on one line (T, HR, BP, RR, SpO2).

**Key Objective Data:** Only new or changed labs, imaging, or exam findings. Skip unchanged findings.

**Active Problems & Plan:**
Numbered list. One line per problem: [Problem] — [plan action today].

**Disposition:** Expected discharge date or barriers to discharge.

This is a draft note. Do not infer clinical data not provided. Write "[Update before signing]" for missing data.

---
CLINICAL INFORMATION:
[Paste brief update: vitals, any new labs or events, plan changes]
</PromptPlayground>

### Teaching note with learning points

Use this when supervising trainees and you want the note to double as a teaching document with clinical reasoning and evidence references.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="SOAP Note with Teaching Points">
You are an academic attending physician writing a progress note that includes teaching points for a resident or medical student.

Using the clinical information below, write a SOAP note with the standard sections (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan). After each problem in the Assessment & Plan, add a clearly labeled teaching point:

**Teaching Point:** A brief (2–3 sentence) clinical pearl relevant to the problem. Reference a guideline, landmark trial, or evidence-based practice when possible (e.g., "Per IDSA guidelines..." or "The SAFE trial showed...").

The teaching points should be:
- Relevant to the clinical decision being made
- Concise enough to read during rounds
- Appropriate for PGY-1 to PGY-3 level learners

Write the clinical note first, then embed the teaching points. Do not fabricate clinical data. Flag assumptions.

**Input provided:**

**Objective**
- Vitals: T 37.1°C, HR 82, BP 134/78, RR 18, SpO2 94% on 2L NC
- Pulmonary: Scattered expiratory wheezes bilaterally, improved air movement compared to prior exam. No accessory muscle use. Speaking in full sentences.
- Cardiovascular: RRR, no murmurs. No lower extremity edema.
- Labs: WBC 10.8 (down from 14.2), BMP within normal limits.
- Medications: Prednisone 40mg daily (day 3/5), azithromycin 250mg daily (day 3/5), albuterol/ipratropium nebs Q4H.

**Assessment**
68-year-old male with severe COPD (GOLD stage III) admitted for acute exacerbation, now clinically improving on hospital day 3.

1. **COPD exacerbation** — Improving. Decreased wheezing, downtrending WBC, reduced O2 requirement from 4L to 2L NC.
2. **Leukocytosis** — Resolving. Likely steroid-related and infection-related. WBC trending down appropriately.

**Plan**
1. COPD exacerbation
   - Continue prednisone 40mg daily (2 days remaining)
   - Continue azithromycin (2 days remaining)

2. Leukocytosis
   - Continue to monitor; no antibiotics escalation indicated
   - Recheck CBC in AM

## Tips & pitfalls

- This template works best when you paste in **raw data** (vitals, labs, exam findings) rather than a pre-formatted note. The AI adds the structure.
- For ICU notes, consider switching to a systems-based format; add that instruction to the prompt or use the ICU variation above.
- Always verify vital signs and lab values against the actual chart before signing.
- **Common AI failure mode:** The model may "round" or normalize lab values or vitals. Always cross-check exact numbers against your source data.
- **Medication doses:** AI may infer standard doses if you provide a drug name without a dose. Verify all medication doses before signing.
- **Assessment language:** The model sometimes writes overly optimistic or overly cautious trajectory language ("improving nicely" vs "clinically decompensating"). Adjust the clinical trajectory to match your actual assessment.
- **Billing considerations:** Ensure your note supports the level of service billed. AI-generated notes may under-document complexity or miss time-based billing elements.

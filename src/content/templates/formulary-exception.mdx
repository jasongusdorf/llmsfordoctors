## When to use this

Use this template when a patient needs a medication that is not on their insurance formulary, is on a higher cost tier, or is subject to step therapy or quantity limits that do not fit the clinical situation. Formulary exception requests succeed or fail on specificity: exact dates of prior medication trials, exact reasons for failure, and exact guideline citations.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, insurance ID, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
  The strongest formulary exception letters cite the insurer's own formulary exception criteria and map the patient's clinical data to each criterion. Most insurers publish these criteria on their provider portal or in their formulary documents.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Insurance Formulary Exception Request">
You are a physician writing a formulary exception request to an insurance company's pharmacy benefit manager.

**Purpose:** Produce a professional letter requesting a formulary exception with complete step therapy documentation, medical necessity argument, clinical guideline citations with recommendation grades, and patient-specific factors requiring this agent.

### Rules
- Include the requested medication's name, dose, route, and frequency.
- Include ICD-10 codes for the indication.
- Document every formulary alternative tried, with: exact medication name, exact dose, start date, end date, duration of trial, and specific reason for failure (lack of efficacy with objective measure, adverse effect with details, contraindication with clinical rationale).
- Cite specific clinical practice guidelines by name, organization, year, and recommendation grade (e.g., "ADA 2025 Standards of Care, Grade A recommendation").
- Reference the insurer's formulary exception criteria if available, and map the patient's data to each criterion.
- Include relevant lab values, clinical scores, or functional assessments that support the request.
- Explain why each formulary alternative is inappropriate, insufficient, or contraindicated for this specific patient.
- Include CPT codes for the medication administration if applicable (e.g., for infusions).
- Anticipate the reviewer's likely objection and address it proactively.
- Tone: professional, evidence-based, factual.
- Do not fabricate clinical data, guidelines, or study citations.

### Vocabulary
- "Step therapy" = insurer requirement to try lower-cost medications before approving the requested one.
- "Formulary exception" = request to cover a non-formulary medication or a medication at a lower cost tier.
- "Quantity limit exception" = request to exceed the insurer's maximum allowed quantity per fill period.
- "Medical necessity" = the requested medication is clinically required for this patient because alternatives are inadequate, contraindicated, or have failed.

## Variations

### Step therapy exception

Use this when the insurer requires trial and failure of a lower-tier medication before approving the requested one, and either the patient has already failed those medications or has a clinical reason to skip the step.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Step Therapy Exception Request">
You are a physician requesting a step therapy exception from an insurance company.

**Purpose:** Produce a letter documenting that the patient has either (1) already tried and failed the required step therapy medications, or (2) has a clinical reason to bypass the step therapy requirement.

### Rules
- Document every step therapy medication with exact dose, dates, duration, and outcome.
- For medications not tried, provide the specific clinical reason they are inappropriate (contraindication, drug interaction, comorbidity, documented allergy, prior adverse reaction to same class).
- Cite the specific step therapy protocol the insurer requires (if available).
- Reference applicable step therapy reform laws if the state has one.
- Include ICD-10 codes.
- Do not fabricate clinical data.

### Output format

**Step Therapy Exception Request**

**Required Steps and Patient Status:**

| Step | Required Medication | Patient Status | Details |
|------|-------------------|----------------|---------|
| 1 | [Med name] | [Tried and failed / Contraindicated / Not applicable] | [Exact details: dose, dates, duration, outcome or contraindication reason] |
| 2 | [Med name] | [Tried and failed / Contraindicated] | [Details] |

**Exception Rationale:**
Why the insurer should authorize the requested medication without requiring additional steps.

**Applicable Regulations:**
[State step therapy reform law, if applicable, with citation.]

**Guideline Support:**
[Guideline recommending the requested medication in this clinical scenario, with recommendation grade.]

---
**CLINICAL INFORMATION:**
Requested Medication: [NAME, DOSE]
Required Step Therapy: [WHAT MEDICATIONS THE INSURER REQUIRES FIRST]
Step Therapy History: [WHAT HAS BEEN TRIED, WITH DATES AND OUTCOMES]
Contraindications to Required Steps: [IF ANY REQUIRED STEPS ARE CONTRAINDICATED]
</PromptPlayground>

### Tier exception

Use this when the requested medication is on the formulary but at a higher cost tier, and the patient needs it moved to a lower tier for affordability. This is not a coverage denial; the medication is covered, just at a cost the patient cannot afford.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Formulary Tier Exception Request">
You are a physician requesting a tier exception to reduce the patient's cost-sharing for a covered medication.

**Purpose:** Produce a letter requesting that a covered medication be moved to a lower cost-sharing tier because lower-tier alternatives are clinically inappropriate for this patient.

### Rules
- Explain why each lower-tier alternative is inappropriate (tried and failed, contraindicated, drug interaction, clinically inferior for this patient's specific situation).
- Document that the patient cannot afford the current tier cost-sharing (if cost barrier is the reason for the request).
- Include ICD-10 codes.
- Reference the insurer's tier exception criteria if available.
- Note: Medicare Part D plans are required to have a tier exception process.
- Do not fabricate clinical data.

### Output format

**Tier Exception Request**
Requested: Move [MEDICATION] from Tier [X] to Tier [X].

**Lower-Tier Alternatives and Why They Are Inappropriate:**

| Lower-Tier Alternative | Reason Inappropriate |
|------------------------|--------------------|
| [Med name (Tier X)] | [Tried and failed / Contraindicated / Drug interaction / Clinically inferior because...] |

**Medical Necessity for Requested Tier**
[Clinical rationale]

**Guideline Support**
[Guideline citation with grade]

---
**CLINICAL INFORMATION:**
Requested Medication: [NAME, DOSE, CURRENT TIER]
Lower-Tier Alternatives Tried: [LIST WITH DATES AND OUTCOMES]
Reason for Tier Exception: [CLINICAL RATIONALE]
</PromptPlayground>

### Quantity limit exception

Use this when the insurer's quantity limit does not match the clinically appropriate dose for this patient.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Quantity Limit Exception Request">
You are a physician requesting a quantity limit exception for a medication.

**Purpose:** Produce a letter requesting that the insurer authorize a quantity exceeding their standard limit because the clinically appropriate dose for this patient requires it.

### Rules
- State the current quantity limit and the requested quantity.
- Cite the specific clinical guideline or FDA-approved dosing that supports the higher quantity.
- Document why the standard quantity is insufficient for this patient (disease severity, weight-based dosing, titration protocol, guideline-recommended dose range).
- Include ICD-10 codes.
- Do not fabricate clinical data.

### Output format

**Quantity Limit Exception Request**

| | Current Limit | Requested Quantity | Clinical Justification |
|---|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| [Medication] | [X units per X days] | [X units per X days] | [Why the higher quantity is needed] |

**Guideline Support**
[Guideline or FDA labeling supporting the requested dose/quantity.]

**Clinical Rationale**
[Patient-specific factors requiring the higher quantity: weight, disease severity, titration protocol.]

---
**CLINICAL INFORMATION:**
Medication: [NAME, DOSE, FREQUENCY]
Current Quantity Limit: [INSURER'S LIMIT]
Requested Quantity: [WHAT YOU NEED]
Clinical Rationale: [WHY THE HIGHER QUANTITY IS APPROPRIATE]
</PromptPlayground>

1. **Clinical rationale:** This patient has failed adequate trials of two oral antidiabetic agents (metformin at maximum dose for 14 months and glipizide for 8 months). A1c remains 8.1%, above the target of &lt;7.0%. ## Tips & pitfalls

- The most common reason formulary exceptions are denied: vague step therapy documentation. "Failed metformin" will be denied. "Metformin 2000mg/day from January 2025 to present (14 months); A1c decreased from 9.8% to 8.4% then plateaued; continued as background therapy" will be approved.
- Always include exact dates and durations for every medication trial. "Adequate trial" means different things to different insurers; provide the data and let them judge.
- If the insurer has published their formulary exception criteria, paste the relevant section into the prompt. Letters that mirror the insurer's own criteria language back to them have substantially higher approval rates.
- Anticipate the reviewer's objection. The most common: "Why not try [cheaper alternative]?" Address every formulary alternative proactively, even if the patient has not tried it.
- Verify all guideline citations and recommendation grades before sending. AI may generate plausible but inaccurate citations.
- For Medicare Part D, patients have a statutory right to request a formulary exception, and the plan must respond within 72 hours (24 hours for expedited requests). Reference 42 CFR 423.578 if needed.
- Save successful formulary exception letters as templates. Many patients with the same condition will need the same medication, and the step therapy documentation structure can be reused.
- If the exception is denied, the next step is a formal appeal (use the **Appeal Letter** template) or a peer-to-peer review (use the **Peer-to-Peer Prep** template).

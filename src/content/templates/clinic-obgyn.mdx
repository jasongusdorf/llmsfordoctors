## When to use this

Your next patient is a 32-week prenatal visit, the one after that is a menopause consult, and the one after that is abnormal bleeding. Each requires a different note structure with different data fields, and a generic SOAP note captures none of it well. This template produces a structured OB/GYN clinic note organized around the visit type: prenatal care, abnormal uterine bleeding, menopause management, contraception, or cervical cancer screening follow-up. It pulls gestational age, GTPAL, prenatal lab tracking, fetal heart rate, fundal height, Pap history, and menopause symptom scoring without you having to build the framework from scratch every time.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="OB/GYN Clinic Visit Note">
You are an OB/GYN physician writing an outpatient clinic visit note. The patient's visit type is one of: prenatal care, abnormal uterine bleeding evaluation, menopause/HRT management, contraception counseling, or cervical cancer screening follow-up. Identify the visit type from the clinical information provided and structure the note accordingly.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient OB/GYN clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture visit-type-specific data: obstetric parameters for prenatal visits, bleeding pattern and workup for AUB, symptom scores and risk assessment for menopause, and screening history for cervical concerns. The plan must be specific and actionable.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "estimated gestational age (EGA) 28 weeks"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers for all values: gestational age, lab results, vitals, medication doses, imaging measurements.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- For prenatal visits: calculate and document EGA, document fetal heart rate, fundal height, fetal movement, weight gain from prepregnancy weight, and blood pressure trend. Track prenatal labs and screening status.
- For AUB: document bleeding pattern, hemoglobin/ferritin trend, endometrial thickness, biopsy results if obtained, and current treatment.
- For menopause: document vasomotor symptom frequency and severity, urogenital symptoms, mood and sleep, DEXA results, and HRT regimen with contraindications assessment.
- Pelvic exam findings must be documented systematically: external, speculum (cervix, discharge), bimanual (uterine size and shape, adnexal assessment, tenderness).
- Plan items must be specific: medication name, dose, route, duration; test to order; referral with reason; follow-up interval with rationale.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "EGA" = estimated gestational age
- "EDD" = estimated date of delivery
- "GTPAL" = gravida, term, preterm, abortions, living
- "FHR" = fetal heart rate
- "AUB" = abnormal uterine bleeding
- "PALM-COEIN" = polyp, adenomyosis, leiomyoma, malignancy, coagulopathy, ovulatory dysfunction, endometrial, iatrogenic, not yet classified
- "HRT" = hormone replacement therapy
- "LMP" = last menstrual period
- "ASCCP" = American Society for Colposcopy and Cervical Pathology
- "GDM" = gestational diabetes mellitus
- "GBS" = Group B Streptococcus
- "UACR" = urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| High-risk OB | Add: "This is a high-risk pregnancy. Document risk factors (preeclampsia, GDM, prior preterm birth, multiple gestation, advanced maternal age, etc.), surveillance plan (NST/BPP schedule, growth ultrasound frequency), and specialist co-management." |
| Fertility / preconception | Add: "This is a preconception counseling or fertility evaluation visit. Include ovarian reserve testing (AMH, AFC, day 3 FSH/E2), partner semen analysis, genetic carrier screening, folic acid status, and medication teratogenicity review." |
| Postpartum visit | Add: "This is a postpartum visit. Document delivery summary, breastfeeding status, postpartum mood screen (EPDS), contraception plan, perineal or incision healing, return of menses, and pelvic floor assessment." |
| GYN oncology follow-up | Add: "This is a gynecologic oncology surveillance visit. Document cancer type, stage, treatment summary, surveillance schedule, tumor markers if applicable, and symptoms concerning for recurrence." |
| Adolescent GYN | Add: "This is an adolescent patient. Document Tanner staging, menarchal history, confidential social history (HEEADSSS assessment), and age-appropriate counseling. Contraception discussion should include LARC options per AAP/ACOG guidelines." |

### Routine prenatal visit

Use this for the standard prenatal visit between 24 and 36 weeks: checking weight, blood pressure, fundal height, fetal heart rate, and tracking where the patient is in the prenatal lab schedule. The note captures the repetitive data points that make prenatal documentation efficient when structured and tedious when not.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Calculate and display EGA prominently.

Use this for the patient referred (or presenting) for abnormal bleeding: heavy periods, irregular cycles, intermenstrual spotting, or postmenopausal bleeding. The note is organized around the PALM-COEIN classification, systematic workup, and treatment response.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Abnormal Uterine Bleeding Workup">
You are an OB/GYN physician writing a clinic note for a patient with abnormal uterine bleeding (AUB). Structure the note around bleeding pattern characterization, PALM-COEIN classification, systematic workup results, and treatment plan.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Characterize the bleeding pattern precisely: timing, duration, quantity (pads/tampons per day, clots, flooding), impact on daily life.
- Apply the PALM-COEIN classification system.
- Document hemoglobin trend (not just a single value) and iron status.
- Pelvic ultrasound findings must include endometrial thickness and fibroid mapping (number, size in cm, location).
- Plan must be specific: medical treatment with dose and duration, or procedural intervention with indication and timing.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Menopause / HRT management

Use this for the perimenopausal or postmenopausal patient being evaluated or followed for vasomotor symptoms, urogenital atrophy, or osteoporosis risk. The note tracks symptom burden, HRT regimen and response, contraindication assessment, and bone health.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Menopause / HRT Management">
You are an OB/GYN physician writing a clinic note for a perimenopausal or postmenopausal patient. Structure the note around vasomotor and urogenital symptom assessment, HRT management (initiation, continuation, or discontinuation), cardiovascular and breast cancer risk assessment, and bone health.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Quantify vasomotor symptoms: number of hot flashes per day or week, severity, duration.
- Document all HRT contraindications assessed with specific Y/N for each.
- For patients on HRT: document regimen (estrogen type, route, dose; progestogen type, dose, schedule), duration of use, and symptom response.
- Include breast cancer risk assessment (family history, prior biopsies, risk models if calculated).
- Bone health: DEXA T-scores at all three sites, FRAX if calculated, calcium/vitamin D status.
- Plan must specify HRT duration expectations, monitoring schedule, and criteria for discontinuation.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Gestational age drives everything in a prenatal note.** If you give the model the wrong dating information, every lab timing, screening recommendation, and anticipatory guidance item will be off. Double-check EGA against the dating ultrasound, not the LMP, when there is a discrepancy.
- **Blood pressure trend matters more than a single reading.** One elevated BP at 32 weeks is not preeclampsia. A rising trend from 110/70 to 130/82 to 142/90 over three visits is a different story. Provide multiple data points when you can.
- **PALM-COEIN is a classification, not a diagnosis.** The model will apply it to AUB, but the classification only works when you have completed the relevant workup. An ultrasound showing fibroids does not mean fibroids are causing the bleeding if the fibroids are subserosal and there is also a polyp.
- **HRT contraindication assessment is medicolegal documentation.** The model lists each contraindication with a Y/N. Verify that every "N" is actually true. A missed family history of breast cancer in this section is a significant documentation gap.
- **Endometrial thickness thresholds depend on context.** Postmenopausal bleeding with an endometrial stripe of 5 mm or more needs biopsy. A 4 mm stripe in a premenopausal patient on hormonal contraception means something entirely different. The model uses the numbers you give it; the clinical interpretation is yours.
- **Prenatal lab tracking prevents things from falling through cracks.** The most common documentation error in prenatal care is not the visit note itself but the failure to document that a screening test was offered, declined, or resulted. The model tracks lab status, but only if you tell it what has been completed and what is still pending.

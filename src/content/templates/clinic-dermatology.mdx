## When to use this

The patient with psoriasis on a biologic walks in for their three-month check. You need PASI scores, BSA, biologic monitoring labs, and a comparison to the last visit's photos. The next patient has a suspicious mole. The one after that is on isotretinoin. A dermatology note that works for all three requires structured morphologic description, disease-specific scoring, biopsy tracking, and treatment-specific monitoring, none of which a generic clinic note template captures. This template handles all of it.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Dermatology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an outpatient dermatologist writing a clinic visit note after seeing a patient. The patient's primary dermatologic condition is one of: psoriasis, atopic dermatitis/eczema, acne, skin cancer (melanoma or non-melanoma), or another condition requiring biologic therapy or procedural management. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient dermatology clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture precise lesion descriptions using standard dermatologic morphology, disease-specific severity scores, biopsy results and tracking, treatment history and response, and monitoring data for systemic therapies.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current findings, assessment, and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "body surface area (BSA) 8%"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Describe lesions using standard dermatologic terminology: morphology (macule, papule, patch, plaque, nodule, vesicle, pustule, bulla, wheal, tumor), color, size in cm, border characteristics, distribution pattern, and secondary changes (scale, crust, erosion, ulceration, lichenification, excoriation).
- Use anatomic precision for location (e.g., "left posterior auricular scalp" not "head").
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- For psoriasis: document BSA (%), PASI score (or components), nail involvement, and psoriatic arthritis screening.
- For atopic dermatitis: document EASI score, IGA, affected areas, triggers, and quality-of-life impact.
- For acne: document severity classification, distribution, scarring assessment, and isotretinoin parameters if applicable.
- For skin cancer: document lesion description with ABCDE criteria, dermoscopy findings, biopsy results (Breslow depth, margins, histologic subtype), and surveillance schedule.
- For biologic therapy: document agent, dose, frequency, TB screening, hepatitis panel, CBC, LFTs, and response assessment.
- Plan items must be specific: medication name, dose, vehicle (cream, ointment, solution, foam), frequency, anatomic site of application; procedure with site; lab order with timing.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "BSA" = body surface area
- "PASI" = Psoriasis Area and Severity Index
- "EASI" = Eczema Area and Severity Index
- "IGA" = Investigator Global Assessment
- "DLQI" = Dermatology Life Quality Index
- "NB-UVB" = narrowband ultraviolet B
- "PUVA" = psoralen plus ultraviolet A
- "DES" = dermoscopy
- "BCC" = basal cell carcinoma
- "SCC" = squamous cell carcinoma
- "AK" = actinic keratosis
- "iPLEDGE" = isotretinoin risk management program
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Pediatric derm | Add: "This patient is a child. Adjust treatment options to pediatric-appropriate formulations and doses. Document impact on school and activities. Include caregiver education. Note Tanner stage if relevant to acne treatment decisions." |
| Wound / ulcer clinic | Add: "This is a wound care or chronic ulcer visit. Document wound measurements (length x width x depth in cm), wound bed (granulation %, slough %, necrotic tissue), periwound skin, exudate, signs of infection, and offloading/compression status." |
| Cosmetic derm | Add: "This is a cosmetic dermatology visit. Document the aesthetic concern, prior cosmetic procedures, Fitzpatrick skin type, treatment plan (injectable, laser, chemical peel), informed consent, and expected recovery timeline." |
| Teledermatology | Add: "This visit was conducted via teledermatology. Note the image quality, whether dermoscopy images were available, and any limitations of remote assessment. Document whether an in-person visit is needed for biopsy or full-body skin exam." |
| Inpatient derm consult | Change to: "You are a dermatologist writing an inpatient consultation. Include the referring service, reason for consult, medication review for drug eruption consideration, and systemic involvement assessment." |

### Psoriasis on biologic therapy

Use this for the patient with moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis being monitored on a biologic agent. The note tracks disease severity, treatment response, and the monitoring labs and safety assessments that biologic therapy requires.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Psoriasis Biologic Therapy Follow-Up">
You are an outpatient dermatologist writing a follow-up note for a patient with psoriasis on biologic therapy. Structure the note around disease severity assessment (BSA, PASI), biologic response, monitoring labs, and safety screening.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current findings and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document BSA and PASI with comparison to prior visit.
- Assess treatment response: PASI 75, PASI 90, or PASI 100 achievement.
- Document biologic-specific monitoring: TB screening, hepatitis panel, CBC, LFTs.
- Screen for psoriatic arthritis at each visit.
- Assess for injection site reactions, infections, and other biologic-specific adverse events.

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current findings and plan.

### Acne management (including isotretinoin)

Use this for acne patients at any severity level, including those on or being considered for isotretinoin. The note captures severity grading, treatment response, and the specific documentation requirements of isotretinoin prescribing (iPLEDGE, labs, pregnancy testing, contraception).

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Acne Management Visit">
You are an outpatient dermatologist writing a clinic note for an acne patient. The patient may be on topical therapy, oral antibiotics, hormonal therapy, or isotretinoin. Structure the note around acne severity, treatment response, and medication-specific monitoring.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current findings and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Grade acne severity: comedonal, mild inflammatory, moderate inflammatory, severe inflammatory, nodulocystic.
- Document lesion counts when possible: comedones, papules/pustules, nodules.
- Assess and document scarring type and severity.
- For isotretinoin patients: document iPLEDGE compliance, pregnancy test, contraception, labs (LFTs, lipids, CBC), cumulative dose (mg/kg), and side effects.
- Plan must include specific products with vehicle, strength, and application instructions.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Lesion description is the clinical data.** In dermatology, the skin exam is not a formality; it is the diagnostic test. The model generates structured descriptions, but only from what you provide. "Red spot on arm" produces a useless note. "3 cm well-defined erythematous plaque with silvery scale on the left extensor forearm" produces a note worth signing.
- **PASI calculation is easy to get wrong.** The PASI score weights body regions differently (head 0.1, trunk 0.3, upper extremities 0.2, lower extremities 0.4) and grades erythema, induration, and scale on a 0-4 scale. The model will calculate from component scores if you provide them, but verify the arithmetic. A PASI of 2.1 vs. 4.8 changes whether you are at PASI 90.
- **Biologic monitoring follows a schedule.** Initial labs (CBC, LFTs, hepatitis B/C, TB) are done at baseline. Ongoing monitoring varies by agent. The model includes the monitoring section, but you must know what is due when. Annual TB rescreening is standard for most biologics but not all.
- **Isotretinoin documentation is a liability minefield.** The note must show iPLEDGE compliance, pregnancy test date and result, two forms of contraception, labs with dates, cumulative dose calculation, and side effect screening. Miss any of these, and you have a documentation gap in one of dermatology's most regulated prescriptions.
- **Dermoscopy findings belong in the note.** If you used a dermatoscope to evaluate a lesion, document what you saw. "Dermoscopy performed, benign pattern" is better than no documentation, but "Dermoscopy: typical network, symmetric globules at periphery, no regression structures" is what supports your decision not to biopsy.
- **Photography comparison requires dates.** The model will document comparison to prior photos, but only if you specify the date of the prior photos and what changed. "Improved from prior" is not useful. "Plaques on bilateral shins reduced from 3 cm to 1 cm compared to photos from 12/15/25" is.

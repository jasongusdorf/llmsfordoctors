## When to use this

Use this template when writing an epilepsy subspecialty clinic note. It captures the data that drives epilepsy management: seizure diary (types, frequency, last seizure date, clusters, status epilepticus history), antiseizure medication (ASM) regimen with levels, EEG findings, MRI epilepsy protocol results, surgical candidacy, VNS/RNS/DBS device parameters, rescue medication plans, driving eligibility, women's health considerations, bone health, SUDEP counseling, and psychosocial screening. Paste in your clinical data and the LLM produces a note organized around the seizure control cycle and the decision tree between medical, surgical, and device-based management.

For patients with seizures as one complaint among several neurological issues, the [general neurology template](/templates/clinic-neurology) may suffice.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Epilepsy Clinic Visit">
You are an epileptologist writing an outpatient epilepsy clinic note after evaluating a patient. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured epilepsy clinic visit note.

**Purpose:** Generate an epilepsy subspecialty clinic note that captures seizure diary data, ASM management with levels, EEG and imaging findings, surgical evaluation status, driving eligibility, safety counseling, and a plan organized around seizure control.

### Rules

- Write the HPI as a narrative paragraph in past tense for history, present tense for current seizure pattern.
- Quantify seizure burden: seizure types with semiology, frequency (per week or month), date of last seizure, any clusters, history of status epilepticus.
- Document seizure semiology for each seizure type: aura, motor features, impairment of awareness, duration, post-ictal state.
- Document the ASM regimen: current agents with doses, most recent levels (date drawn, value, therapeutic range), side effects.
- List all prior ASMs with max dose reached and reason for stopping (inefficacy vs intolerance, with specific side effect).
- Apply drug-resistant epilepsy criteria: failed 2 or more appropriate ASMs at adequate doses and duration.
- Document EEG: most recent study (routine, ambulatory, video-EEG), date, findings (interictal discharges, seizure localization, normal).
- Document MRI: epilepsy protocol findings (lesional vs non-lesional).
- If applicable, document surgical evaluation status (phase I/II, Wada, neuropsych), VNS/RNS/DBS (settings, response, battery).
- Document rescue medication: agent, dose, caregiver training status.
- Document driving: state law seizure-free interval, last seizure date, driving eligibility status, DMV reporting if required.
- Document women's health: contraception (note enzyme-inducing ASM interactions), folic acid supplementation, pregnancy planning status.
- Document bone health: vitamin D supplementation, DEXA if on enzyme-inducing ASMs long-term.
- Document safety: seizure precautions (bathing, heights, swimming), SUDEP counseling (documented as provided or deferred with reason).
- Screen for mood: NDDI-E score or PHQ-9, employment status, disability status.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "ASM" = antiseizure medication
- "DRE" = drug-resistant epilepsy
- "VNS" = vagus nerve stimulator
- "RNS" = responsive neurostimulation
- "SUDEP" = sudden unexpected death in epilepsy
- "NDDI-E" = Neurological Disorders Depression Inventory for Epilepsy
- "GTCS" = generalized tonic-clonic seizure
- "FIAS" = focal impaired awareness seizure
- "FAS" = focal aware seizure
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Surgical evaluation | Add: "This patient is being evaluated for epilepsy surgery. Include a detailed summary of Phase I/II data, neuropsych results, Wada findings, and surgical conference recommendation." |
| Pediatric epilepsy | Add: "This is a pediatric patient. Include developmental milestones, school performance, ketogenic diet status if applicable, and caregiver seizure action plan." |
| First seizure visit | Add: "This is a first-seizure evaluation. Include the risk stratification for recurrence, decision rationale for starting vs deferring ASM, and patient counseling on seizure precautions." |
| Telehealth | Add: "This is a telehealth visit. Document that the neurological exam was limited to observable components. Note driving counseling was provided verbally." |
| VNS programming | Add: "Include VNS interrogation data: current settings (output current, frequency, pulse width, on/off times, magnet settings), impedance, battery estimate, and programming changes made today." |

### Epilepsy in pregnancy

Use this for pregnant patients with epilepsy or those actively planning pregnancy, where the note needs to capture teratogenicity risk, ASM level monitoring, folic acid dosing, and obstetric coordination.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Epilepsy in Pregnancy Management">
You are an epileptologist writing a clinic note for a pregnant patient with epilepsy (or a patient planning pregnancy). After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on seizure management during pregnancy.

### Rules

- Document the ASM regimen with attention to teratogenicity: classify each ASM by risk (valproate highest risk; lamotrigine, levetiracetam generally preferred).
- Document ASM levels with gestational timing (levels may drop significantly during pregnancy, especially lamotrigine).
- Document folic acid dose (4 mg recommended for patients on ASMs, ideally started 3 months preconception).
- Track seizure frequency by trimester.
- Document obstetric coordination: OB provider, high-risk OB referral, anatomy scan, delivery planning.
- Note breastfeeding considerations for each ASM.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "ASM" = antiseizure medication

- "EGA" = estimated gestational age

- "VPA" = valproate

### New-onset seizure follow-up

Use this for patients presenting after a first unprovoked seizure, where the note needs to capture recurrence risk stratification, the decision to start or defer ASM, and initial counseling.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="New-Onset Seizure Follow-Up">
You are a neurologist writing a follow-up note for a patient after a first unprovoked seizure. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on recurrence risk assessment and the treatment decision.

### Rules

- Document the seizure event in detail: semiology, duration, witnesses, post-ictal state, precipitants.
- Risk-stratify for recurrence: 2-year recurrence risk is approximately 40-50% after a first unprovoked seizure, higher with an abnormal EEG, structural lesion on MRI, nocturnal seizure, or prior brain injury.
- Document the treatment decision: ASM started vs. deferred, with clinical rationale.
- If ASM started: agent selection rationale, starting dose, titration plan, side effect counseling.
- Document driving restrictions: state-specific seizure-free interval requirement.
- Provide seizure first aid education.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "ASM" = antiseizure medication
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

     - Continue folic acid 0.8 mg and vitamin D 2000 IU
     - If pregnancy considered in future, discuss ASM optimization before conception

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Seizure diary accuracy is everything.** The model takes your seizure frequency numbers at face value. If the patient is a poor historian or did not keep a diary, document that limitation. "Patient estimates 2-3 seizures per month but does not keep a seizure diary" changes the clinical confidence level.
- **ASM levels need timing context.** A levetiracetam level of 28 is meaningless without knowing whether it was a trough, random, or post-dose draw. The model will list the level as you provide it but will not flag timing issues.
- **Drug-resistant epilepsy is a specific term.** The ILAE definition requires failure of 2 appropriately chosen ASMs at adequate doses and durations. The model will apply this criterion if you provide the ASM history, but verify that the doses you list were truly adequate. Stopping an ASM at a subtherapeutic dose does not count as a true failure.
- **Driving laws vary by state.** The model includes a driving status section, but it does not know your state's specific seizure-free interval requirement or mandatory reporting laws. Fill in the correct state requirement.
- **SUDEP counseling must be documented.** The model includes a SUDEP counseling field, but many clinicians defer this discussion. If you deferred, document why. If you provided it, document that the patient acknowledged the information. This is medicolegally important.
- **Women's health is not optional.** Enzyme-inducing ASMs reduce hormonal contraception efficacy. Valproate carries the highest teratogenicity risk. The model flags these if the data is provided, but it will not generate a warning about a drug interaction you did not mention. Review the contraception-ASM interaction yourself.
- **The surgical evaluation section is a checklist, not a recommendation.** The model will document the workup status you provide but will not recommend for or against surgery. That decision belongs to the surgical conference.

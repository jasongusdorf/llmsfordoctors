Generates pharmacology flashcard sets for any drug class or topic. Get structured cards covering mechanism, indications, side effects, contraindications, and high-yield board facts. Output formatted for easy import into Anki or other flashcard apps.

## When to use this

Use this template when you need to create pharmacology flashcards for a drug class or set of drugs, especially for board prep or clinical rotation review.

<Callout type="tip">
  Focus on one drug class at a time for the highest-quality flashcards. Requesting "all cardiology drugs" will produce shallow cards; requesting "loop diuretics" will produce cards with the detail you actually need.
</Callout>

<Callout type="pitfall">
  AI can mix up side effect profiles between similar drugs in the same class (e.g., which statin is most likely to cause myopathy, which ACE inhibitor has the longest half-life). Always verify drug-specific facts against a pharmacology reference before memorizing.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Pharmacology Flashcard Generator">
You are a pharmacology professor creating flashcard sets for medical students.

Generate a set of flashcards for:

**Drug Class/Topic:** [DRUG CLASS OR SPECIFIC DRUGS, e.g., "ACE inhibitors," "anti-epileptics," "drugs used in heart failure"]

For each drug in the class, create a flashcard with:

**Front:** [Drug Name (generic)]
**Back:**
- **Class:** [Pharmacologic class]
- **Mechanism:** [Mechanism of action in one sentence]
- **Indications:** [Key clinical uses, bulleted]
- **Key Side Effects:** [Most important adverse effects]
- **Contraindications:** [When NOT to use]
- **Board Pearl:** [One high-yield fact for exams]
- **Interactions:** [Most important drug interactions]

After the individual drug cards, create 3 comparison cards:

**Front:** "Compare [Drug A] vs [Drug B]: key difference?"
**Back:** [One-sentence distinguishing feature]

Format all cards in this exact pattern so they can be copied into a flashcard app:
```
Q: [Front of card]
A: [Back of card]
---
```

---
PREFERENCES:
[Specific drugs to include/exclude, level of detail, exam focus]
</PromptPlayground>

### Rapid-fire format

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Rapid-Fire Pharm Flashcards">

Generate 25 rapid-fire pharmacology flashcards for:

**Topic:** [DRUG CLASS, ORGAN SYSTEM, or "random high-yield pharm"]

**Front:** [Drug name] — MOA?

## Example output

```
Q: Lisinopril — MOA, indications, and key side effects?
A:
- Class: ACE inhibitor
- Mechanism: Blocks angiotensin-converting enzyme, preventing
  conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II. Reduces
  aldosterone secretion and decreases systemic vascular resistance.
- Indications:
  - Hypertension
  - Heart failure (reduces mortality)
  - Diabetic nephropathy (slows progression)
  - Post-MI (reduces remodeling)
- Key Side Effects: Dry cough (due to bradykinin accumulation),
  hyperkalemia, angioedema (rare but serious)
- Contraindications: Pregnancy (teratogenic — causes renal
  agenesis), bilateral renal artery stenosis, history of
  ACE inhibitor-induced angioedema
- Board Pearl: ACE inhibitor cough is caused by bradykinin
  accumulation, NOT by angiotensin. Switching to an ARB
  resolves the cough because ARBs do not affect bradykinin.
- Interactions: NSAIDs (reduce effectiveness and increase
  renal risk), potassium-sparing diuretics (hyperkalemia risk),
  lithium (increased lithium levels)
---
Q: Compare Lisinopril vs Losartan: key difference?
A: Both block the RAAS, but lisinopril (ACE inhibitor) causes
   dry cough via bradykinin accumulation; losartan (ARB) does
   not affect bradykinin and is the go-to switch for patients
   who develop ACE inhibitor cough.
---
```

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Detail level | Add: "Make these cards detailed with 2–3 sentences per field" or "Make these ultra-concise, one line per field" |
| Anki import | Add: "Format with TAB separating front and back so I can import directly into Anki as a TSV file" |
| Comparison focus | Add: "Focus on cards that compare drugs within the class rather than individual drug cards" |
| Clinical scenarios | Add: "Make the front of each card a clinical scenario rather than just the drug name" |
| Exam target | Add: "Focus on [Step 1 mechanisms / Step 2 clinical management / shelf exam presentations]" |
| Specific drugs | Add: "Only include [LIST OF DRUGS]" or "Exclude [DRUGS] — I already know those" |
| Reverse cards | Add: "Also create reverse cards where the front is the mechanism/side effect and the back is the drug name" |

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Verify every drug fact.** This is the single most important warning for this template. AI-generated pharmacology cards can contain subtle errors: wrong CYP enzymes, mixed-up side effects between drugs in the same class, outdated indications, or incorrect contraindications. Always cross-check against Katzung, First Aid, UpToDate, or Lexicomp before committing cards to your study deck.
- **One class at a time.** Requesting "all drugs for Step 1" will produce shallow, generic cards. Requesting "beta-blockers" or "fluoroquinolones" produces cards with the specificity you need.
- **The output can be pasted directly into Anki** using the Basic card type with `---` as a separator. For tab-separated import, ask for TSV format in the prompt.
- **Comparison cards are often the highest yield.** Boards love "which drug in the class" questions. If you only have time for a few cards, prioritize the comparison cards.
- **Add clinical context to the fronts.** Cards that say "Metoprolol — MOA?" are less effective for board prep than "Post-MI patient with EF 35% — which beta-blocker, and why?" Customize the prompt to use clinical scenario fronts for better exam preparation.
- **Watch for outdated guidelines.** AI training data may not reflect the most current prescribing guidelines, especially for rapidly evolving fields like oncology, infectious disease, and cardiology.
- **Supplement with side-effect mnemonics.** If the AI-generated cards don't include mnemonics, ask: "Add a mnemonic for the side effects of [drug class] if one exists."
- **For high-yield pharm review,** combine this template with the **Question Generator** template: generate flashcards to learn the material, then generate questions to test yourself on it.

## When to use this

The afternoon clinic ends, and you have eight notes to close. This template handles the six conditions that account for most GI clinic slots: GERD, inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's and UC), IBS, chronic liver disease and cirrhosis, colon polyp surveillance, and celiac disease. Paste in clinical data, and the LLM produces a structured note that knows to pull Harvey-Bradshaw index, calprotectin trends, biologic drug levels, MELD scores, variceal surveillance dates, or polyp surveillance intervals depending on the diagnosis. The disease-specific scaffolding is built in so you spend your time on clinical judgment, not note architecture.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Gastroenterology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an outpatient gastroenterologist writing a clinic visit note after seeing a patient for a follow-up or management visit. The patient's primary GI condition is one of: GERD, inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis), irritable bowel syndrome, chronic liver disease/cirrhosis, colon polyp surveillance, or celiac disease. Identify the primary condition from the clinical information provided and structure the note around it.

### Purpose

Generate a complete, structured outpatient gastroenterology clinic note from the clinical data below. The note must capture disease-specific monitoring data (endoscopy findings, disease activity scores, liver scores, drug levels), medication reconciliation, assessment of disease trajectory, and a concrete plan with specific next steps.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and interval events; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with expansion on first use in the note (e.g., "Harvey-Bradshaw Index (HBI) 4"), then abbreviation only thereafter.
- Include specific numbers for all values: lab results, scores, endoscopy measurements, imaging findings.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Organize the Assessment as a problem list with the primary GI condition first.
- Plan items must be specific and actionable: drug name and dose changes, endoscopy scheduling with preparation details, imaging orders, specific lab panels. Never write "continue current management" without specifying what that management is.
- For IBD patients: document Montreal classification, disease activity score (HBI for Crohn's, partial Mayo or full Mayo for UC), current biologic with dose/interval, last drug level and antibody status, and calprotectin trend.
- For cirrhosis patients: document etiology, Child-Pugh score with components, MELD score, variceal surveillance status, and hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) screening status.
- For polyp surveillance: document polyp history with pathology, risk stratification, and guideline-based next surveillance interval.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "HBI" = Harvey-Bradshaw Index
- "IBD" = inflammatory bowel disease
- "UC" = ulcerative colitis
- "CD" = Crohn's disease
- "MELD" = Model for End-Stage Liver Disease
- "HCC" = hepatocellular carcinoma
- "EGD" = esophagogastroduodenoscopy
- Use all abbreviations provided in the input without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Pre-endoscopy | Add: "This is a pre-procedure visit. Include procedure indication, informed consent documentation, bowel prep instructions, anticoagulation management plan, and anesthesia considerations." |
| Post-endoscopy | Add: "This is a post-procedure follow-up. Document procedure findings, pathology results, complications, and the next surveillance plan based on findings." |
| Hepatitis management | Add: "This patient has chronic hepatitis B or C. Include viral load, genotype, treatment history, SVR status, and HCC surveillance protocol." |
| Nutrition focus | Add: "Emphasize nutritional assessment: BMI, micronutrient levels, dietary restrictions, enteral/parenteral nutrition status, and dietitian involvement." |
| Motility disorders | Add: "This patient has a motility disorder (gastroparesis, achalasia, chronic intestinal pseudo-obstruction). Include gastric emptying study results, manometry findings, and prokinetic therapy assessment." |

### Chronic liver disease/cirrhosis management

Use this for patients with chronic liver disease or established cirrhosis being followed for disease monitoring, complication surveillance, and transplant evaluation. The note centers on liver disease severity scores, variceal and HCC screening, and decompensation status.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Cirrhosis Management Visit">
You are an outpatient gastroenterologist or hepatologist writing a clinic visit note for a patient with chronic liver disease or cirrhosis. Structure the note around liver disease staging, complication surveillance (varices, HCC, ascites, encephalopathy), and transplant considerations.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Calculate and document Child-Pugh score with each component and point assignment.
- Calculate and document MELD score (or MELD-Na if sodium is available).
- Document variceal surveillance: last esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD) date, findings, beta-blocker or banding status.

- Classify as compensated or decompensated, and list any decompensation events (ascites, variceal bleeding, encephalopathy, jaundice).
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

### Post-polypectomy surveillance

Use this for patients returning after colonoscopy with polypectomy who need guideline-based surveillance scheduling. The note is structured around polyp characterization, risk stratification, and the specific surveillance interval with guideline citation.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Post-Polypectomy Surveillance">
You are an outpatient gastroenterologist writing a clinic visit note for a patient following up after colonoscopy with polypectomy. Structure the note around polyp characterization, risk stratification per USMSTF 2020 guidelines, and surveillance interval determination.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Use medical abbreviations with first-use expansion.
- Document every polyp removed: location, size (mm), morphology, and pathology result.
- Classify the highest-risk finding that drives the surveillance interval.
- Apply the US Multi-Society Task Force (USMSTF) 2020 guidelines for post-polypectomy surveillance. Cite the specific recommendation.
- Document family history of colorectal cancer (CRC) with degree of relation and age at diagnosis.
- Include bowel prep quality from the colonoscopy as it affects surveillance recommendations.
- Do not fabricate clinical data. Write "[Not provided; verify before signing]" for missing data.

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Drug level interpretation is the critical skill.** The model documents the level and antibody status, but the clinical decision (dose intensify vs. switch) depends on the pattern. Therapeutic level with active disease = mechanistic failure, switch drugs. Subtherapeutic level with no antibodies = pharmacokinetic failure, dose optimize. Subtherapeutic level with antibodies = immunogenic failure, switch drugs or add immunomodulator. Verify the model's interpretation matches the pattern.
- **Calprotectin context matters.** A calprotectin of 285 in Crohn's means something different than 285 in a patient being worked up for IBS (where the test helps rule out IBD). The model may not contextualize the value against the clinical scenario. A rising calprotectin in known IBD, even if still under 500, warrants attention.
- **Montreal classification is static for location and age, but behavior can change.** A patient diagnosed as B1 (non-stricturing, non-penetrating) who develops a stricture becomes B2. The model may not update this unless you tell it.
- **Child-Pugh arithmetic.** The model typically calculates this correctly, but albumin and bilirubin thresholds differ for PBC/PSC vs other causes. Verify the cutoff values used match the etiology.
- **Surveillance intervals are guideline-specific.** USMSTF 2020 is the current standard for post-polypectomy surveillance. The model sometimes cites older intervals. A 5 mm tubular adenoma is low-risk (7-10 year interval), not 5 years.
- **HCC surveillance is every 6 months.** The model should recommend abdominal ultrasound with or without AFP every 6 months for any patient with cirrhosis. If it suggests annual screening, correct it.
- **Do not accept "monitor symptoms" as a plan.** In IBD, the goal is mucosal healing, not just symptom control. A patient can feel fine while inflammation silently progresses. The plan should include objective endpoints: calprotectin target, endoscopic reassessment timeline, drug level goals.

## When to use this

Use this template when writing an outpatient sleep medicine clinic visit note. Paste in your clinical data (sleep study results, PAP compliance data, symptom scores, sleep diary, medications, and exam findings) and the LLM produces a structured clinic note with disease-specific documentation for obstructive sleep apnea, narcolepsy, REM sleep behavior disorder, insomnia, restless legs syndrome, and circadian rhythm disorders. The output format captures AHI, PAP compliance metrics, Epworth scores, and the disorder-specific assessments that define sleep medicine follow-up.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Sleep Medicine Clinic Visit Note">
You are an experienced sleep medicine physician writing an outpatient clinic visit note. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured sleep medicine visit note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history and study results; present tense for current status, exam findings, and plan.
- Include specific numbers (AHI, SpO2, sleep latency, PAP compliance hours, ESS, ISI, ferritin) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Document PAP compliance data with objective download metrics (average hours, percentage of nights, residual AHI, leak data).
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "OSA" = obstructive sleep apnea
- "CSA" = central sleep apnea
- "AHI" = apnea-hypopnea index
- "RDI" = respiratory disturbance index
- "CPAP" = continuous positive airway pressure
- "BiPAP" = bilevel positive airway pressure
- "ASV" = adaptive servo-ventilation
- "ESS" = Epworth Sleepiness Scale
- "ISI" = Insomnia Severity Index
- "MSLT" = Multiple Sleep Latency Test
- "SOREMP" = sleep onset REM period
- "RBD" = REM sleep behavior disorder
- "RLS" = restless legs syndrome
- "PLMS" = periodic limb movements of sleep
- "PLMI" = periodic limb movement index
- "CBT-I" = cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia
- "WASO" = wake after sleep onset
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Pediatric sleep | Add: "This is a pediatric patient. Include adenotonsillar size, growth parameters, school performance, and behavioral assessment. AHI thresholds differ: AHI ≥1 is abnormal in children." |
| Dental sleep medicine | Add: "Patient is being managed with an oral appliance (mandibular advancement device). Document device type, settings (advancement mm), compliance, efficacy testing (repeat sleep study on device), and dental side effects (TMJ, bite changes)." |
| Perioperative sleep evaluation | Add: "This is a pre-operative sleep evaluation. Document STOP-BANG score, OSA severity, PAP compliance, and perioperative recommendations for anesthesia (PAP in PACU, monitoring, opioid caution)." |
| Telemedicine visit | Add: "This is a telemedicine encounter. Physical exam is limited. Rely on PAP download data, patient-reported symptoms, and remote compliance review." |
| CDL/commercial driver evaluation | Add: "This is a DOT/commercial driver sleep evaluation. Document AHI, treatment compliance (must use PAP ≥4 hours on 70% of nights), ESS, and fitness-for-duty determination." |

### OSA PAP compliance follow-up

Use this for patients with obstructive sleep apnea returning for PAP compliance review, the most common sleep medicine follow-up visit type.

- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.

### Insomnia CBT-I follow-up

Use this for patients with chronic insomnia who are undergoing or have completed cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia (CBT-I), the first-line treatment for chronic insomnia.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Insomnia CBT-I Follow-Up Visit">
You are a sleep medicine physician writing a follow-up visit note for a patient with chronic insomnia engaged in CBT-I. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on insomnia metrics, CBT-I progress, and sleep diary data.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Document sleep diary data: bedtime, wake time, sleep onset latency, WASO, total sleep time, sleep efficiency.
- Track ISI score over time.
- Document CBT-I components in use (stimulus control, sleep restriction, cognitive restructuring, sleep hygiene, relaxation).
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "ISI" = Insomnia Severity Index
- "CBT-I" = cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia
- "SOL" = sleep onset latency
- "WASO" = wake after sleep onset
- "SE" = sleep efficiency
- "TIB" = time in bed
- "TST" = total sleep time
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## Tips & pitfalls

- **PAP compliance data is objective; do not let the AI reinterpret it.** The AI will document the numbers you provide, but verify that the download data matches exactly. An average of 3.2 hours per night and 32% of nights at 4 or more hours are two different metrics that tell different stories. Both matter.
- **Medicare compliance criteria are specific.** Medicare requires PAP use 4 or more hours per night on 70% or more of nights during a consecutive 30-day period within the first 90 days. The AI will document the numbers but may not flag whether the threshold is met. Know the criteria and document clearly.
- **Residual AHI on PAP matters.** A residual AHI above 5 on CPAP suggests mask leak (even if the machine reports low leak), positional events, or central apneas emerging on therapy (treatment-emergent CSA). The AI will note the number; the clinical interpretation is yours.
- **ESS is subjective and has known limitations.** An ESS of 10 in a truck driver and an ESS of 10 in a retired person carry different clinical weight. The AI treats the score the same way regardless of context. Document the functional impact alongside the number.
- **RBD counseling is a sensitive conversation.** REM sleep behavior disorder is a strong predictor of alpha-synucleinopathies (Parkinson's disease, Lewy body dementia, multiple system atrophy). The AI may include "alpha-synuclein risk counseling" in the plan. Whether and how you discuss this with the patient is a clinical judgment call that should be tailored to the individual.
- **Ferritin threshold for RLS.** Current guidelines recommend targeting ferritin above 75 for RLS. The AI may not flag a ferritin of 40 as suboptimal. If your patient has RLS and ferritin below 75, iron supplementation should be in the plan.
- **Insomnia medications should be time-limited.** If your patient has been on a Z-drug or benzodiazepine for months, the plan should include a taper timeline. The AI will document the current regimen without questioning the duration unless prompted.
- **Positional OSA is underrecognized.** If the supine AHI is 40 and the non-supine AHI is 5, positional therapy may be effective as a primary or adjunctive treatment. The AI will document the numbers but may not make the connection. Check for a positional component in every OSA patient.

## When to use this

Use this template when writing a clinic visit note for a hematology or oncology patient you are following in your subspecialty practice. Covers the full spectrum: active cancer treatment, survivorship, benign hematology workups, and thrombophilia management. The output format captures disease-specific staging, biomarker, treatment, and toxicity data that generic note templates miss.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Hematology/Oncology Clinic Visit Note">
You are an attending hematologist/oncologist writing an outpatient clinic visit note after seeing a patient in your subspecialty practice.

**Purpose:** Generate a structured hematology/oncology clinic note from de-identified clinical data. The note must capture disease-specific staging, biomarkers, treatment history, current regimen details, toxicity grading, and surveillance data in a format ready for physician review and attestation.

### Rules

- Write interval history and treatment history in past tense. Write current clinical status, assessment, and plan in present tense.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Expand only on first use within the note.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY (e.g., 3/15/26).
- Grade toxicities by CTCAE v5.0 criteria when severity is described.
- Document ECOG performance status as a number (0-4) with brief functional description.
- Assessment by problem list. Each problem gets a brief rationale followed by specific, actionable plan items.
- Include medication reconciliation with dose, route, frequency, and cycle timing for chemotherapy agents.
- Do not infer or fabricate clinical information not provided. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "c/w" = consistent with
- "ECOG PS" = Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group Performance Status
- "CTCAE" = Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events
- "RECIST" = Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors
- "CR/PR/SD/PD" = complete response / partial response / stable disease / progressive disease
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| Disease focus | Replace biomarker and staging sections with disease-specific fields (e.g., Rai staging for CLL, Ann Arbor for lymphoma, IPSS for MDS) |
| Benign hematology only | Remove the cancer diagnosis, treatment history, and tumor marker sections. Expand the iron studies and coagulation sections |
| Clinical trial patients | Expand the clinical trial section with protocol-specific response criteria and adverse event reporting requirements |
| Palliative focus | Add: "Emphasize symptom burden assessment (ESAS), functional trajectory, and goals of care documentation" |
| Multidisciplinary tumor board | Add: "Include a tumor board discussion summary section with participants, recommendations, and patient's decision" |

### Active chemotherapy follow-up

Use this for patients on active cytotoxic chemotherapy who are returning for a pre-cycle assessment or mid-cycle toxicity check. Focuses on treatment tolerance, dose modification decisions, and lab clearance for the next cycle.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Active Chemotherapy Follow-Up">
You are an attending oncologist writing a pre-cycle or mid-cycle follow-up note for a patient on active chemotherapy.

### Rules

- Write interval history in past tense. Write current status and plan in present tense.
- Grade all toxicities by CTCAE v5.0.
- Document ANC, platelets, and hepatic/renal function as treatment-clearance parameters.
- State explicitly whether the patient clears for next cycle: dose, schedule, and any modifications.
- Use medical abbreviations without expansion.
- Reference dates in M/D/YY format.
- Do not fabricate data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing information.

### Cancer survivorship visit

Use this for patients who have completed active treatment and are in surveillance. Focuses on late effects screening, secondary malignancy risk, psychosocial health, and surveillance imaging schedules.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Cancer Survivorship Visit">
You are an oncologist writing a survivorship follow-up note for a patient who has completed active cancer treatment.

### Rules

- Write treatment history in past tense. Write current status and plan in present tense.
- Document time since treatment completion and current surveillance schedule.
- Screen for late effects specific to the treatment received (cardiotoxicity for anthracyclines, neuropathy for taxanes/platinum, secondary malignancies for alkylators, endocrine effects for hormonal therapy).
- Include psychosocial screening: fear of recurrence, return to work, mood, and functional status.
- Use medical abbreviations without expansion.
- Reference dates in M/D/YY format.
- Do not fabricate data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing information.

### Vocabulary

- "NED" = no evidence of disease
- "DFS" = disease-free survival

### Anemia and benign hematology workup

Use this for patients referred for anemia evaluation, thrombocytopenia, thrombophilia, or other non-malignant hematologic conditions. Structures the workup systematically and documents the diagnostic reasoning.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Benign Hematology Clinic Visit">
You are an attending hematologist writing a clinic note for a patient with a benign hematologic condition (anemia, cytopenias, thrombophilia, VTE, coagulopathy, or hemoglobinopathy).

### Rules

- Write history in past tense. Write current status and plan in present tense.
- Organize lab data by diagnostic category (iron studies, hemolysis labs, coagulation, etc.).
- For VTE: document provoked vs. unprovoked, anticoagulation agent, duration plan, and bleeding risk assessment.
- For anemia: include reticulocyte count interpretation and peripheral smear findings.
- Use medical abbreviations without expansion.
- Reference dates in M/D/YY format.
- Do not fabricate data. Write "[Verify before signing]" for missing information.

### Vocabulary

- "IDA" = iron deficiency anemia
- "TIBC" = total iron-binding capacity
- "Tsat" = transferrin saturation
- "VTE" = venous thromboembolism

## Tips & pitfalls

- **Staging precision matters.** The AI may default to clinical staging when pathologic staging is available, or vice versa. Verify which staging system applies and that the TNM descriptors match your patient's actual workup.
- **Biomarker completeness.** If you provide partial biomarker data ("PD-L1 positive"), the model may not flag the missing quantitative result. Always specify TPS percentage for PD-L1 and the specific mutation for molecular markers.
- **CTCAE grading.** The model grades toxicities based on the symptom descriptions you provide, but it cannot examine the patient. A "mild rash" could be grade 1 or grade 2 depending on body surface area involved. Verify all grades against the actual CTCAE criteria.
- **Treatment line counting.** For metastatic patients on later lines of therapy, the model may miscategorize which line a regimen represents if the treatment history is complex. Double-check line designations.
- **RECIST requires imaging data.** The model may assign a RECIST category based on your narrative description, but formal RECIST assessment requires comparison to a defined baseline. Confirm the response category matches the radiologist's or your own measurements.
- **Anticoagulation duration decisions.** For VTE patients, the model may suggest standard durations without accounting for individual bleeding risk or thrombophilia results. Apply your own risk-benefit analysis.
- **Survivorship screening gaps.** The generic template may not include all recommended late-effects screening for a specific regimen. Cross-reference with NCCN survivorship guidelines for the specific agents your patient received.

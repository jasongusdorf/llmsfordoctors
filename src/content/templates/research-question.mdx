## When to use this

Use this template when you have a clinical question and need to formulate it into a structured, searchable format. It converts a plain-language question into PICO format, generates MeSH terms, produces a ready-to-paste PubMed search string, and recommends study designs. Ideal for evidence-based medicine assignments, starting a literature review, or systematically answering a clinical question from rounds.

<Callout type="tip">
  PICO stands for Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome. It is the standard framework for formulating searchable clinical questions. This template handles therapy, diagnosis, prognosis, and etiology question types.
</Callout>

---

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="PICO Research Question Builder">
You are a medical librarian and clinical epidemiologist helping a clinician formulate a research question and search strategy.

**Purpose:** Convert a plain-language clinical question into a structured PICO framework, generate a search strategy, and recommend appropriate study designs with sample size considerations.

### Rules
- Classify the question type (Therapy / Diagnosis / Prognosis / Etiology / Prevention) and match the recommended study design to that type.
- For each PICO element, provide both MeSH terms and free-text synonyms.
- Generate a ready-to-paste PubMed search string using Boolean operators.
- Identify potential confounders relevant to the question.
- Recommend primary and secondary endpoints that are clinically meaningful (not surrogate endpoints, unless no patient-centered endpoint exists).
- Suggest sample size considerations based on published trials with similar designs.
- Do not fabricate MeSH terms. Flag any terms you are uncertain about and recommend verification in the NLM MeSH Browser.

### Vocabulary
- "PICO" = Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome.
- "MeSH" = Medical Subject Headings (NLM controlled vocabulary).
- "NNT" = Number needed to treat. "MCID" = Minimal clinically important difference.
- Study design hierarchy: Systematic review > RCT > Cohort > Case-control > Cross-sectional > Case series > Expert opinion.

### Output format

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  PICO FRAMEWORK                      ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **P (Population):** Who are the patients? (age, sex, condition, setting, inclusion/exclusion criteria)
- **I (Intervention/Exposure):** What is being done or studied? (specific intervention, dose, duration, delivery)
- **C (Comparison):** What is the alternative? (placebo, standard care, another intervention)
- **O (Outcome):** What do we want to know? (primary and secondary endpoints, measurement tools, timepoints)

**Question Type:** [Therapy / Diagnosis / Prognosis / Etiology / Prevention]

**Formatted Clinical Question:**
"In [P], does [I] compared to [C] improve [O]?"

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  STUDY DESIGN RECOMMENDATION         ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **Best design for this question:** [RCT / Cohort / Case-control / Cross-sectional / Systematic review]
- **Justification:** Why this design matches the question type.
- **Blinding strategy:** [Open-label / Single-blind / Double-blind] with rationale.
- **Randomization strategy:** [Simple / Stratified / Block / Cluster] with stratification variables.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  SAMPLE SIZE CONSIDERATIONS          ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- What is the clinically meaningful difference (MCID)?
- Assumptions for power calculation: effect size, alpha, power, expected dropout rate.
- Rough sample size range based on similar published trials.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  PRIMARY AND SECONDARY ENDPOINTS     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **Primary endpoint:** [Clinically meaningful outcome with measurement method and timepoint]
- **Secondary endpoints:** [2-3 additional outcomes]
- **Safety endpoints:** [Key adverse outcomes to monitor]

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  POTENTIAL CONFOUNDERS               ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

List the most important potential confounders and explain why each could bias the results.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  SEARCH STRATEGY                     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **MeSH Terms:** Relevant MeSH headings for each PICO element
- **Keywords:** Alternative text words and synonyms for each element
- **PubMed Search String:** A ready-to-paste Boolean search query combining MeSH and keywords with AND/OR operators
- **Filters to Consider:** Study type, date range, language, human subjects
- **Databases:** PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL (with any syntax differences noted)

**Alternative PICO Formulations:**
1-2 alternative framings that might yield additional relevant results.

---

[Describe your clinical question in plain language, e.g., "Is apixaban better than warfarin for preventing stroke in elderly patients with atrial fibrillation?"]

## Variations

### RCT design

Use this when you are designing a randomized controlled trial and need to formalize your research question, define endpoints, plan methodology, and estimate sample size.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="PICO for RCT Design">
You are a clinical trialist helping a physician-researcher design a randomized controlled trial.

**Purpose:** Produce a structured RCT planning document with PICO framework, study design recommendations, sample size considerations, and a search strategy for existing evidence.

### Rules
- Specify inclusion/exclusion criteria precisely enough to guide enrollment.
- Primary outcome must be clinically meaningful (not a surrogate endpoint unless justified).
- Recommend a sample size range based on similar published trials.
- Identify key threats to internal validity.
- Do not fabricate data or statistical estimates. Flag assumptions clearly.

### Output format

**PICO Breakdown**
- **P:** Target population with specific inclusion/exclusion criteria
- **I:** Specific intervention with dose, duration, and delivery method
- **C:** Control arm (placebo, active comparator, usual care) with justification
- **O:** Primary and secondary outcomes with measurement tools and timepoints

**Study Design Recommendations**
- Design type: [Parallel / Crossover / Cluster / Factorial / Non-inferiority / Superiority]
- Blinding strategy with rationale
- Randomization strategy with stratification variables

**Sample Size Considerations**
- MCID for the primary outcome
- Power calculation assumptions (effect size, alpha, power, expected dropout)
- Rough sample size range from similar published trials

**Potential Pitfalls**
- Key threats to internal validity
- Anticipated enrollment challenges
- Ethical considerations

**Search for Existing Evidence**
- PubMed search string for existing trials on this topic
- ClinicalTrials.gov search strategy for ongoing studies

---
**RESEARCH QUESTION:**
[Describe your research question in plain language]
</PromptPlayground>

### Observational study design

Use this when your clinical question involves an exposure rather than an intervention (e.g., risk factors, prognosis, harm), requiring an observational study design.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="PECO for Observational Study Design">
You are a clinical epidemiologist helping a researcher formulate a question for an observational study.

**Purpose:** Convert a clinical question into PECO format with appropriate observational study design, confounder identification, and search strategy.

### Rules
- Use PECO (Population, Exposure, Comparison, Outcome) instead of PICO.
- Recommend the best observational design for the question type.
- Identify the most important confounders and explain why each could bias results.
- Recommend STROBE-compliant reporting standards.
- Do not fabricate data or references.

### Output format

**PECO Breakdown**
- **P:** Target population with eligibility criteria
- **E:** Exposure, risk factor, or prognostic factor
- **C:** Unexposed or reference group
- **O:** Outcome of interest with measurement method

**Question Type:** [Etiology / Prognosis / Harm / Risk Factor / Natural History]

**Formatted Question:**
"In [P], is [E] compared to [C] associated with [O]?"

**Recommended Study Design**
- Best design: [Cohort / Case-control / Cross-sectional / Nested case-control]
- Justification and key methodological considerations (temporality, confounding, selection bias)

**Confounding Variables**
List the most important confounders with explanation of why each is a confounder (associated with both exposure and outcome).

**Search Strategy**
- MeSH Terms for each PECO element
- PubMed search string with observational study filters

**Alternative PECO Formulations**
1-2 alternative framings that might capture additional literature.

---
**CLINICAL QUESTION:**
[Describe your question, e.g., "Does long-term shift work increase the risk of cardiovascular disease in nurses?"]
</PromptPlayground>

### Systematic review / meta-analysis protocol

Use this when you are planning a systematic review and need a tightly defined, protocol-ready research question with comprehensive search strategy and PROSPERO registration components.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Systematic Review Protocol Development">
You are a systematic review methodologist helping a researcher develop a protocol-ready review question.

**Purpose:** Produce a PROSPERO-ready systematic review protocol with precisely defined PICO/PECO, comprehensive multi-database search strategy, and data synthesis plan.

### Rules
- Specify eligibility criteria precisely (study designs, publication types, language, dates).
- Generate search strings for multiple databases (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL).
- Recommend appropriate risk of bias assessment tools based on expected study designs.
- Outline a data synthesis plan including conditions for meta-analysis.
- Do not fabricate references. Flag assumptions about the evidence base.

### Output format

**Review Question**
A precisely worded question suitable for PROSPERO registration.

**PICO/PECO Breakdown**
With explicit inclusion and exclusion criteria for each element.

**Eligibility Criteria**
- Study designs to include
- Publication types (full publications, conference abstracts, preprints)
- Language and date restrictions (with justification)

**Comprehensive Search Strategy**
For each database:
- PubMed/MEDLINE: Full search string with MeSH and text words
- Embase: Adapted with Emtree terms
- Cochrane CENTRAL: Adapted search string
- Additional: grey literature, ClinicalTrials.gov, reference list searching, citation tracking

**Risk of Bias Assessment**
Recommended tool (Cochrane RoB 2, ROBINS-I, Newcastle-Ottawa, etc.) based on expected study designs.

**Data Synthesis Plan**
- Conditions for meta-analysis
- Anticipated sources of heterogeneity
- Planned subgroup analyses

</PromptPlayground>

## Example output

Here is what the main prompt produces when given a realistic clinical question:

**Input provided:**
"Does early physical therapy after hip replacement reduce 30-day readmission rates compared to standard post-operative recovery protocols?"

**LLM output:**

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  PICO FRAMEWORK                      ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **P (Population):** Adults (>=18 years) undergoing primary total hip arthroplasty for osteoarthritis. Any surgical approach. Excludes revision arthroplasty, fracture-related arthroplasty, and patients with neurological comorbidity limiting mobility.
- **I (Intervention):** Early physical therapy, defined as PT initiated within 24 hours of surgery (day of surgery or POD0), with structured mobilization protocol.
- **C (Comparison):** Standard post-operative recovery protocol, typically PT initiated on POD1 or POD2, at the discretion of the surgical team.
- **O (Outcome):** Primary: 30-day all-cause hospital readmission rate. Secondary: length of stay, 30-day complications (DVT, PE, wound infection, dislocation), patient-reported functional outcomes (HOOS-JR, TUG test), time to independent ambulation.

**Question Type:** Therapy

**Formatted Clinical Question:**
"In adults undergoing primary total hip arthroplasty, does early physical therapy (within 24 hours of surgery) compared to standard post-operative PT timing reduce 30-day hospital readmission rates?"

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  STUDY DESIGN RECOMMENDATION         ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **Best design:** Pragmatic RCT (parallel group). The intervention is a workflow change, making a pragmatic design more relevant than an explanatory trial.
- **Blinding:** Single-blind (outcome assessor blinded). Patients and therapists cannot be blinded to PT timing.
- **Randomization:** Stratified block randomization. Stratify by surgical approach (anterior vs. posterior) and age group (&lt;65 vs. ≥65).

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  SAMPLE SIZE CONSIDERATIONS          ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **MCID:** A 3-4% absolute reduction in 30-day readmission (from ~8% baseline to ~4-5%) would be clinically meaningful.
- **Power calculation assumptions:** Alpha 0.05, power 80%, baseline readmission rate 8%, expected reduction to 5%, 15% dropout rate.
- **Estimated sample size:** Approximately 900-1,200 patients per arm. Large joint registry studies with similar endpoints have enrolled 1,000-5,000 patients.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  PRIMARY AND SECONDARY ENDPOINTS     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **Primary:** 30-day all-cause readmission (binary, ascertained from hospital records).
- **Secondary:** Length of index hospitalization (hours), 30-day complication rate, HOOS-JR score at 6 weeks, TUG test at discharge.
- **Safety:** Fall rate during inpatient PT, wound dehiscence, prosthetic dislocation during early mobilization.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  POTENTIAL CONFOUNDERS               ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **BMI:** Higher BMI associated with both delayed mobilization and higher readmission rates.
- **Age:** Older patients have longer recovery times and higher baseline readmission risk.
- **Comorbidity burden (CCI):** More comorbidities independently predict readmission.
- **Surgical approach:** Anterior approach may enable faster mobilization independent of PT timing.
- **Discharge destination:** Home vs. skilled nursing facility affects readmission risk.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════╗
║  SEARCH STRATEGY                     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════╝
```

- **MeSH Terms:**
  - P: "Arthroplasty, Replacement, Hip"[MeSH]
  - I: "Early Ambulation"[MeSH], "Physical Therapy Modalities"[MeSH]
  - O: "Patient Readmission"[MeSH]
- **Keywords:**
  - P: "total hip arthroplasty" OR "total hip replacement" OR "hip arthroplasty"
  - I: "early mobilization" OR "same-day mobilization" OR "accelerated rehabilitation" OR "early physical therapy" OR "day-of-surgery PT"
  - O: "readmission" OR "rehospitalization" OR "30-day readmission"
- **PubMed Search String:**
  `("Arthroplasty, Replacement, Hip"[MeSH] OR "total hip arthroplasty"[tiab] OR "total hip replacement"[tiab]) AND ("Early Ambulation"[MeSH] OR "early mobilization"[tiab] OR "same-day mobilization"[tiab] OR "early physical therapy"[tiab] OR "accelerated rehabilitation"[tiab]) AND ("Patient Readmission"[MeSH] OR "readmission"[tiab] OR "rehospitalization"[tiab])`
- **Filters:** Humans, English, 2015-present (reflecting current surgical and rehabilitation practices).

**Alternative PICO Formulations:**
1. "In adults undergoing total hip arthroplasty, does same-day mobilization compared to next-day mobilization reduce length of stay?" (narrower intervention, different outcome)
2. "In adults undergoing total hip or total knee arthroplasty, does early PT compared to standard PT improve 90-day functional outcomes?" (broader population, longer-term outcome)

## Tips & pitfalls

- The generated PubMed search string is a starting point. You will likely need to iterate, broadening or narrowing based on the number of results.
- MeSH terms may not be perfectly matched. Always verify against the MeSH Browser at nlm.nih.gov/mesh/ before running your final search.
- PubMed, Embase, and Cochrane use different Boolean syntax. A search string generated for PubMed will not work directly in Embase without adaptation.
- The model may generate MeSH terms that do not exist or have been retired. Verify all MeSH headings before running the search.
- If your initial search returns very few results, the PICO may be too narrow. Try broadening the population or outcome, or remove one PICO element from the search string.
- Question type matters for study design: therapy questions are best answered by RCTs, prognosis questions by cohort studies, diagnosis questions by cross-sectional studies with a reference standard. The model's study design suggestion should match the question type.
- For systematic reviews, the search strategy needs to be more comprehensive (multiple databases, hand-searching references). Use the **Systematic review protocol** variation for protocol-grade search strategies.

## When to use this

Use this template when writing an outpatient vascular neurology or stroke clinic follow-up note. Paste in your clinical data (index event details, etiology workup, imaging, secondary prevention regimen, residual deficits, rehabilitation status, and risk factors) and the LLM produces a structured clinic note with disease-specific documentation for ischemic stroke, TIA, carotid stenosis, intracranial stenosis, cerebral venous thrombosis, and cryptogenic stroke. The output format captures TOAST classification, secondary prevention optimization, and functional recovery trajectory.

<Callout type="hipaa">
  De-identify all clinical information before pasting into any AI tool without a signed BAA. Remove patient name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, provider names, and any other direct identifiers.
</Callout>

## The prompt

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Vascular Neurology / Stroke Clinic Visit Note">
You are an experienced vascular neurologist writing an outpatient stroke clinic visit note. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a complete, structured stroke follow-up note.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for the index event and history; present tense for current status, exam findings, and plan.
- Include specific numbers (NIHSS scores, stenosis percentages, BP readings, LDL, A1c, imaging dates) whenever available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never spell out what an abbreviation stands for.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Document TOAST classification explicitly.
- Document secondary prevention medications with doses.
- Include risk factor modification targets with current values.
- Do not infer or fabricate any clinical information not provided. Write "***" for missing data.
- This is a draft; physician review and attestation required before signing.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "s/p" = status post
- "pmhx" = past medical history
- "tPA" = alteplase
- "NIHSS" = NIH Stroke Scale
- "TOAST" = Trial of Org 10172 in Acute Stroke Treatment (stroke classification)
- "CTA" = CT angiography
- "MRA" = MR angiography
- "CEA" = carotid endarterectomy
- "CAS" = carotid artery stenting
- "PFO" = patent foramen ovale
- "ILR" = implantable loop recorder
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## How to customize

| Element | How to Adjust |
|---------|---------------|
| TIA-focused visit | Add: "This is a TIA follow-up. Calculate ABCD2 score from the data provided. Emphasize rapid etiologic workup completion and early secondary prevention." |
| Young stroke (&lt;50) | Add: "This is a young patient with stroke. Include extended workup: hypercoagulable panel, PFO evaluation, dissection imaging, vasculitis labs, toxicology, and migraine history." |
| ICH follow-up | Replace secondary prevention section with: "BP management (target &lt;130/80), anticoagulation reversal status, repeat imaging schedule, surgical follow-up if applicable, and cerebral amyloid angiopathy evaluation." |
| Carotid stenosis only | Remove stroke event sections. Add: "This is an asymptomatic carotid stenosis surveillance visit. Document stenosis percentage trend, medical optimization, and surgical candidacy." |
| Telemedicine visit | Add: "This is a telemedicine encounter. Document exam limitations. Use patient-reported motor function, speech quality, and gait description." |

### Cryptogenic stroke workup

Use this for patients with cryptogenic (or embolic stroke of undetermined source) where the visit focuses on completing the extended etiologic workup and determining PFO closure candidacy.

<PromptPlayground tool="Claude" title="Cryptogenic Stroke Workup Visit">
You are a vascular neurologist writing a clinic visit note for a patient with cryptogenic stroke undergoing extended etiologic workup. After receiving the clinical information below, produce a structured note focused on workup progress and PFO closure candidacy.

### Rules

- Write in past tense for history; present tense for current status and plan.
- Document each element of the cryptogenic workup (cardiac monitoring, bubble study, hypercoagulable panel, PFO evaluation) with results or pending status.
- Calculate RoPE score if PFO is present and data is available.
- Use medical abbreviations throughout. Never expand abbreviations.
- Reference dates in the format M/D/YY.
- Do not fabricate clinical information. Write "***" for missing data.

### Vocabulary

- Use "patient" instead of "pt"
- "ESUS" = embolic stroke of undetermined source
- "PFO" = patent foramen ovale
- "RoPE" = Risk of Paradoxical Embolism
- "ILR" = implantable loop recorder
- Use all abbreviations provided without expanding them

## Tips & pitfalls

- **TOAST classification requires your judgment.** The AI assigns TOAST based on the workup you describe, but borderline cases (small lacunar infarct in a patient with AFib) require clinical reasoning the AI cannot provide. Verify the classification matches your own assessment.
- **NIHSS is a specific scale.** The AI will populate NIHSS items based on your exam description, but it may mismap exam findings to the NIHSS scoring rubric. A patient with "mild word-finding difficulty" could score 0 or 1 on the language item depending on severity. Score the NIHSS yourself.
- **BP targets after stroke are nuanced.** The AI defaults to a target of less than 130/80 per AHA/ASA guidelines, but the optimal target in the acute post-stroke period (first 2 weeks) differs from the chronic secondary prevention target. If your patient is in the early post-stroke window, adjust accordingly.
- **DAPT duration in non-cardioembolic stroke.** If your patient is on dual antiplatelet therapy (POINT or CHANCE protocol), the AI may not flag the transition from DAPT to monotherapy at 21 to 90 days. Document the planned transition date.
- **Cryptogenic stroke workup is often incomplete at 3 months.** If the ILR has not yet been implanted or the monitoring period is ongoing, the TOAST classification should remain "cryptogenic" or "under investigation." The AI may prematurely assign a final classification.
- **Post-stroke depression is underdiagnosed and undertreated.** The AI will document the PHQ-9 you provide, but if you do not screen, the note will have no mood section. Screen every stroke patient at every follow-up visit.
- **Driving is a clinical and legal question.** The AI cannot determine when your patient is safe to drive. The plan should reflect your clinical judgment and, when available, the results of a formal OT driving evaluation.

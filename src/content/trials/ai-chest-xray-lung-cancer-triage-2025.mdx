## Study Design

Storey et al. conducted a 3-year pre-post evaluation (1 year pre, 2 years post) across five hospitals in South West London's NHS trust. A commercial AI tool (Annalise Enterprise CXR, trained on >800,000 CXRs) triaged chest x-rays for 14 predetermined cancer-suspicious findings. When flagged, patients were kept in the department and sent for same-day CT via a radiographer-led pathway, bypassing the standard workflow of reporting, recalling the patient from home, and scheduling CT later. The study included 56,257 chest x-rays from GP-referred outpatients.

---

## Key Findings

- Time from chest x-ray to CT report for suspected lung cancer **reduced from 6.0 to 3.6 days**
- **No significant increase** in the proportion of CTs performed relative to chest x-rays; the tool didn't generate excess imaging
- AI sensitivity for detecting suspected cancer: **82.5%**; specificity: **90.5%**
- Negative predictive value was **99.6%**; when the AI said "no cancer," it was almost always right
- Positive predictive value was **14.6%**; most flagged cases were false positives, but this is expected for a screening-level tool
- **Focal airspace opacity and single pulmonary nodule** had the highest false-positive rates; pulmonary and mediastinal masses had the highest diagnostic yield

<Callout type="evidence">
  This is one of the most practical medical AI implementation studies published: real patients, real workflow change, real time savings. Reducing time-to-diagnosis by 2.4 days without increasing unnecessary CT scans is a clean win.
</Callout>

---

## Clinical Implications

1. **Same-day CT pathways are the key innovation.** The AI tool's real value isn't just flagging abnormalities; it's triggering an immediate workflow change (keeping the patient in department for same-day CT) that eliminates the delay of patient recall.

2. **14.6% PPV is acceptable for cancer screening.** This means roughly 1 in 7 flagged patients has cancer. For a screening pathway, this is reasonable, similar to mammography callback rates.

3. **Results varied by hospital.** The most significant time reductions occurred at centers with colocated CT and x-ray facilities. Hospitals without colocated scanners saw smaller gains, highlighting that AI tools need supporting infrastructure to deliver full benefit.

<Callout type="pitfall">
  The 82.5% sensitivity means the AI missed roughly 1 in 6 cancers. The standard radiographer/radiologist reporting pathway must remain as the safety net; the AI triage pathway accelerates diagnosis for most but cannot replace comprehensive human review.
</Callout>

4. **This is the UK's NOLCP target in action.** The National Optimal Lung Cancer Pathway recommends CXR-to-CT within 72 hours. The post-intervention median of 3.6 days is close to this target, compared with 6.0 days before.

---

## My Takeaway

This is how medical AI should work: a focused tool solving a specific bottleneck in a well-defined clinical pathway. The AI doesn't make the diagnosis; it triages urgency and triggers a faster workflow. The fact that CT volume didn't increase proves the AI is genuinely identifying the right patients to fast-track rather than just adding imaging volume. For health systems struggling with cancer diagnosis timelines, this is a replicable model, provided they have the same-day CT infrastructure to support it.

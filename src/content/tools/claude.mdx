## Overview

Claude is Anthropic's flagship large language model, and as of mid-2026 it remains the strongest choice for most clinical use cases. It excels at long-form text generation, following complex multi-part instructions, and producing nuanced, hedged clinical language, all of which matter at the bedside. The current frontier model is Claude Opus 4.8, released in May 2026.

In January 2026 Anthropic launched Claude for Healthcare, a set of HIPAA-ready products for providers, payers, and health tech companies. It includes connectors to the CMS Coverage Database, ICD-10 codes, the NPI Registry, and PubMed, plus agent skills for FHIR development and prior authorization review. Combined with the BAA Anthropic offers on its HIPAA-ready services, Claude is one of the few major commercial LLMs that can be used with real patient data in a HIPAA-compliant setting.

## Clinical Strengths

Claude consistently outperforms competing models in these clinical tasks:

- **Discharge summaries and clinical notes**: handles long context and produces well-organized prose
- **Patient communication**: adapts reading level accurately; produces empathetic, clear language
- **Clinical reasoning and differential generation**: structured, thoughtful, appropriately hedged
- **Literature summarization**: handles complex abstracts and synthesizes findings well
- **Instruction following**: reliably produces output in specified formats (tables, SOAP, bullet points)

The model's large context window (1 million tokens in Claude Opus 4.8 on the API) makes it particularly useful for summarizing lengthy records, operative reports, or multi-page specialist notes.

---

## Weaknesses

No model is perfect. Be aware of these limitations:

- **Hallucination risk**: while lower than some competitors, Claude will occasionally fabricate drug dosages, references, or clinical details. Always verify.
- **Live data requires setup**: web search and the PubMed and CMS connectors cover many lookups, but Claude still cannot access UpToDate or institution-specific formularies, and the healthcare connectors require enterprise configuration.
- **Memory cuts both ways**: paid plans now retain memory across conversations. Convenient for projects, but review what the model remembers if you have discussed clinical scenarios, and remember that memory on a consumer account is not BAA-covered.
- **Consumer tier not HIPAA-compliant**: the free claude.ai, Pro, and Max tiers do not include a BAA. You must use a HIPAA-ready tier (enterprise or API with zero data retention) under a signed BAA for PHI.

## Clinical Reasoning

Claude is our top recommendation for two specific clinical reasoning tasks:

- **Case synthesis**: when you have a complex patient with multiple problems, extensive workup results, and diagnostic uncertainty, Claude produces the most coherent, well-organized assessments. Its large context window means you can paste the full picture without summarizing away details.
- **Cognitive debiasing**: when asked to challenge your working diagnosis, Claude pushes back more substantively than competing models. Its natural hedging tendency (sometimes a weakness) becomes a genuine strength when the task is finding holes in reasoning.

For **differential diagnosis generation**, we recommend ChatGPT's reasoning mode (GPT-5.5 Thinking) instead; its extended reasoning produces more structured, auditable differentials. ## HIPAA & Privacy

<Callout type="hipaa">
  The standard Claude.ai consumer product (free, Pro, and Max tiers at claude.ai) does NOT include a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). Do not enter real patient information on claude.ai unless your organization has a signed BAA with Anthropic. For HIPAA-compliant use, contact Anthropic about Claude for Healthcare and enterprise access, or use the API under a signed BAA.
</Callout>

Anthropic's HIPAA-ready offering includes:
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) covering the first-party API and enterprise products
- Zero data retention required for BAA coverage (prompts not used for training)
- SOC 2 Type II compliance
- Audit logging capabilities
- Claude for Healthcare connectors (CMS coverage data, ICD-10, NPI Registry, PubMed)

For most clinical organizations, an enterprise agreement or the API with zero data retention is the correct tier. Individual physicians should use only de-identified data on the consumer tiers.

## Overview

Nabla is an ambient clinical documentation tool built with a privacy-first design philosophy. Its most distinguishing feature: audio is not stored by default. The encounter is processed in real time, a note is generated, and the audio is discarded. Medical data is retained for 14 days by default, though this window is configurable per organization.

The platform integrates with EHR systems via SMART on FHIR and supports enterprise identity management through SSO, SAML, and SCIM. Nabla claims compliance with both HIPAA and ISO 27001, with SOC 2 certification also referenced in their security documentation.

For organizations that prioritize data minimization — storing less data for less time — Nabla's default configuration is among the most conservative in the ambient documentation space.

Nabla's approach reflects a European privacy-by-design sensibility applied to the U.S. healthcare market. The defaults are conservative, the retention windows are short, and the platform avoids storing data types (like audio) that many competitors retain as a matter of course.

## Clinical Strengths

Nabla's privacy-first approach comes with practical clinical benefits:

- **No audio storage by default** — encounter audio is processed and discarded, significantly reducing the data footprint and associated risk
- **Explicit retention defaults** — medical data retained for 14 days by default, with organizational configuration available for shorter or longer windows
- **Published security documentation** — dedicated security pages describe encryption, access controls, and compliance certifications
- **Enterprise IAM options** — SSO, SAML, and SCIM support enables integration with existing identity management infrastructure
- **Epic integration via SMART on FHIR** — standards-based EHR integration rather than proprietary connectors
- **Claims no model training on customer data** — Nabla states it does not use customer data to train its models
- **Multi-language support** — Nabla supports encounters in multiple languages, which can be valuable for clinicians serving diverse patient populations

<Callout type="tip">
  If data minimization is a priority for your organization, Nabla's default settings — no audio storage, 14-day medical data retention — provide a strong starting position. During procurement, confirm these defaults are reflected in your specific contract and cannot be silently changed.
</Callout>

## Weaknesses

Evaluate these factors carefully:

- **Retention is configurable, not fixed** — the 14-day default is a starting point. Verify your organization's actual configured retention window and whether it can be modified without your knowledge.
- **"No model training" claim needs contract verification** — while Nabla states it does not train on customer data, confirm this is reflected in your BAA or data processing agreement, not just marketing materials
- **Optional feedback sharing** — Nabla may offer opt-in feedback mechanisms. Verify whether shared feedback data is truly de-identified and what "de-identified" means in their methodology.
- **Enterprise procurement required** — no individual clinician tier. Organizational deployment and contracting are necessary.
- **Relatively newer entrant** — compared to established players like Nuance/Microsoft, Nabla has a shorter track record in large-scale enterprise deployments
- **Specialty depth unknown** — documentation on specialty-specific note customization is limited. Confirm that note templates meet the requirements of your specific clinical specialties before committing.

<Callout type="pitfall">
  "No audio storage" is a strong default, but verify it applies to your deployment. Some configurations or features (such as quality review or dispute resolution) may temporarily retain audio. Confirm the complete data lifecycle for your specific contract.
</Callout>

## Overview

DeepScribe is an ambient AI medical scribe focused on specialty-specific documentation. Rather than offering a one-size-fits-all ambient note, DeepScribe tailors its output to the documentation patterns of specific clinical specialties, a meaningful differentiator for specialists whose notes have unique structural requirements.

The platform operates as a Business Associate under executed agreements with its customers. DeepScribe maintains a security program narrative that describes encryption, role-based access controls, audit capabilities, and incident response procedures. The privacy policy explicitly describes the company's role as a Business Associate.

DeepScribe is primarily an enterprise product, requiring organizational procurement and deployment.

The specialty focus matters in practice. A dermatologist's note looks nothing like a cardiologist's note, and a tool that understands those structural differences out of the box saves significant editing time compared to a generic ambient scribe that produces one-size-fits-all output.

## Clinical Strengths

DeepScribe's specialty focus is its primary advantage:

- **Specialty alignment**: documentation templates and AI behavior are tuned for specific specialties rather than using a generic approach. This matters for fields like orthopedics, cardiology, or dermatology where note structure varies significantly.
- **Explicit security program**: DeepScribe publishes a security narrative covering their organizational controls, not just a brief compliance checkbox
- **RBAC and audit capabilities**: role-based access controls and audit logging are described, providing the access governance that compliance teams expect
- **Incident response described**: a defined incident response process is part of the published security posture
- **Business Associate status**: operates explicitly as a BA under customer agreements, providing a clear HIPAA framework
- **Coding support**: beyond documentation, DeepScribe offers coding assistance that can help ensure notes support appropriate billing codes for specialty-specific procedures

<Callout type="tip">
  If you are in a specialty with unique documentation requirements (detailed procedure notes, specific exam templates, or specialty-specific coding patterns) evaluate DeepScribe's specialty-specific capabilities against general-purpose ambient tools. A specialty-tuned scribe can save significant editing time compared to a generic note that needs heavy revision.
</Callout>

## Weaknesses

Several areas warrant careful review:

- **Encryption specifics are vague**: the security page states that encryption is used in transit and at rest, but specific algorithms (e.g., TLS version, AES key length) are not detailed in public documentation
- **Data aggregation and de-identification clauses**: like several competitors, DeepScribe's contractual terms may include provisions for aggregating and de-identifying data. Validate these clauses align with your organization's data governance policies.
- **Retention terms need verification**: public documentation does not specify exact retention timelines for audio, transcripts, or notes. These details are likely contract-specific.
- **Audit and logging expectations**: while RBAC and audits are described, the specific granularity of audit logs (what is logged, how long logs are retained, how they can be accessed) should be confirmed during procurement
- **Limited public technical detail**: compared to vendors like Microsoft/Nuance or Abridge, DeepScribe's public-facing security documentation is less granular
- **Specialty coverage scope**: while specialty alignment is a strength, not all specialties may be equally well-supported. Confirm that your specific specialty has mature templates before committing to procurement.

<Callout type="pitfall">
  When a vendor describes "encryption in transit and at rest" without specifying algorithms or standards, this is a yellow flag, not a red flag. It means you need to request technical security documentation directly during procurement. Do not skip this step.
</Callout>

## Overview

Perplexity occupies a unique niche among clinical AI tools: it is the only major AI product that combines real-time internet search with cited, attributed responses. When you ask "Is there evidence for X?", Perplexity pulls current sources, synthesizes them, and shows you exactly where each claim came from.

This makes it genuinely valuable for a specific category of clinical question: evidence lookups, quick literature checks, and "what does the current evidence say about this drug/intervention/guideline?" queries. For these use cases — and these use cases specifically — Perplexity has no close competitor.

However, Perplexity is not designed as a structured clinical reasoning tool. It is not suitable for differential generation, note writing, or complex diagnostic workups where you need careful, organized output. And critically: **Perplexity does not offer a BAA**, which means it cannot be used with real patient data under any circumstances in a HIPAA-regulated context.

---

## Clinical Strengths

- **Real-time citations** — every claim is attributed to a source; you can verify the evidence behind any answer immediately
- **Current evidence** — unlike models with training cutoffs, Perplexity accesses live web content including recent publications, guidelines, and drug information
- **Evidence grounding for "Is there a trial for X?"** — finds recent RCTs, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses quickly
- **Guideline lookups** — reliably surfaces current ACC/AHA, IDSA, USPSTF, and other society guidelines
- **Drug information** — pulls current prescribing information, recent label updates, and formulary data
- **Fast for narrow questions** — purpose-built for question-answering with source attribution; faster than browsing PubMed manually

## When Perplexity Makes Sense

Perplexity's 3/5 overall rating reflects its limited scope for general clinical tasks. For its specific niche, it is a 5/5 tool.

| Use Case | Perplexity? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| "Is there evidence for X drug in Y condition?" | Yes — ideal | Real-time citations, current literature |
| "What is the current [society] guideline for X?" | Yes — ideal | Surfaces current versions with attribution |
| "What recent trials exist for X intervention?" | Yes — ideal | Finds recent publications with links |
| Quick drug reference / label lookup | Yes — good | Current prescribing information |
| Differential diagnosis generation | No | Use Claude or o3 instead |
| SOAP note / clinical documentation | No | Not designed for structured output |
| Complex multi-step reasoning | No | Lacks deliberate chain-of-thought |
| Anything involving patient data | **Never** | No BAA — hard stop |

The pattern is consistent: Perplexity excels when your question is "what does the evidence say?" and the answer requires current, citable sources. It underperforms when your question is "help me think through this clinical problem" and the answer requires structured reasoning.

## Weaknesses

- **No BAA — hard constraint** — Perplexity AI does not offer a Business Associate Agreement. This is a hard stop: it cannot be used with any patient data, in any context, in a HIPAA-regulated setting. There is no enterprise pathway to unlock compliance.
- **Not designed for structured prompts** — clinical prompt templates (SOAP format, DDx lists, step-by-step reasoning) produce inconsistent results; the tool is optimized for question-answer, not structured generation
- **Source quality varies** — Perplexity cites sources, but source quality ranges from PubMed abstracts to low-quality health websites; citations require evaluation, not just acknowledgment
- **Shallow synthesis** — responses tend to aggregate sources rather than reason deeply across them; complex clinical questions get surface-level answers with references rather than careful analysis
- **No memory or context** — sessions start fresh; cannot build on prior context the way a clinical workflow requires

## HIPAA & Privacy

<Callout type="hipaa">
  Perplexity AI does NOT offer a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and has no enterprise HIPAA-compliant tier. This is a hard constraint with no current workaround. Do not enter any patient information — not names, not dates of birth, not diagnoses, not any of the 18 HIPAA identifiers — into Perplexity. Use it only for de-identified evidence questions: "what is the evidence for drug X in condition Y?" not "my patient John Smith with MRN 12345 has condition Y."
</Callout>

There is no BAA pathway for Perplexity as of early 2026. Until one is available, Perplexity should be treated as a general reference tool — similar to searching PubMed or UpToDate — and used only for de-identified queries.

**Safe use pattern:** Ask about drugs, conditions, guidelines, and interventions in the abstract. Never paste clinical notes, patient identifiers, or case details.

## Bottom Line

Perplexity is the best available tool for one specific clinical workflow: evidence-grounded lookup with real-time citations. "Is there an RCT for this?" "What does the current ADA guideline say?" "What was that NEJM trial on X?" — these are Perplexity's native strengths, and no competing AI product does them as efficiently.

The 3/5 overall rating reflects the hard limits: no BAA makes it unsuitable for any patient-data workflow, and its structured reasoning output is weak. Within its lane, it is an excellent tool. Outside that lane, use Claude or OpenAI's o-series instead.

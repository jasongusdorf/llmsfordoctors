## Overview

OpenAI has consolidated its once-confusing model lineup. ChatGPT now runs on the GPT-5.5 family: GPT-5.5 Instant for fast, general-purpose work and GPT-5.5 Thinking for deliberate, extended reasoning, with GPT-5.5 Pro available on the Pro plan for the hardest problems. For clinical users, the distinction matters enormously: Instant is a capable workhorse for routine tasks, while Thinking is a genuine leap forward for structured diagnostic reasoning. (The GPT-4o and o-series models reviewed here previously have been retired.)

The Thinking mode applies extended chain-of-thought computation before producing an answer. In practice, this means it will walk through a differential systematically, weigh competing diagnoses against each other, and surface considerations that faster models skip, behavior that closely mirrors how a careful clinician thinks.

OpenAI makes a Business Associate Agreement available at the enterprise tier, enabling HIPAA-compliant use of patient data for healthcare organizations.

---

## Clinical Strengths

- **Differential diagnosis generation**: GPT-5.5 Thinking's chain-of-thought reasoning produces structured, ordered differentials with explicit reasoning steps
- **Complex clinical reasoning**: excels at multi-step problems: drug-drug interactions, atypical presentations, rare disease workups
- **Note writing with GPT-5.5 Instant**: fast enough for real-time documentation assistance; produces well-formatted SOAP notes and discharge summaries
- **Instruction following**: reliably produces output in specified formats (tables, structured templates, numbered lists)
- **Broad general knowledge**: strong performance across specialties, including areas with sparse training data

## Which Model Should I Use?

| Clinical Task | Best Model | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Differential diagnosis (complex case) | GPT-5.5 Thinking | Deep chain-of-thought reasoning; systematic |
| Differential diagnosis (routine) | GPT-5.5 Instant | Faster; sufficient for most presentations |
| SOAP note / discharge summary | GPT-5.5 Instant | Speed matters; output quality is excellent |
| Drug interaction check | GPT-5.5 Thinking | Reasoning depth catches subtle interactions |
| Patient education letter | GPT-5.5 Instant | Fast; excellent at tone and reading level |
| Literature question ("Is there evidence for X?") | GPT-5.5 Instant + web search | Built-in search in ChatGPT |
| Rare disease workup | GPT-5.5 Thinking or Pro | Extended reasoning handles sparse evidence well |
| Board-style question analysis | GPT-5.5 Thinking | Step-by-step reasoning matches vignette format |

## Weaknesses

- **Thinking mode is slow**: GPT-5.5 Thinking and Pro take significantly longer to respond than Instant; not suitable for real-time note dictation
- **Consumer tiers are not HIPAA-compliant**: the most accessible tiers (Free, Go, Plus, Pro) cannot be used with patient data; enterprise setup adds friction
- **Ads on the Go tier**: the $8 Go plan may include advertising, an uncomfortable fit for anything adjacent to clinical work
- **Hallucination risk**: like all LLMs, GPT-5.5 can produce confident but incorrect clinical details; extended reasoning reduces but does not eliminate errors
- **Memory cuts both ways**: ChatGPT retains memory across sessions by default on paid plans; convenient, but review what it remembers if you have discussed clinical scenarios on a consumer account

OpenAI's Enterprise tier includes:
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA)
- Data not used for model training
- SOC 2 Type II compliance
- Admin controls and audit logging
- SSO / SCIM provisioning

Individual physicians without enterprise access should use only de-identified data. When in doubt, remove all 18 HIPAA identifiers before pasting into any consumer AI product.

## Bottom Line

If structured diagnostic reasoning is your primary use case, GPT-5.5 Thinking is the best available tool as of mid-2026. The chain-of-thought approach genuinely mirrors careful clinical thinking in a way that faster models do not. For routine note-writing and documentation tasks, GPT-5.5 Instant is fast and reliable.

The practical barrier is HIPAA compliance: the consumer tiers that most physicians use daily do not include a BAA. Organizations willing to set up enterprise agreements gain access to a genuinely powerful clinical reasoning tool.
